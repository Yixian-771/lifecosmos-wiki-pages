    I. Overview and Definition¶

The subconscious is the Dao, and it is the Dao that operates all happenings and changes. The subconscious not only possesses ancient memory, but is also in communication with the thinking of the Greatest Creator. It is the source of inspiration, the bond and bridge of telepathy, the channel of thought transmission, a microcosm of the universe, and the fountainhead of imagination. The subconscious is more sensitive, more truthful, and closer to the reality of the universe than conscious awareness. What spiritual schools call the "Greater Self" is the subconscious, and the "smaller self" is conscious awareness; the Greater Self determines everything about the smaller self. In the Lifechanyuan system, the subconscious goes beyond the interpretations of Freud and Jung and is directly positioned as the contact interface between the Dao and the human being, the projection of cosmic consciousness within individual life, and the deep passage by which one moves toward spirituality and toward the celestial realms.

    II. Revealing the Nature of the Subconscious Through Urgently Searching for a Toilet in a Dream: Full Text of Heavenly Enlightenment from Searching for a Toilet in a Dream¶

    (Xuefeng Corpus, X09 Essays, Heavenly Enlightenment from Searching for a Toilet in a Dream, complete original text):

    All the myriad things, matters, and phenomena in the great thousand world form a wordless heavenly scripture. Dreaming of urgently searching for a toilet when one needs to urinate is one such phenomenon, one page of that heavenly scripture. We need to read it and see what the heavenly scripture is saying.

    Once a person falls asleep, one is no longer aware of everything in the external world, yet one runs into the antimatter world.

    When a person is awake and needs to urinate, one will look for a toilet to relieve oneself. The key is that when we are asleep, we are not conscious of the urgency to urinate. Yet now the issue is that the self in the dream is anxiously searching for a toilet. In the dream, we are awake.

    So the questions arise. 1. We are asleep, yet awake. Are we asleep or are we awake? 2. Outside the dream, we are asleep; inside the dream, we are awake. Could there be two selves? 3. Did the urgency to urinate wake the self in the dream, or did the self in the dream become aware of the urgency to urinate? 4. The sleeping self, in the stage of deep sleep, is actually not much different from a vegetative person, and one could even say it is a "dead person" whose heart is still beating. Why then does an awake self appear in the dream? Does this not tell us that after a person dies, another self still exists?

    Let us now unfold the analysis and reasoning.

    There is no doubt that we are asleep. When a person is in deep sleep, one cannot feel anything taking place in the surrounding material world, unless someone shouts loudly, shakes the person, or there is a sudden piercing burst of noise. But once the body experiences pain, extreme hunger or thirst, an urgent need to urinate, and the like, then in lighter cases, activities seeking to resolve the body's discomfort will appear in the dream; in heavier cases, one will naturally wake up. This shows that we have a self that is awake and a self that is asleep. The awake one is dull, while the self during sleep is instead sensitive, and is awake. The enlightenment this gives us is that the intuitive self, the spiritual self, is more sensitive and more correct than the rational self in ordinary waking reality.

    A further enlightenment is that feeling is more reliable than analysis, and spirituality is more correct than rationality.

    The self in the dream and the self outside the dream are two selves that are not entirely the same. The self outside the dream, when needing to urinate, can easily find a toilet and solve the problem, but the self within the dream is always unable to find a place to urinate. In other words, the self in the dream cannot solve physiological problems. For example, a hungry person can easily dream of abundant delicious food, but that does not solve the problem of hunger.

    This shows that to solve problems of the flesh, one must use the resources of the material world; to solve problems of the spirit and soul, one must use the resources of the antimatter world.

    When one urgently needs to urinate, the only solution is to urinate. If sleep is light, the need to urinate will wake the person and send them to the toilet. If sleep is deep, then one will urinate in bed. Once there is no urine left in the bladder, the dream of urgently searching for a toilet will not appear.

    This enlightens us that if our body is healthy, our mind is free of distracting thoughts, there are no inverted dreams or delusions, and the soul is perfect, then we will dream very little. Only when one urgently needs to urinate will one dream of searching for a toilet. If there is no urine in the bladder, there will be no dream of searching for a toilet to urinate. Likewise, if the stomach is not hungry, there will be no dream of eating delicious food. If the heart is free of fear and worry, there will be no dream of being chased by tigers, leopards, or wicked people. Other dreams may be inferred in the same way.

    The psychoanalytic school holds that people have manifest consciousness (preconsciousness), consciousness, and the subconscious. So, according to psychoanalytic theory, the awareness a person has while awake belongs to manifest consciousness; the one who searches for a toilet to urinate after falling asleep belongs to consciousness; and the sudden flash of inspiration while doing something belongs to the subconscious. The reason the Russian chemist Mendeleev was able to produce the periodic table of chemical elements was the result of the action of the subconscious.

    Buddhism speaks of the eight consciousnesses: eye-consciousness, ear-consciousness, nose-consciousness, tongue-consciousness, body-consciousness, consciousness, manas-consciousness, and alaya-consciousness. The first six consciousnesses belong to manifest consciousness, alaya-consciousness belongs to the subconscious, and urgently searching for a toilet belongs to manas-consciousness.

    The question now is: when bees make honey, spiders spin webs, and migratory birds migrate, what consciousness is it?

    My answer is that they do not involve any such consciousness or not-consciousness. It is purely instinct. A deeper question is, what kind of consciousness is instinct?

    The answer is that instinct is the subconscious, which in Buddhism is called alaya-consciousness.

    Spiritual cultivation schools hold that a person is divided into the smaller self and the Greater Self. The smaller self is manifest consciousness, and the Greater Self is the subconscious. The Greater Self determines everything about the smaller self.

    Now an interesting question arises: in the dream of urgently searching for a toilet, who exactly is it that is doing the searching?

    We must first ask a question: who designed for our LIFE the dream of urgently searching for a toilet when the need to urinate arises?

    Without a doubt, it is the subconscious.

    Then, is the self in the subconscious still me?

    My answer is: the subconscious is the Dao, and it is the Dao that operates all happenings and changes.

    Hunger and wanting to eat, sleepiness and wanting to sleep, the bladder being full and wanting to urinate, pain and wanting to moan, and so on, are natural phenomena that result from the operation of the Dao. That is called the natural Dao. If one follows the natural Dao, one is healthy, smooth, and without blame. If one goes against the natural Dao, one becomes ill, things go badly, and disasters arise, and naturally one may wet the bed.

    The final enlightenment that the dream of urgently searching for a toilet gives us is this: the Dao controls the occurrence and transformation of all things, and the best path in life is to follow the natural Dao.

    Thus what becomes clearer and clearer to us is that the higher the level of LIFE, the more it pursues things of spirituality; the lower the level of LIFE, the more it pursues rational and material things. This is why the Guide asks everyone to live a spiritual life rather than a rational life, because rationality is the characteristic of humans, while spirituality is the characteristic of celestials. Rationality belongs to the material level, while spirituality belongs to the antimatter level.

    III. Collective Subconscious and Individual Manifest Consciousness: Cosmic Consciousness Transcends Everything¶

    (Xuefeng Corpus, X09 Essays, The Relationship Between the Collective Subconscious and Individual Manifest Consciousness, complete original text):

    We discover this kind of phenomenon: the younger a person is, the more adept they are at using computers and mobile phones; the older a person is, the less they understand the terminology and programs of computers and mobile phones. So we must ask: when younger people have less knowledge and shallower understanding, how do they come to grasp the operating procedures and techniques of computers and mobile phones?

    The apparent logic should be this: the older one is, the harder it is to understand new knowledge and new programs. What one already knows instead becomes an obstacle to understanding and accepting new knowledge, new technology, and new programs. By contrast, the younger one is, the fewer fixed obstacles there are in consciousness, and the more easily one can become a person who understands and accepts new knowledge, new technology, and new programs.

    From this phenomenon we know that the more knowledge a person has, the more likely that person is to become a force of resistance and opposition to the advance and civilizational leap of society. It is like a people or a country: the longer its history, the weaker its capacity for innovation.

    From the principle of entropy increase, the more closed a system is, the more rigid and stubborn it becomes; the more open a system is, the more flexible it is and the more full of vitality and life-force it becomes.

    Let us look at another phenomenon: 'One dog barks at a shadow, a hundred dogs bark at the sound.' In a village at night, once one dog barks, the dogs in every household will bark. The dog that gives the first bark has probably seen the shadow of a stranger, while the other dogs bark entirely because they heard a dog bark and simply join in.

    On a street with many dogs, once one dog bites a person, the other dogs, without distinguishing right from wrong, will also pounce and bite.

    When mainstream culture occupies the dominant position, once a non-mainstream culture appears, everyone will, without thinking, lash out at and attack the non-mainstream culture.

    In a group of people, once a few kneel before some figure or thing and begin to worship, others too will involuntarily kneel and worship.

    From these phenomena we discover that the power of the collective subconscious is far greater than that of individual manifest consciousness.

If we trace back to the source, we will discover that our choices and decisions never really escape the circle of the collective subconscious. Our choices and decisions are completely controlled by the collective subconscious.

    At such a moment, we think of the warning words of Jesus the Christ: 'Enter through the narrow gate. Put into plain language, this means: 'The road the masses walk leads to death.'

    The road the masses walk is in fact caused by the collective subconscious. The road the masses walk is relatively safe, but its result is tragic. From this we can judge what result our present conduct will lead to. If what we do accords with the wishes of the masses, then certainly there will be less resistance, greater safety, and more smoothness, but the result is destruction. If what we do is always out of tune with the masses, and the road we walk is one of great resistance, many difficulties, and opposition from the masses, then the result is instead eternal life.

He says: 'The vulgar are bright and clear, I alone am dim and dull; the vulgar are sharp and discriminating, I alone am muddled and confused.' 'Knowing the male, keep to the female; knowing the white, keep to the black; knowing honor, keep to disgrace.' In plain words, when everyone else goes into the city, I go to the countryside; when everyone else is pragmatic, I attend to the intangible. In any case, one must not let the collective subconscious control one's own choices and decisions.

    Now let us analyze why younger people seem to be born already knowing how to operate electronic new products such as computers and mobile phones.

    This is not caused by the collective subconscious at all. Then what causes it?

    It is caused by cosmic consciousness.

    IV. The Saint Has No Dreams: Verification of Subconscious Purification, Full Text of Analyzing the Principle of 'The Saint Has No Dreams' Through Urgently Searching for a Toilet in a Dream¶

    (Xuefeng Corpus, X09 Essays, Analyzing the Principle of 'The Saint Has No Dreams' Through Urgently Searching for a Toilet in a Dream, complete original text):

    Zhuangzi said, 'The perfected person has no dreams,' which later evolved into 'the saint has no dreams.' What is the principle behind this?

    In our dreams we are often anxious to go to the toilet because of an urgent need to urinate, yet we are always unable to find a suitable place. This is the subconscious warning us that the bladder is full and the problem of urinary pressure needs to be resolved.

    If there is no urine in the bladder, we will not have dreams of anxiously going to the toilet. Only when the bladder is full of urine will we have such dreams.

    From this we discover a pattern, namely: if there is no physiological problem in us, then we will not dream. Once there is a physiological problem, we will have dreams related to it.

    The overwhelming majority of people have nightmares, whether they are being chased by ghosts, monsters, villains, or the wild beasts they fear, or whether floods arrive or mountains collapse and the earth splits. This shows that there is fear and anxiety within us. It shows that if there is a problem in our spiritual world, we will likewise have corresponding dreams.

    Thus we arrive at a conclusion: when a person has physiological or psychological problems, the subconscious will alert us through dreams.

    Dreams are warnings that there are problems in our own physiology and psychology.

    The above conclusion leads to another phenomenon: the more dreams a person has, the more it proves that many physiological and psychological problems exist; the fewer dreams a person has, the more it proves that there are very few physiological and psychological problems; if one simply does not dream at all, that proves there is not the slightest problem in physiology or psychology. Thus, the principle of 'the saint has no dreams' becomes clear. Because the saint maintains balance in both physiology and psychology, the saint does not dream.

    If the above analysis is reasonable, then whether one dreams or not becomes one of the touchstones for measuring the results of a person's cultivation and refinement.

    If one dreams a lot, it proves that cultivation and refinement have not reached the mark. If one dreams less and less, it proves that cultivation and refinement are making great progress and the results are remarkable. If one almost no longer dreams, it proves that cultivation and refinement have already reached home.

    Please verify some of the following dream states.

    Urgently searching for a toilet proves that the bladder is full.

    Dreaming of eating food proves that the stomach is hungry.

    Dreaming of intimate contact with the opposite sex, or even obtaining sexual satisfaction, proves that you lack sexual life.

    Frequently dreaming of a certain person or certain people proves that you have debt relations or emotional entanglements with that person or those people.

    Dreaming that you are wearing new clothes proves that you lack clothing or are about to suffer loss.

    Nightmares indicate that there is worry and fear in your heart.

    Dreaming that your hands and feet cannot move and that you cannot even speak proves that the flow of the cardiovascular system is not smooth, and that you need to change your sleeping posture.

    Seeing excrement in a dream, or getting excrement on your hands or feet, tells you that it is time to bathe, wash your clothes, and clean up garbage in your surroundings.

    Other dream states can be inferred by analogy.

    Everything said above is only my inferential analysis. It is not necessarily entirely correct and is offered only for reference.
