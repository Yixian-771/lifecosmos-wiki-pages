## Abstract

Sound Wave Energy is Lifechanyuan's systematic treatment of the relationship between sound and LIFE energy. Xuefeng builds on a physics framework (frequency → energy → effect) and deploys four categories of evidence — Chladni figures (experimental physics), the Guanyin Heart Mantra and Amitabha pronunciation (spiritual practice), Chinese literary and historical accounts (Meng Jiangnu, Dou E), and world religious/cultural traditions (Jericho, the Egyptian pyramids) — to argue that sound waves exert real effects on the material world and on LIFE. The argument concludes with a practical binary classification and a subjective criterion: the sounds you enjoy are beneficial; the sounds you dislike are harmful. The framework also has an ethical dimension: since sound wave effects are bidirectional, cursing another person simultaneously harms oneself.

## III. The Chladni Figures as a Scientific Anchor

Ernst Chladni (1756–1827) was a real physicist and the Chladni figures are a verified physical phenomenon — sand sprinkled on a metal plate vibrated at specific frequencies arranges itself into geometric patterns corresponding to those frequencies. Xuefeng uses this as scientific confirmation that "sound waves do indeed change the material world," and then extrapolates this principle to larger-scale phenomena (snowfall in June, collapsing walls, floods and droughts).

## IV. Pronunciation Precision in Spiritual Practice

Xuefeng's treatment of Amitabha pronunciation carries two significant implications:

1. **Precision of sound waves**: A single vowel difference (*ā*/*ē*) produces diametrically opposite effects — one "falls into a dark abyss," the other "enters the Kingdom of Heaven"
2. **Rigor of cultivation practice**: Chanting is not a symbolic ritual but a real sound wave energy operation that requires precision

This situates Sound Wave Energy within Lifechanyuan's framework of cultivation practice.

## VI. The Bidirectional Effect: An Ethical Implication

"When a person curses someone, they harm the other person and harm themselves simultaneously."

This extends the framework from outward acoustic effect (sound affects others) to bidirectional acoustic effect (sound simultaneously affects the speaker), generating an ethical argument against harmful speech: cursing is not only a moral wrong but a self-inflicted injury to LIFE energy.
