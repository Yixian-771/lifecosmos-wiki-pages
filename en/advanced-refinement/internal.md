# Advanced Refinement · Source Text Edition

> This edition presents all original texts by Guide Xuefeng on Advanced Refinement — the founding summary, the philosophical foundation, and all ten methods — arranged in chronological order of composition, with editorial notes situating each piece within the broader refinement framework.

## The Founding Statement

*Lifechanyuan Corpus · Cultivation Practice · "Advanced Refinement" — Xuefeng*

Advanced Refinement is the practice undertaken after achieving celestial being status. If your heart still holds concepts of parents, children, family, ethnicity, or nation; if you have not yet tasted all the bitterness and sweetness of human life; if you have not yet read the *Bible*, the *Quran*, the Buddhist Sutras, and the *Tao Te Ching* — you are not yet able to begin. Currently, no one in human society meets these conditions, so there is no need for explanation.

> **Editorial Note:** These three sentences establish the central paradox of Advanced Refinement: the founding text declares that no one currently qualifies — and then Guide Xuefeng proceeded to write ten detailed method articles. The paradox is intentional. Advanced Refinement's description is both a terminal destination and a working map: knowing what the destination looks like is itself part of the journey. The three prerequisites form a natural progression. Freedom from family-and-nation concepts is the outcome of Resolving the Heart (Intermediate Refinement's culmination). Having tasted all human experience is the completion of Giving the Whole Heart (Elementary Refinement's culmination). Having studied all five sacred traditions is the fruit of Rectifying the Heart (Intermediate Refinement's fourth method). Advanced Refinement's starting point is exactly where the two preceding stages converge.

## Philosophical Foundation: Dialogue with the Subconsciousness

*Other Writings by the Guide · October 6, 2007 — Xuefeng*

Jesus Christ said: "I am the way, the truth, and the life."

The Buddha Śākyamuni said: "Above heaven and below heaven, I alone am honored."

Lifechanyuan's Guide says: "I am the messenger of the Greatest Creator, the embodiment of Jesus and Śākyamuni. I am the wisest person in the world. I have come to the human world to 'harvest the ripe grain' — to inaugurate the Lifechanyuan Era for humanity."

Are these three people arrogant and delusional? Let me reveal the secret.

Freud coined the term "subconsciousness" — the unseen, untouchable impulse that continuously shapes human behavior without our awareness.

What is the subconsciousness? It bears some resemblance to the Buddhist ālayavijñāna, but is not the same.

[Discussion of the Conversations with God author, the channeler Jane Roberts, Chinese diviners, Zhuge Liang's predictions, telekinesis, and other phenomena...]

The answer to all these mysteries lies in the "subconsciousness."

In fact, "subconsciousness" is a name imposed for convenience — what Laozi would call "forced naming" (*qiáng wéi míng*). The name limits its range and fluidity; it can only serve as a starting point.

The entire universe is the subconsciousness — from the distant past to the infinite future, from human society to the twenty parallel universes, from our current dimension to the thirty-six-dimensional space. This vast spacetime network is the subconsciousness.

Memory cannot be found by dissecting the brain, because memory resides in the subconsciousness, not in the brain. The brain is only a bridge between the conscious mind and the subconsciousness — just as there is no image inside the television set itself.

The subconsciousness is difficult to speak of — it can only be sensed, not fully articulated. Like the Buddha's wordless transmission at Gṛdhrakūṭa, expressed only by the gesture of a lifted flower: once spoken fully, it distorts. Yet it cannot go unsaid. What follows is a "forced description" — a starting point, not the thing itself.

**The Eight Characteristics of the Subconsciousness:**

1. Boundless and formless — infinitely far, infinitely near — invisible and untouchable.

2. Active across all spacetime.

3. Indifferent to right and wrong, true and false, good and evil.

4. Responsive to all requests — plant melons, get melons; plant beans, get beans. Whatever information is input, that is what it returns.

5. Connected to all LIFE — you can communicate with ants and flowers without worrying whether they understand; the subconsciousness will translate and transmit your thoughts accurately in the form they can receive.

6. Unreserved and wholehearted in all it does.

7. Contains everything.

8. Belongs to the domain of the negative universe.

[Examples of how careless words, habitual fears, and vivid desires activate the subconsciousness and shape outcomes...]

Those who understand this principle have achieved small awakening (*xiǎo kāiwù*). Truly understanding not just that this is so but why it is so requires entering the chaos-thinking state (*hùndùn sīwéi*). But even this small awakening is already extraordinary.

Sorry — I have brought you to the door. The door is open. Walk through it yourself. To say more would be to transgress.

> **Editorial Note:** This essay is the philosophical foundation of all ten Advanced Refinement methods. It redefines "subconsciousness" not as an individual psychological layer (as in Freud) but as the entire universe — a spacetime network that receives and mirrors every intention. The eight characteristics reveal the operational logic: the subconsciousness is a perfectly neutral amplifier. Characteristic Three (indifferent to good and evil) and Characteristic Four (responsive without discrimination) together establish why clarity of intention matters so much — the subconsciousness executes whatever signal it receives, accurately and completely. This is the philosophical ground for Method Eight (express yourself clearly): vague inputs produce vague outputs. The "forced naming" framing echoes Laozi's opening line — the Guide signals that "subconsciousness" is a doorway, not a destination. The reader is explicitly instructed to eventually discard the label.

## Method One: Ascend and Revive in Love

*Lifechanyuan Corpus · Cultivation Practice — Xuefeng*

Seeds germinate in soil. Eggs hatch at the right temperature. Vinegar sours through fermentation. Particles produce energy in collision. A majestic structure takes generations to grow from a seed. Everything requires the right conditions — without them, iron cannot become steel.

The core of LIFE lies in its non-material structure. What the structure is determines the quality of LIFE. Different governing structures determine how nations flourish or decline; similarly, different LIFE structures determine the quality of a person's existence. To become a celestial being, one must transform at the level of LIFE structure.

What constitutes LIFE structure? It is primarily constituted by LIFE's consciousness. Therefore, to become a celestial being, we must refine ourselves at the level of consciousness.

The core ingredient in higher LIFE consciousness is love. The higher the LIFE form, the greater the proportion of love in its consciousness. The lower the LIFE form, the smaller that proportion. Survey a happy family and an unhappy family — in the unhappy family, everyone lacks love; in the happy family, everyone is full of it.

Since LIFE essence lies in structure, structure is primarily consciousness, and the love-content of consciousness determines which dimensional space a LIFE inhabits — our Advanced Refinement begins with love.

How to begin with love? First: understand what love is, grasp its scope and depth. Then: practice love. [Love as self-sacrifice completing the self, not diminishing it — participate in building Lifechanyuan as the practice; love resides in the process, not the outcome.]

*Note: This content was originally not to be made public. It is published here as a gift to the Three Sisters...*

> **Editorial Note:** This method establishes what might be called the physics of LIFE: **structure → consciousness → love** is the three-layer hierarchy underlying all Advanced Refinement. Love here is not a sentiment but a structural component — the primary material of high-dimensional consciousness, as foundational as carbon is to organic chemistry. The nation-structure analogy is key: what determines whether a country flourishes is not its people's moral effort but the design of its governing system. Similarly, the LIFE that enters higher-dimensional spaces is not necessarily more morally praiseworthy — its LIFE structure simply contains more love-consciousness. The note that this content was "originally not public" is significant: it confirms that the founding paradox ("no one currently qualifies") was genuine, but the Guide chose to release the map anyway, for those already on the road.

## Method Two: Learn Supernatural Transformation

*Lifechanyuan Corpus · Cultivation Practice — Xuefeng*

All things arise from consciousness. To attain the status of a celestial being, one must learn self-transformation — its principle is subtle, profound, and unfathomable.

*[The story of Hú Zǐ and Jì Xián from Liezi · Chapter on the Yellow Emperor:]*

A divine shaman named Jì Xián came from Qi to Zheng. He could predict anyone's life and death, fortune and calamity, longevity and early death — with month-and-day precision. The Zheng people would run from him when they saw him coming. Liezi was captivated, and told his teacher Hú Zǐ. Hú Zǐ said: "Bring him to read my face." The next day, Jì Xián read Hú Zǐ and said: "Your teacher is dying — not ten days remain." [Three more visits follow, each time Hú Zǐ presents a different internal state — earth-pattern, heaven-earth balance, supreme void, then finally the groundless root. Each time Jì Xián reads something different, and on the fourth visit he flees in terror.]

What Hú Zǐ demonstrated was supernatural transformation.

*[The story of Hào Èr who became a tiger through years of absorbed fascination with tiger paintings:]*
*[The story of painter Zhào Zǐáng who dreamed he became a horse through total immersion in painting horses:]*

The deep truth is contained within these stories. May you too develop supernatural transformation. Sun Wukong had seventy-two transformations — hopefully you can surpass him with ten thousand.

> **Editorial Note:** All three stories illustrate a single proposition: **all phenomena arise from mind** (*wànfǎ wéixīn zào*). The crucial insight is not that transformation is miraculous but that it is mechanical — consciousness penetrates deeply enough into an object, and the boundary between self and object dissolves. Hú Zǐ's four transformations (earth pattern / heaven-earth / supreme void / groundless root) are a sequential demonstration of chaos-thinking (*hùndùn sīwéi*): a master practitioner can inhabit any "form" and abandon it without residue, making it impossible for an observer to "read" or categorize him. The Hào Èr and Zhào Zǐáng stories are inadvertent demonstrations — their consciousness was absorbed without intent, and transformation still occurred. The implication: intentional, directed absorption is even more powerful. This method does not instruct through steps; the stories are the instruction.

## Method Three: See the World with Eyes Closed

*Lifechanyuan Corpus · Cultivation Practice — Xuefeng · December 4, 2006*

Conventional thinking has constrained us too long — it assumes that closing your eyes means seeing nothing. In fact, you see more with your eyes closed, and more clearly.

We dream with our eyes closed — and in dreams we see an entire world.

People near death, whose bodies lie with eyes closed, can truly see everything.

The physical eye can only see the material world. The non-material world can only be accessed when the physical eye is at rest.

[Discussion of 3D/4D hidden-image pictures; parallels to space telescopes seeing more clearly without atmospheric interference; the author's own first experience of seeing the non-material world while lying in bed with eyes closed under a blanket...]

I cannot tell you exactly how to see the world with your eyes closed — you must explore and accumulate your own sense of it. But four things will help:

1. **Stillness** (*jìng*).
2. **A mind that is not scattered** (*sīxù bù fēnluàn*).
3. **A childlike quality** (*háitóng xìng*).
4. **Turning-identification** (*zhuǎn shí*) — having the awareness of redirecting one's mode of knowing.

> **Editorial Note:** The four conditions are the most operationally specific guidance in the ten methods. Conditions one and two are prerequisites already cultivated through Intermediate Refinement (stilling the heart / settling the heart). Condition three — childlike quality (*háitóng xìng*) — is the entry point to Methods Three and Ten: children have not yet installed the "that's impossible" filter that blocks access to non-ordinary perception. Condition four — *zhuǎn shí* — is a technical term: *shí* (識) means consciousness/cognition/identification; "turning" it means redirecting the cognitive mode from sense-object grasping to open reception. This is not relaxation; it is a specific reorientation of the knowing faculty. The claim that memory does not reside in the brain, and that the non-material world is more real than the material one, follows directly from the cosmological framework established in the Subconsciousness essay: the entire universe is the subconsciousness, and brain-based cognition only accesses a small portion of it.

## Method Four: Concealment

*Lifechanyuan Corpus · Cultivation Practice — Xuefeng*

Everything extraordinary lies not in training the body or practicing postures — it lies in training the mind.

I have successfully concealed myself, disappeared — so I know some of the secrets. Not in the material world.

Concealment is extremely easy — it lies in a single flash of thought.

The key to concealment is mastering chaos-thinking (*hùndùn sīwéi*).

A person's capability is determined by their thinking. One step higher in thinking, and the difference between people becomes immeasurable.

[Discussion of the eight thinking levels and their progression...]

A person can compress themselves into a single cell — and can do so instantaneously.

In a given finite space: the greater the energy, the more formless; the smaller the energy, the more formed. A mountain is a mountain — but if you compress a mountain's energy into a matchbox, the mountain disappears, becomes invisible.

To become invisible: either compress yourself into a single cell, or absorb all of Earth's energy.

Difficult? No. The key is the shift in thinking.

This is actually a very enjoyable game. Give it a try in your free moments.

> **Editorial Note:** "The key to concealment is mastering chaos-thinking" places this method at the apex of the eight thinking levels: *wùzhì* → *xíngxiàng* → *liánxiǎng* → *míhuàn* → *xīnxiàng* → *tàijí* → *wúxiàng* → *hùndùn* (material → image → associative → psychedelic → heart-image → taiji → formless → chaos). Only at the chaos-thinking level does consciousness become fully fluid — without fixed reference to a stable "self-form," making invisibility a natural consequence rather than a trick. The energy-density paradox ("greater energy = more formless") inverts ordinary intuition and is key: what makes a celestial being "invisible" to ordinary perception is not that they have less, but that their energy is too vast and undifferentiated to register as a specific form. The casual "this is a very enjoyable game" is characteristic of Advanced Refinement's overall tone: at this level, what previously looked like cosmic mystery has become play.

## Method Five: Roaming the Thinking Labyrinths

*Lifechanyuan Corpus · Cultivation Practice — Xuefeng · December 15, 2006*

The highest-level game is the game of thinking. Thinking has many labyrinths; only the wise can navigate them. Here are some, as advanced refinement exercises.

Where is the beginning and end of a circle?

If you could travel in time to your great-great-grandmother's era and kill her — could you be born? If you couldn't be born, she would survive, so you'd be born after all. And if you're born, you kill her again...

An aircraft flying in a straight line traces a curved path. Does this mean straight = curved?

"None of us will say the word 'dirty.'" Where is the problem in this sentence?

"There are no absolute truths." Is that statement itself an absolute truth?

"The Tao that can be spoken is not the eternal Tao." If it cannot be spoken, how is it being spoken now?

[Russell's Barber Paradox · Socrates' Paradox · Achilles and the Tortoise · Zeno's Arrow · The Bandit's Dilemma · The Omnipotence Paradox · "White Horse Is Not a Horse" · The Two-Sentence Loop · The Prophet's Daughter Paradox · Socrates' Student...]

These are some thinking games — both collected and invented — for you to play.

> **Editorial Note:** The purpose of collecting these unsolvable paradoxes is not to solve them — it is to train the mind to inhabit irresolution without distress, and eventually without even noticing the irresolution. This is the path into chaos-thinking: not the elimination of contradiction but the transcendence of the need to resolve it. Most human discomfort in the face of contradiction comes from the assumption that contradiction is a problem requiring a solution. At the level of Advanced Refinement, contradiction is the texture of reality — especially at cosmological scales. The inclusion of "The Tao that can be spoken is not the eternal Tao" among the paradoxes is a meta-move: Laozi's own founding statement is a labyrinth, and by placing it here, the Guide signals that even sacred formulations are thinking-games, not fixed walls. "The highest-level game is the game of thinking" — this line is the Guide's most direct characterization of Advanced Refinement's nature: at this stage, the universe has become a playground, not an obstacle.

## Method Six: Playing the Five-Element Game

*Lifechanyuan Corpus · Cultivation Practice — Xuefeng · December 27, 2006*

The total energy of the universe sums to zero — this is the principle of "one yin, one yang makes the Tao; excess of either makes disease." The zero state (*líng tài*) is therefore the perfect state, the state capable of instantaneous transformation into anything — this is the nature of the universe.

[Discussion of seeing the nature (见性): once you see it, everything becomes simple; everything becomes a game worth playing; reaching this is becoming celestial...]

The game has strict rules — understand them and you can "act freely without transgressing" and "navigate with ease." Without understanding the rules, you're a headless fly crashing into walls.

The Five Elements are: Metal, Wood, Water, Fire, Earth.

**The Five Elements generate each other:** Metal generates Water; Water generates Wood; Wood generates Fire; Fire generates Earth; Earth generates Metal.

**The Five Elements overcome each other:** Fire overcomes Metal; Metal overcomes Wood; Wood overcomes Earth; Earth overcomes Water; Water overcomes Fire.

[Extended example: Zhang San's birthdate, eight characters, five-element analysis — how to interpret the imbalances and navigate toward equilibrium, including direction, color, profession, relationship choices...]

When you play this game well, you can mobilize the entire universe — a butterfly's wingbeat in Alaska can set off a storm at the Cape of Good Hope.

From playing the Five-Element game, one arrives at heaven-human unity (*tiānrén héyī*).

*Note: Play this in leisure, after completing Elementary and Intermediate Refinement. Do not become a fortune teller!*

> **Editorial Note:** The foundational statement — "the universe's total energy sums to zero, and zero is the perfect state" — is the cosmological basis for Method Seven (constant zero-state). The Five-Element game is the practical interface for this cosmology: not abstract theory but an operational system for perceiving and adjusting the energy flows between heaven and human. The instruction "do not become a fortune teller" marks an important boundary: the goal of Five-Element play is to experience heaven-human unity — to sense oneself as a node in the universal energy network — not to calculate personal advantage. "See the nature (*jiàn xìng*) and everything becomes a game": this sentence defines the phenomenology of Advanced Refinement — not a state of strain but of effortless play, once the nature is glimpsed.

## Method Seven: Constant Zero-State

*Lifechanyuan Corpus · Cultivation Practice — Xuefeng*

Reading a student's reflection on "the middle-way experience," I was suddenly illuminated — as if another window of the heart had opened onto a boundless cosmos. Her reflection is a further development of Laozi's "acting without action generates everything" and the Buddha's formless-thinking.

**Laozi's "non-action" (*wúwéi*), the Buddha's "formlessness" (*wúxiàng*), and the middle-way are all pointing to the zero-state (*líng tài*).**

What is the zero-state? It is a condition of "empty luminous elegance" (*kōng líng xiù*). Everything useful is in the zero-state.

Every weighing scale rests at zero. Every measuring instrument points to zero. Only from zero can it accurately register what is placed before it. If a scale begins at 80, it cannot tell you the weight of anything — only 80-plus-the-weight. The zero-state is the precondition for all accurate reception.

"Acting without action generates everything" — "all sages differ in their no-action" — "no sensation in order to experience all sensation." Laozi, the Buddha, and Qiánkūn Yuánchū stand on the same horizon of wisdom.

Only in zero-state can a building be useful. Only in zero-state can a computer serve. Only in zero-state can a practitioner manifest buddha-nature. Only in zero-state can a person achieve celestial quality.

"When wind blows through sparse bamboo, the wind passes and the bamboo retains no sound. When geese cross a cold pool, the geese leave and the pool retains no reflection." This is the zero-state of the bamboo and the pool.

Maintain the zero-state — this is Advanced Refinement.

The most exquisite swordsmanship uses no sword. The most exquisite heart-method uses no heart. The most profound Buddha-Dharma contains no Buddha. The greatest self is no-self. All of these are forms of the zero-state.

Moving toward the zero-state is moving toward the Buddha, toward the celestial being, toward the suchness (*rúlái*).

Whatever one is attached to — an event, an object, a theory, a religion, a political party, a nation, a family, a person — is movement away from the Tao, away from the zero-state.

The zero-state is not emptiness. It contains "the root of LIFE" — the hidden transformation of heaven and earth, forces of immense potency, infinite variation, ten thousand appearances. **The zero-state is nothing, yet is everything.**

Remaining in the zero-state is the experience of heaven-human unity, of living in the permanence of LIFE.

> **Editorial Note:** This is the philosophical apex of the ten methods — the point where Intermediate Refinement's terminal state (Resolving the Heart) and Advanced Refinement's operating state (zero-state) are shown to be the same thing at different depths. The key move: Laozi's *wúwéi*, the Buddha's *wúxiàng*, and the middle-way (*zhōngdào*) are declared equivalent to the zero-state. This is not a casual comparison — it is the Guide's claim that all three traditions, articulated independently, converged on the same inner structure. The scale analogy is the most precise formulation of zero-state's nature: not emptiness (a scale with no platform), but maximal receptivity (a scale resting at zero, ready to register anything). "Wind passes and bamboo retains no sound" — this is the same line used in Intermediate Refinement's Method Six (Settling the Heart), but at a higher level: Settling the Heart actively works to not retain; the zero-state has no residue to manage. **"The zero-state is nothing, yet is everything"** — this sentence is arguably the most concentrated philosophical statement in all of Advanced Refinement.

## Method Eight: Express Yourself Clearly

*Lifechanyuan Corpus · Cultivation Practice — Xuefeng*

I asked the Greatest Creator: "Why do so many people's wishes fail to come true?"

The Greatest Creator answered: "Because they have not expressed themselves clearly."

[The Guide reflects on observing people's condition after returning from the non-material world, confirming this diagnosis...]

We have not expressed clearly where we want to go, what we really need. Our hearts shift with circumstances — sometimes three times in a single day — so the Greatest Creator cannot cooperate with us.

For example, "I want to be rich" — this wish does not express its content clearly. How rich? In what way? Material wealth? Spiritual wealth? What is the standard? Without clarity, the wish cannot be fulfilled — not because the cosmos is unfair, but because we have not expressed ourselves clearly.

Similarly, "I want to go to the celestial realm" — which one? The Thousand-Year World? The Ten-Thousand-Year World? With what kind of life? What does it look like? Without a clear image, the Greatest Creator cannot cooperate.

There are a thousand beds — we can only sleep in one. A hundred ships — we can only board one. Hundreds of flights — we can only take one. Which bed, ship, flight? If we don't decide, we circle and circle and never depart.

[Two satirical passages demonstrating what "not expressing yourself clearly" looks like — dense spiritual-sounding language that says nothing specific:]

[Passage One: "XX's thinking is the active resonance of Bach's melodies in the quantum-mechanical electrolysis of its nature, the subtlety of its heart-method lies in the philosophical application of Pavlovian learning theory in Newtonian classical mechanics..." (continues for a long paragraph of meaningless combinations)]

[Passage Two: "Phenomenon is the spacetime-law reflection of the body, the actual projection of emptiness, the consciousness-image of self-nature in the form-realm..." (continues similarly)]

The above two passages are deliberate satire — hollow at their core. The more we read such writing, the more chaotic our thinking becomes, the more deeply we get lost in word-games. Those who have not expressed themselves clearly cannot be helped — because no one, and nothing in the universe, knows what they are actually asking for.

> **Editorial Note:** The practical import of this method connects directly to the philosophical foundation essay: the subconsciousness responds to whatever signal it receives, accurately and completely — "plant melons, get melons." Vague intention produces vague output. This is not a moral failing but an engineering problem: the input must be precise for the output to be precise. The two satirical passages are extraordinary — they are dense, impressive-sounding, and utterly without specific content. The Guide demonstrates the diagnosis precisely: these passages have no clear interior structure, no stated goal, no path. Reading them produces not clarity but confusion. "Expressing yourself clearly" is Advanced Refinement's version of the most practical spiritual work: know what you want, in specific enough terms that the universe can actually deliver it.

## Method Nine: Create a Celestial LIFE Structure and Absorb Special Energy

*Lifechanyuan Corpus · Cultivation Practice — Xuefeng · February 16, 2008*

[Extensive opening: why do sheep not eat fish while cats do? Why does asbestos insulate while metal conducts? Why do spheres roll and cubes don't? Why do flutes and violins make music but pots do not?]

**Conclusion: All differences among all things arise from structural differences.**

[Honeybee queen lives 4-5 years; worker bees live 2 months. Monkeys live 20 years; humans 60; celestial beings in the Thousand-Year World live a thousand years or more.]

**Conclusion: To live long — to achieve longevity — one must work at the level of LIFE structure.**

Thermodynamics introduces a term: *entropy*. The higher the entropy, the closer to death. An isolated, closed system's entropy necessarily increases to maximum — which is the death state. To prolong LIFE, one must prevent entropy from increasing. The most effective way: prevent your LIFE structure from becoming isolated and closed. The ideal: become a **dissipative structure** (*hàosàn jiégòu*).

"Flowing water does not stagnate; a door-hinge does not rot." The more open the structure, the longer the lifespan. A house sealed with windows and doors for years collapses. A company with no talent exchange declines. A country under prolonged isolation decays. A person who stops learning and thinking goes rigid and tends toward death. Why? Because in isolation, entropy only increases — eventually the system suffocates.

[Example: why do couples lose the intensity of new love? Because emotional entropy increases in a closed two-person system. "Half an acre of clear water reflects the sky and drifting clouds — ask how it can be so clear: because living water flows in from the source." (Zhang Zai)]

How does one build a celestial LIFE structure? Break the old consciousness structure with non-conventional thinking. Liberate the LIFE's consciousness from the confines of family, political organizations, religion, and nation. Let awareness soar freely across infinite spacetime. When our consciousness fully embodies celestial awareness, we have achieved celestial LIFE structure. When we have fully achieved it, we necessarily inhabit the celestial environment — this is the principle of like-attracts-like.

Having the celestial LIFE structure, one must also have the corresponding energy. A building designed without construction materials remains a design. How to absorb celestial energy?

**First, enter the non-material world.** In consciousness, place yourself on your own island in the Thousand-Year World, the Ten-Thousand-Year World, or the Elysium Islands. In this state, the needed energy is automatically absorbed.

**Second, create a pyramid in consciousness.** Imagine a pyramid of ten thousand stars — or build one from ten thousand flowers. Place yourself inside it, sitting cross-legged with hands in mudra position, or standing with palms open in a figure-eight position reaching upward. In this posture and state, special energy is absorbed.

**Conclusion:** Replace traditional closed structure with a dissipative structure. Do not erect constraints that limit consciousness's free soaring. This prevents mental entropy from increasing, prevents tendency toward death-state. Then envision yourself as a celestial being in the celestial realm — and in that state, absorb the special energy that a celestial LIFE structure requires.

> **Editorial Note:** This method introduces dissipative structure theory (Ilya Prigogine's work, Nobel Prize in Chemistry, 1977) into the context of LIFE refinement. Dissipative structures are systems far from equilibrium that maintain their organization by continuously exchanging matter and energy with their environment — the opposite of isolated, closed systems that tend toward entropy maximum. The Guide's application is precise: emotional and intellectual closure is the entropy of consciousness. The "flowing water does not stagnate" proverb is the ancient Chinese version of the same thermodynamic insight. The sequence — **structure determines what energy can be absorbed; energy reinforces and solidifies structure** — forms a positive feedback loop: begin by building celestial consciousness (Method One's love, Method Seven's zero-state, Method Three's open perception), and celestial energy will accumulate, further deepening the structure.

## Method Ten: Practice Dialogue with the Greatest Creator

*Lifechanyuan Corpus · Cultivation Practice — Xuefeng*

All events, all things, all traces are the language of the Greatest Creator. All phenomena, all processes, all transformations are the Greatest Creator speaking.

The language of the Greatest Creator is recorded in the **Wordless Book** (*wúzì shū*). Only those who can read the Wordless Book can know, understand, and be intimate with the Greatest Creator.

The sun, moon, and stars; rivers, lakes, and seas; mountains, valleys, and hills; birds, beasts, plants, and people — all are the Wordless Book.

The changing of oceans to mulberry fields; the roar of a lion; rain, wind, snow; the flowing of streams; cattle plowing; fragrance rising from flowers — all are the Wordless Book.

Day and night alternating; the four seasons cycling; clearing and clouding; growth and death; lightning and thunder; migrating birds — all are the Greatest Creator speaking.

Back aches and ear ringing; grief and longing; healing and damage; flying in dreams; eating and sleeping — all are the Greatest Creator speaking.

The Greatest Creator never speaks in language. Never records intentions in writing. Never enters a person's mind to give special instruction. Never tests or examines anyone. Never fears a god or demon seizing the throne. Never judges anyone.

The Greatest Creator treats all LIFE with complete fairness and justice. Whoever is willing — any person — can enter into dialogue with the Greatest Creator.

**The only way to dialogue with the Greatest Creator is through heart-spirit interaction (*xīnlíng hùdòng*).** To do this, one must first open one's *língjué* (spirit-awareness). Sight, hearing, smell, taste, and touch alone cannot accomplish the dialogue.

*[A practical example: the Guide is clearing branches entangled with vines and notices that dead branches are hard and difficult to cut; almost-dead branches are tough; actively growing branches are soft and easy. He asks the Greatest Creator what this means:]*

The Greatest Creator says: "**Dead LIFE is rigid. Near-death LIFE is tough. LIFE full of vitality is soft and yielding.** Look at children — how soft they are. They are the ones most full of vitality. Those who are curious about the future and expect good things from it; those who are constantly learning and readily embrace new ideas; those who are easily moved, easily taught, easily led — those are the ones brimming with LIFE vitality."

"**The celestial realm absolutely does not need rigid or tough LIFE. It needs LIFE full of vitality — people who are like children can enter the celestial realm.**"

This is my dialogue with the Greatest Creator. Everyone can practice this. We are surrounded by phenomena at every moment. The Greatest Creator is always ready to converse with anyone who is willing, humble, holds the Greatest Creator in their heart, and walks the path of the Greatest Creator.

> **Editorial Note:** The Wordless Book (*wúzì shū*) concept gives every natural phenomenon a double nature: it is simultaneously what it appears to be, and speech from the Greatest Creator. This is not metaphor — it is a literal account of how the universe communicates, without language, through the fabric of events. The key capability is *língjué* (spirit-awareness) — the faculty that translates natural phenomena into understood communication. This faculty is not available through the five physical senses; it opens through the combination of stillness (Method Three's first condition), childlike quality (Method Three's third condition), and the humility and inner orientation described here. The conclusion — "the celestial realm needs LIFE like children" — is the most direct statement of the human type that Advanced Refinement produces: not a solemn saint, but someone who is soft, curious, easily moved, easily led, with no internal authority and no fixed map. The child, paradoxically, is the most advanced form.

## Supplementary Text: Card-Flipping Is a Form of Advanced Refinement

*Other Writings by the Guide · April 9, 2019 — Xuefeng*

Those who possess supreme love (*zhì ài*) ascend to become celestial beings.

What is supreme love? Supreme love is an energy radiated from oneself, not directed toward any particular object, that brings other LIFE joy and spiritual vitality. The radiating sun is supreme love. A flower in full bloom is supreme love.

Card-flipping is one method and path for cultivating supreme love. [In Lifechanyuan communities, members write their names on cards and partners are assigned by random flip.] The process — who you "flip" to, you don't know in advance; only after flipping do you know — and the heart's response and how you handle it is the refinement of heart-nature. If you can respond with joy and warmth to whomever you flip — offering a few words that bring the other person happiness — this proves you have attained supreme love. If you burn with passion for those you like and freeze for those you don't — you have a long way to go.

**Three mirrors** that reveal how far cultivation has progressed:

1. Tell this person: give away everything you own — all your money, possessions, and loved ones — to others without reservation. Whether they can do this reveals the nature of their LIFE.

2. Tell this person: come with me now to a place you will never return from. If they follow without hesitation, it proves their worldly debts are cleared. If they need to collect money, sell the house, and say farewell — they still have unfinished human business.

3. Test this person with beauty, charm, money, and power. If they are moved and linger — they are still ordinary people; they will never reach the celestial realm.

Card-flipping contains all three mirrors simultaneously. [Discussion of the Buddhist teaching "응무소주이생기심" (应无所住而生其心) and how attachment to appearance, voice, or personality reveals one's distance from non-attachment...]

Perhaps some think card-flipping is trivial and shallow. But in my view, this simple game is the most profound cultivation method. When you have not yet awakened, when your cultivation foundation is not yet deep, when your heart is still human — this simple game becomes an uncrossable ravine.

> **Editorial Note:** This 2019 text extends Method One's love-as-structure into the most concrete possible daily application: a card game. Supreme love (*zhì ài*) as defined here — "energy radiated from oneself without any particular object, bringing others joy" — is structurally identical to what Sufism calls *al-Maḥabba* (the love station beyond which no station exists) and to what Christian theology calls *agape* (love that does not depend on the lovability of its object). The three mirrors are the most explicit evaluation criteria in all of Advanced Refinement: one-no-possessions, two-no-return, three-no-temptation. What appears as a simple game reveals with devastating clarity where the heart still clings. "A simple game is the most profound cultivation method" — this is the signature move of Lifechanyuan's approach: the most advanced practices appear elementary; what looks straightforward becomes the hardest test.
