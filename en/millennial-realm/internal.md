# Thousand-year World (Internal English Version)

## 1. Overview and Definition

The Thousand-year World is the entry-level heaven among the three major heavenly worlds (the Thousand-year World, the Ten-thousand-year World, the Elysium World), collectively known as Heaven.

It is described as a real, physical planet located approximately 960 light-years from Earth, ten times the size of Earth, currently inhabited by roughly 200 million beings. The beings (Celestial Beings) on this planet have a base lifespan of about one thousand years (relative to Earth time), hence the name "the Thousand-year World."

The Thousand-year World belongs to the positive universe. From a material perspective, it is a concrete, objective existence—visible and tangible—and all entities on it have lifespans.

Within the coordinate system of the twenty parallel worlds, the Thousand-year World exists in the xy-z world (while the human world is in the xy world, the Ten-thousand-year World in the x-y-z world, and the Celestial Islands Continent in the xyz world).

The Thousand-year World is purely a world of truth, goodness, beauty, love, peace, and joy—a world of elevated human nature.

Anyone who has attained perfect human nature can undoubtedly go there; there is no quota limit.

## 2. The Necessity of the Thousand-year World

The Thousand-year World, the Ten-thousand-year World, and the Celestial Islands Continent of the Elysium World are not imaginary constructs. They are necessities derived from the law that the sum of positive and negative energy equals zero—just as planets like Mercury, Saturn, and Mars must exist in the solar system, or just as the human body must have seven orifices, hair, limbs, and internal organs.

Guide Xuefeng explicitly states: The Thousand-year World, the Ten-thousand-year World, the Elysium World, and the Celestial Islands Continent within the Elysium World are real existences in the universe.

For beings who have never lived in heaven, these Celestial Worlds appear illusory. But for those who once lived in heaven, these descriptions trigger their spiritual intuition, instantly awakening a sense that these realms are the homeland they yearn for, with fragmentary memories faintly surfacing.

## 3. Hierarchical Position

Within the cosmic life hierarchy system revealed by Lifechanyuan, the Thousand-year World occupies the following position:

### First
The Thousand-year World is higher than the human world. It is one of the higher-life realms and the first heavenly layer that humans can reach through cultivation and refinement.

The six realms—Cool Realm, Celestial Being Realm, the Elysium World, Yin Pole Black Hole Body, the Ten-thousand-year World, and the Thousand-year World—are collectively known as the higher-life realms.

### Second
The life forms (Celestial Beings) in the Thousand-year World belong to the Human Celestial Being level.

Human Celestials have physical bodies, but their consciousness differs from ordinary humans. They understand the structure of the universe, know the thirty-six dimensions of space, understand the mechanisms of karmic transformation, possess no private property, engage in no opposition or competition, and embody perfect human nature.

### Third
The Thousand-year World serves as a transfer station from the human world to the Ten-thousand-year World and the Elysium World.

Humans first go to the Thousand-year World, Celestial Beings in the Thousand-year World strive toward the Ten-thousand-year World, Terrestrial Celestial Beings in the Ten-thousand-year World work toward becoming Divine Celestial Beings, and ultimately reach the Celestial Islands Continent of the Elysium World.

### Fourth
Although the Thousand-year World is still far from the worlds of Super Celestial Beings and Buddhas, it is nevertheless a world of elevated human nature—the first true Celestial World that humans can reach through cultivation.

## 4. Geography and Natural Environment

The geographical environment of the Thousand-year World is entirely different from Earth—a world completely free from human suffering.

Departing from Earth, it takes about sixteen minutes (at negative universe velocity, approximately 960 light-years) to reach the Thousand-year World, which is ten times the size of Earth.

The Thousand-year World has five continuous mountain ranges, each around 10,000 meters high, and 8,800 independent peaks rising from flat ground, ranging from 3,000 to 6,000 meters in height. Ten great rivers flow into a vast lake resembling a land of fish and rice. Within this expansive lake are nearly 100,000 islands surrounded by water. There are no deserts, no barren lands, no oceans—everywhere there are mountain springs, streams, waterfalls, and lakes.

**Climate:** The light in the Thousand-year World comes from many distant stars; there is no sun and no distinction between day and night. Objects have no shadows. The temperature is nearly constant, the sky is azure blue, and white clouds often drift by, changing colors as they float, inspiring endless imagination. It never rains or snows, there is no thunder or lightning, but clouds and mist often linger, and the climate is mild.

**Plants:** Plant species are innumerable. Trees are everywhere but not dense, remaining lush and green year-round, with flowers in constant bloom. One type of flower continuously changes color, emitting various exotic fragrances.

Plant growth in the Thousand-year World is extremely slow: a single leaf takes more than three years (Earth time) to fully form, and a peach takes about ten years to ripen. The lifespans of all animals and plants are approximately ten times those of Earth species.

**Animals:** The Thousand-year World hosts hundreds of thousands of land, air, and aquatic animal species, as well as terrestrial insects. However, there are no carnivorous animals or reptiles. Lions, wolves, foxes, snakes, and lizards are absent. Among insects, there are no flies, mosquitoes, bedbugs, fleas, centipedes, or millipedes. The most "cruel" event in the Thousand-year World is birds eating insects.

**Food:** Food is abundant everywhere. Fruits are readily available and extremely plentiful. After ripening, they remain fresh for about half a year without rotting, so there is always abundant food, and no one worries about hunger.

There are no carnivorous animals, no venomous snakes or spiders that harm people, so it is very safe.

## 5. Social Structure and Lifestyle

The people of the Thousand-year World come from three main sources: those who are native-born, those who descended from the Ten-thousand-year World due to violations, and those from Earth who have cultivated perfect human nature.

The Thousand-year World has no class society, no wealth disparity, no nations or ethnic divisions, no competition, opposition, war, robbery, or theft. There is no modern industry, agriculture, or animal husbandry, no cities or villages—but games and recreations abound.

The Thousand-year World has nearly two million villages. The largest villages have about 300 people, the smallest about 30. Village populations are not fixed but fluid; people often visit one another. Wherever they go becomes home, and staying three to five years is perfectly acceptable.

People in the Thousand-year World do not marry, so there are no families or clans. The social structure somewhat resembles matrilineal tribal societies on Earth. Intimate relationships between men and women form naturally and spontaneously, but unlike on Earth, sexual activity is infrequent—people have only three to four mating periods per year. Female fertility is extremely low; most bear only one or two children in a lifetime, rarely more than three.

The Thousand-year World has a management layer, but this management is entirely different from that in the human world: there is no one managing, let alone a management class. Everything runs in orderly fashion; everyone lives peacefully and joyfully, experiencing happiness, freedom, and abundance.

This is the manifestation of Hundun Management in the heavenly realm.

The two greatest laws are: First, no selfishness, no possessiveness; second, no waste.

The greatest punishment for breaking the law is exclusion from collective labor. The ultimate consequence is that, after a thousand years of life, the individual will descend to the Earth's human world to continue as a human being.

The people of the Thousand-year World do not eat meat. Their staple foods are fruits and some semi-cooked grains. They drink beverages made from honey, a tea-like leaf, and spring water. Fruits are abundant everywhere, available year-round.

Men's primary labor includes road repair, boat building, and house construction. Women's main work involves caring for children, raising silkworms, spinning, and weaving.

Clothing is made from thin, dyed silk woven from silkworm thread. Wearing these colorful, lightweight silk garments, people naturally exude a transcendent, ethereal Celestial Being aura.

There are no diseases—no congenital or infectious diseases, no illnesses caused by diet or labor, and no mental or psychological illnesses resulting from tense interpersonal relations or material scarcity.

The only mode of death is dying peacefully of old age without illness.

People reach adolescence at one hundred years old. Adolescence extends from age one hundred to over seven hundred. From seven hundred years until death is the cultivation period, also the period when individuals begin participating in the Thousand-year World management.

## 6. Ideology and Values

The spiritual and value systems of the Thousand-year World are fundamentally different from those of the human world on Earth.

Because objective reality is so different from Earth, people's perceptions, feelings, and thought processes differ greatly from those of Earth humans. There are no classes, no oppression or exploitation, no wealth disparity, no political organizations or religions, no competitive strife. Thus, people's mental states remain perpetually calm, natural, and harmonious.

There are no moral concepts as understood by Earth humans, no factionalism or pursuit of dominance. Everything is natural and spontaneous. There is no jealousy or rivalry, and there is no need for factories, shops, restaurants, or hotels.

People's greatest pleasure is participating in collective labor, followed by games and sightseeing.

There is no factionalism, no selling of one's soul, no desire-driven competition or emotional anxiety as in modern cities, no pursuit of fame and profit.

The interpersonal relationships among Celestial Being beings are those of siblings, relatives, and lovers. Romantic love unfolds naturally and spontaneously, without fixed partners, without possessiveness or jealousy.

The Joy Pavilion is a dedicated place for romantic enjoyment and intimate play. Sexuality in the Thousand-year World is sacred and noble. Ensuring that every Celestial Being being fully enjoys the freedom and happiness of sexual love is a shared value in the Thousand-year World.

The Thousand-year World is aware of Earth and its people but does not pay much attention to human affairs.

## 8. The Path to Entering the Thousand-year World

Entering the Thousand-year World requires passage through a spatial tunnel.

When the physical body dies, consciousness disappears instantly, but another form of consciousness is immediately born. Upon entering the spatial tunnel, one experiences physical and mental pleasure, boundless beauty, and exhilaration. Bundles of colored threads are seen flying backward from all directions. Just before reaching the Thousand-year World, one dimly sees a rainbow bridge in the distance. At the moment of seeing the rainbow bridge, consciousness is lost again, entering a sleep-like state. When consciousness re-emerges, one first hears beautiful Celestial Being music, then opens one's eyes to find oneself cradled in the arms of a female Celestial Being, surrounded by kind and respectful people cheering and celebrating one's birth—signaling arrival in the Thousand-year World.

Going to the Thousand-year World requires the first cosmic velocity; going to the Ten-thousand-year World requires the second cosmic velocity; going to the Elysium World requires the third cosmic velocity. Without reaching these speeds, one absolutely cannot go.

## 9. Relationship with the Ten-thousand-year World and the Elysium World

The three realms all belong to heaven but differ in level, and the difficulty of entry increases progressively.

The Thousand-year World belongs to the positive universe and has a material form. The Ten-thousand-year World also belongs to the positive universe, has a material form, and is more advanced than the Thousand-year World. The Elysium World belongs to the negative universe; everything there is illusory, time nearly disappears, and it is a higher-level life realm.

The life characteristics of the three realms ascend progressively:

The Thousand-year World is a world of elevated human nature, with human form, no marriage, no illness or disaster, and a lifespan of a thousand years.

Terrestrial Celestial Beings in the Ten-thousand-year World have lifespans of approximately 35,000 years, possess certain supernatural powers, and can ride clouds and mist.

Heavenly Celestial Beings in the Elysium World have no fixed form, can manifest in countless ways, can sense the consciousness of the Greatest Creator, and their ultimate destination is the Celestial Islands Continent.

Whether one can harmoniously integrate into Second Home life from the human world is the first test of how well one's spiritual garden has been cultivated. Whether one can harmoniously integrate into the Thousand-year World life from Second Home is the second step. From the Thousand-year World life, progressing to the Ten-thousand-year World life is the third step. The final step is entering the Celestial Islands Continent of the Elysium World.

When we possess the consciousness of the Thousand-year World Celestial Beings, we can go to the Thousand-year World.

When we possess the consciousness of the Ten-thousand-year World Celestial Beings, we can go to the Ten-thousand-year World.

When we possess the consciousness of Buddhas in the Elysium World, we can enter the Elysium World.

When we possess the consciousness and character of Super Celestial Beings, we can enter the Celestial Islands Continent.
