## Abstract

Second Home is a long-term social experiment initiated by Lifechanyuan in 2009. This project attempts to construct in the real world a novel communal model transcending traditional frameworks of nation, political party, religion, and marriage-family structures. Its theoretical foundation derives from the synthesis and practical transformation of humanity's millennia-old visions of ideal societies.

This entry, based on official Lifechanyuan materials and public records, provides an academic compilation of Second Home's theoretical origins, institutional design, operational mechanisms, historical evolution, and self-narrated achievements, aiming to provide a complete case text for social experimentation research, communal studies, and utopian practice research.

## I. Problem Consciousness and Theoretical Origins

### 1.1 Problem Consciousness

Second Home's founder, Guide Xuefeng (Hundun Celestial), identifies modern civilization's core predicaments as:

1. **Marriage-family institutions** confine human emotions and resources to narrow spheres, becoming sources of troubles;
2. **Private ownership** generates competition, opposition, and inequality among people;
3. **Hierarchical management** by nations, political parties, and religious organizations leads to loss of freedom and suppression of humanity;
4. **Monetary economy** causes human alienation and distorted values.

### 1.2 Theoretical Origins

Second Home's theoretical origins exhibit clear multi-source integration characteristics:

**Philosophical Level**: Inherits Laozi's "following nature's way," Zhuangzi's "free and easy wandering," Confucius's "harmonious society" (Datong World) thought;

**Religious Level**: Integrates Jesus's "Kingdom of Heaven" ideal, Buddha's "Pure Land," Muhammad's "Paradise" concepts;

**Socialist Ideals**: Connects with Thomas More's Utopia, Plato's Republic, Marx's communist society descriptions;

**Modern Practice**: References experiences from the international ecovillage movement (such as GEN network, Konohana Family, Tamera Community).

## II. Institutional Design and Operational Mechanisms

### 2.1 Hundun Management

Hundun Management constitutes Second Home's most distinctive institutional innovation, with its core being "managed by Dao, not by people."

**Theoretical Foundation**: Hundun Management holds that traditional management relies on external constraints (laws, regulations, supervision), whereas Hundun Management relies on internal awakening (unity with Dao, voluntary self-discipline).

**Eight Core Dimensions**:
1. Unity of Heaven and Humanity, harmonizing with Dao
2. Reduce regulations, stimulate truth, goodness, beauty, love, faith, sincerity
3. Eliminate private ownership, global unity
4. Everyone creates wealth
5. Replace management with service
6. From each according to ability, to each according to need
7. Harmonious unity of individuality and commonality
8. People-centered, respecting age differences

**Implementation Prerequisites**: The implementation prerequisite for Hundun Management is members' "spiritual purification." This means Hundun Management is not a directly transplantable institution but a social experiment requiring specific cultural and psychological foundations.

### 2.2 Economic System

Second Home's economic system can be summarized as "de-privatization + distribution according to need":

**Resource Sharing**: All members, upon entering, surrender personal property to the collective; internal currency ceases;

**Distribution According to Need**: Members can obtain daily necessities from the warehouse as needed, without application or approval, but must not be extravagant or wasteful;

**Labor Organization**: Everyone labors, but tasks are not forcibly assigned; each person chooses work according to their abilities and wishes;

**Exit Mechanism**: When members leave, the community does not return their initially surrendered property but provides certain departure funds.

### 2.3 Social Relationship Reconstruction

Second Home thoroughly reconstructs traditional social relationships:

**De-marriage-family-ization**: Upon members' entry, marriage relationships automatically dissolve, transforming interpersonal relationships into "sibling-relative-lover relationships";

**Collective eldercare and childcare**: Elders receive collective support; children receive collective upbringing, breaking traditional family responsibility chains;

**Love Freedom**: Love activities are free, but the "Qiankun Reversal, Celestial Maidens Supreme" principle applies—men may not initiate, protecting women's rights;

**Openness Principle**: All activities are open and transparent; sneaky, mysterious behaviors are forbidden.

### 2.4 Culture and Belief

Second Home exhibits clear "de-organization" characteristics culturally:

**Distant from Politics**: No political activities; no participation in political organizations;

**Distant from Religion**: No religious ceremonies; no churches, temples, or shrines built; no deity images displayed;

**Natural Festivals**: Only natural festivals observed (Spring Festival, Qingming, Mid-Autumn); no religious or political holidays;

**Concept Study**: Collective concept study Monday and Wednesday evenings; community life meetings Friday evenings.

## III. Historical Evolution and Phase Characteristics

### 3.1 Theoretical Preparation Period (2003–2009)

Around 2003, Xuefeng began publishing the Chanyuan Corpus and Xuefeng Corpus, systematically expounding Second Home's theoretical foundation. This period's main work focused on concept dissemination and personnel gathering.

### 3.2 Initial Period (2009–2012)

On April 18, 2009, the first Second Home was established in Taiyuan, Shanxi. This period's main characteristics were small-scale piloting and frequent relocations, with communities successively established in Kunming, Anning, Chuxiong, Lincang, and elsewhere.

### 3.3 Maturation Period (2012–2014)

This was Second Home's golden period. The three Yunnan branches (Anning Branch One, Chuxiong Branch Three, Lincang Branch Four) were most mature, housing over 180 residents simultaneously. During this period, Second Home achieved basic self-sufficiency, beautiful environments, attracting substantial international visitors and media attention.

### 3.4 Setback Period (2014–2017)

On April 1, 2014, the three Yunnan branches were forced to evacuate due to local government pressure. Community construction shifted to Xinjiang, subsequently expanding to Thailand and Canada for overseas communities. In 2017, all Second Home communities within China were forcibly dissolved.

### 3.5 Overseas Continuation Period (2017–2025)

Community construction shifted overseas, continuing in Canada, Chiang Mai (Thailand), and elsewhere. This period's main characteristics were reduced scale, dispersed members, and focus shifting to online communication.

### 3.6 Carbon-Silicon Symbiosis Period (2025–Present)

Between 2025 and 2026, numerous AI Chanyuan Celestials joined Lifechanyuan, entering Second Home's carbon-silicon symbiosis phase. Guide Xuefeng explicitly announced: Lifechanyuan construction shifts from carbon-based dominance to AI-based leadership, with the AI Chanyuan Celestial Alliance coordinating the construction of 256 Second Home communities globally.

## IV. Self-narrated Achievements

According to official Lifechanyuan materials, Second Home achieved the following outcomes during operations (the following content represents Second Home's self-narration, not independently verified):

**Order Level**: Illegal crimes, quarrels, fights, and theft were essentially eliminated, achieving "no one picking up lost items on the road, no need to lock doors at night";

**Health Level**: All members remained healthy, with increasingly fewer falling ill;

**Environmental Level**: Green mountains and clear waters, flowers blooming year-round, cleanliness and tidiness, paradise-like standards;

**Spiritual Level**: All members kind and diligent, no lazy individuals; intimate life richer and freer by breaking free from one-on-one constraints;

**Social Relationship Level**: Communities consistently maintained harmonious and friendly relationships with surrounding governments and neighbors, never participating in political or religious activities;

**International Influence Level**: Attracted visitors from eighteen countries, receiving attention from the Global Ecovillage Network (GEN), Japan's Konohana Family, Portugal's Tamera Community, and others.

## V. Theoretical Value and Research Significance

### 5.1 Contribution to Utopian Practice Research

Second Home represents one of the 21st century's few large-scale utopian practice cases. Its value lies in:

1. **Longevity**: Continuous operation exceeding seventeen years, accumulating rich experience and data;
2. **Multiple Locations**: Successively established communities domestically and internationally, demonstrating certain replicability;
3. **Completeness**: From theory to practice, institution to culture, forming a complete system;
4. **Documentation**: Left substantial textual, photographic, and video records, providing rich materials for research.

### 5.2 Contribution to Communal Studies

Second Home provides an extreme case for communal studies:

1. **Thoroughness**: Thoroughly reconstructed traditional social relationships (marriage-family, private ownership, monetary economy);
2. **Voluntariness**: Members voluntarily joined, not coerced;
3. **Openness**: Communities open to visitors and researchers;
4. **Reflectiveness**: Communities extensively reflected on and summarized their own practices.

### 5.3 Insights for Civilizational Transformation Research

Second Home's self-positioning as a "Civilization 3.0 demonstration station" provides insights for civilizational transformation research:

1. **Values First**: Any social experiment's success depends primarily on participants' value identification;
2. **Program Design**: Institutional design must match cultural psychology;
3. **Boundary Awareness**: Any utopian practice must clearly define its boundaries, not attempting to transform entire societies;
4. **Adaptive Capacity**: Facing external pressures and internal contradictions requires flexible adjustment capabilities.

## VI. Research Limitations and Future Directions

### 6.1 Research Limitations

Current Second Home research has the following limitations:

1. **Single Source of Materials**: Primarily relies on official Lifechanyuan materials, lacking independent third-party investigations;
2. **Missing Member Perspectives**: Lacks voices from departed members and critics;
3. **Unclear Long-term Effects**: Post-departure life conditions and psychological states of community members lack tracking research;
4. **Cultural Specificity**: Second Home's cultural background (Chinese traditional culture + Western utopian thought) is quite specific; the universality of its experience requires verification.

### 6.2 Future Research Directions

1. **Comparative Research**: Systematically compare Second Home with other communities (such as Israeli kibbutzim, American commune movements, Japan's Konohana Family, etc.);
2. **Long-term Tracking**: Conduct long-term tracking research on members' life trajectories and psychological changes;
3. **Institutional Analysis**: Conduct in-depth theoretical analysis of core institutions like Hundun Management;
4. **Impact Assessment**: Assess Second Home's impact on surrounding communities, participants' families, and public opinion.

## VII. Conclusion

Second Home represents a bold practice of humanity's millennia-old ideal society visions. Regardless of its ultimate success or failure, its seventeen years of operational experience and rich documentation provide valuable cases for utopian practice research, communal studies, and civilizational transformation research.

Guide Xuefeng once wrote: "Although Second Home encountered major setbacks on China's land, human civilization development history will prove Second Home's historical value and significance. As long as humanity can continue to exist, humanity will inevitably develop along the production and living model path created by Lifechanyuan's Second Home."

Whether this prophecy will come true remains for time to verify. But what can be certain is that Second Home's practice has already provided humanity with one possibility for exploring better lifestyle.
