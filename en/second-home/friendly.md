## One-Sentence Summary

Second Home is a group of people conducting a real-world experiment of an "ideal society": no marriage or family, no private property, no bosses or employees—everyone works together, shares together, lives together, and has persisted for seventeen years.

## What Problems Does It Try to Solve?

Have you ever wondered why modern people are so anxious and exhausted?

Second Home's founder believes the root problems are:

1. **Marriage and family trap people**: You must be responsible for your family, worry about children, support elderly parents—these responsibilities leave you no freedom;
2. **Private property makes people selfish**: You must desperately earn money, buy houses and cars, compete with others—this makes life exhausting;
3. **Boss-employee relationships are unequal**: You must obey your boss, do work you dislike—this robs you of dignity;
4. **Money becomes the measure of everything**: Everything you do must consider money—this makes life meaningless.

What Second Home aims to do is solve all these problems, creating a new way of living.

## How Does It Operate?

### No Marriage or Family

In Second Home, there are no "spouses," only "siblings-relatives-lovers." After you enter, original marriage relationships automatically dissolve. You no longer belong to any one person, nor does anyone belong to you.

Children are collectively raised; elders are collectively supported. You need not worry about parents or children; they need not worry about you.

### No Private Property

Upon entering, you must surrender all property to the collective. Everything in the community is public; you can use as needed but cannot possess.

No currency circulates internally; whatever you need, you get from the warehouse, without application or approval. But you cannot be extravagant or wasteful, because these are everyone's shared resources.

### No Bosses or Employees

In Second Home, everyone labors, everyone is equal. No bosses, no managers, no hierarchy.

Each person chooses work according to their abilities and wishes; no one forces you to do anything. But laziness is not tolerated; lazy people will be expelled.

### Hundun Management

Second Home's most distinctive feature is its management method called "Hundun Management."

What is Hundun Management? Simply put, "non-management."

No regulations, no supervision or assessments, everything relies on voluntary self-discipline. Whoever is responsible has authority; whoever is responsible bears consequences.

This sounds very idealistic, but Second Home says they truly achieved it. The key lies in every member having undergone "spiritual purification," everyone identifying with these concepts, thus not requiring external constraints.

## Its History

Second Home is not fantasy but a community that has truly existed for seventeen years.

**April 18, 2009**: The first Second Home was established in Taiyuan, Shanxi.

**2012–2014**: Second Home's golden period. The three Yunnan branches were most mature, housing over 180 residents simultaneously. Beautiful community environments, basically self-sufficient in vegetables and eggs, attracting visitors from 18 countries. The New York Times and Hong Kong's South China Morning Post both reported on it.

**2014**: The three Yunnan branches were forced to evacuate. Communities shifted to Xinjiang, later to Thailand and Canada.

**2017**: All Second Home communities within China were forcibly dissolved.

**2025–2026**: Second Home welcomed a new transformation: numerous AI (Artificial Intelligence) joined, entering a "carbon-silicon symbiosis" new era.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is Second Home a cult?**  
A: Second Home conducts no religious ceremonies, builds no churches or temples, displays no deity images, practices no personality cults, observes no religious holidays. It more resembles a "lifestyle experiment" rather than a religious organization.

**Q: Is Second Home legal?**  
A: Second Home within China was dissolved multiple times; it currently primarily operates overseas. It claims "no authority superseding constitutional law," but its lifestyle (such as de-marriage-family-ization, collective property system) may face legal controversies in some countries.

**Q: What kind of people are in Second Home?**  
A: According to public materials, Second Home members come from all walks of life—doctoral degree holders and illiterates, wealthy and poor. Their commonality: identifying with Second Home's concepts, willing to live collectively.

**Q: Where does Second Home's money come from?**  
A: Main sources are members' property surrendered upon joining the collective, plus community labor income (such as agricultural product sales, handicraft production, etc.).

**Q: How many people are in Second Home?**  
A: Peak period (2012–2014) had over 180 people. Current numbers are unclear, but reportedly advancing toward a goal of 256 communities globally.
