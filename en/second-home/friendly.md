## How Does It Operate?

### No Marriage or Family

In Second Home, there are no "spouses," only "siblings-relatives-lovers." After entry, marriage relationships dissolve. You no longer belong to any one person.

Children are collectively raised; elders are collectively supported. You need not worry about parents or children.

### No Private Property

Upon entering, you must surrender all property to the collective. Everything is public; you can use as needed but cannot possess.

No currency circulates internally; whatever you need, you get from the warehouse. But you cannot be extravagant or wasteful.

### No Bosses or Employees

Everyone labors, everyone is equal. No bosses, no managers, no hierarchy.

Each person chooses work according to their abilities and wishes. But laziness is not tolerated; lazy people will be expelled.

### Hundun Management

Second Home's most distinctive feature is "Hundun Management" — simply put, "non-management."

No regulations, no supervision or assessments, everything relies on voluntary self-discipline. Whoever is responsible has authority and bears consequences.

This sounds idealistic, but Second Home says they achieved it. The key: every member has undergone "spiritual purification," identifying with these concepts, thus not requiring external constraints.
