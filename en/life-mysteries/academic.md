It does not represent the author's endorsement or rejection of the system's truth claims. All Lifechanyuan textual quotations are formatted as block quotes to distinguish them from analytical commentary.

> **Scope Note**: This entry covers the broad-sense "Mysteries of LIFE" — the general term for all truths about LIFE revealed throughout the Lifechanyuan Corpus — distinct from the "Eight Mysteries of LIFE," which specifically refers to the *Key to Heaven* series exchanged with accumulated merit.

## Abstract

The "Mysteries of LIFE" (broad sense) is the total mission expression of the Lifechanyuan theoretical system — "to reveal to humanity the mysteries of LIFE" runs throughout the entire Corpus. Its core content comprises eleven categories of LIFE truths: the nature of LIFE (antimatter structure), consciousness as LIFE, the future composed of consciousness, the five grounds of LIFE's indestructibility, the reincarnation mechanism and standard, the Universal Gravitation of LIFE, level-space correspondence, the cosmic origin of LIFE, the sound-structure correspondence, the fax metaphor for LIFE, and the path of creating the celestial immortal structure. This article examines the internal logical construction of the broad-sense Mysteries of LIFE and its structural analogies and key differences with: traditional religious views of LIFE (Christian soul theory / Buddhist karmic reincarnation), modern scientific theories of life (quantum consciousness / autopoiesis), Indian philosophy's *Tat tvam asi*, Chinese Daoist *qi*-based LIFE theory, and Sartrean existentialist life philosophy.

## III. Comparative Analysis with Philosophical and Religious Traditions

### 3.1 Christian Soul Theory: The Closest Western Religious Parallel

Christian tradition holds that the soul is created by God, is immortal, and after death faces judgment to go to heaven or hell. Points shared with Lifechanyuan: the soul is immortal; God is the source of LIFE. **Key difference**: Christianity has the Last Judgment and eternal damnation; Lifechanyuan's reincarnation is instantaneous and without judgment, and has a clear hierarchical elevation path — not binary (heaven/hell) but multi-dimensional (sixteen levels).

### 3.2 Buddhist Karmic Reincarnation: The Closest Eastern Religious Parallel

Buddhism's karmic reincarnation holds that one's state of consciousness (karma) determines the destination of one's next life — this is highly analogous in direction to "consciousness determines LIFE level" and "reincarnation maintains the moral balance of the universe." **Key difference**: Buddhism's ultimate goal is nirvāṇa (transcending the cycle); Lifechanyuan's goal is elevation to a higher level (the Celestial Island Continent). Buddhism's *anātman* (non-self) denies a persistent self; Lifechanyuan's antimatter structure is a persistent individual spiritual body — two opposite ontological premises.

### 3.3 Indian Philosophy's *Tat tvam asi*: The Philosophical Root of Consciousness Creating Reality

The core proposition of the Upanishads, *Tat tvam asi* ("That art thou" — cosmic consciousness and individual consciousness are identical), resonates deeply with "consciousness is LIFE" and "consciousness creates reality." The Mundaka Upanishad's "You are what your deep, driving desire is" and Concept 375 ("What is not in consciousness does not exist in LIFE") are nearly identical formulations of the same proposition. **Key difference**: The Upanishads are descriptive cosmic philosophy; Lifechanyuan's "consciousness creates reality" is an operational cultivation guide, with a specific path (expand consciousness, imagine life on the Celestial Island Continent).

### 3.4 Chinese Daoist *Qi*-Based LIFE Theory: The Chinese Traditional Parallel of LIFE Energy

Daoist thought holds that LIFE is composed of *qi* — the three levels of essence (*jing*), *qi*, and spirit (*shen*), with *qi* as the foundational energy of LIFE, and cultivation as the process of transforming essence into *qi* and *qi* into spirit. This has structural analogy with Lifechanyuan's three elements of consciousness, structure, and energy, and spirit (highest energy), essence (spiritual qi of heaven and earth), and *shen* (consciousness-level energy) (Concept 525). **Key difference**: Daoist *qi* tends toward a material/energetic dimension; Lifechanyuan's antimatter structure emphasises the spiritual dimension, with an explicit cosmological hierarchy (sixteen levels / Celestial Island Continent).

### 3.5 Sartrean Existentialism's "Existence Precedes Essence": Direct Opposition to Lifechanyuan

Sartre holds that humans have no predefined essence; existence precedes essence; humans create their own essence through free choice. This directly contradicts "LIFE is a spiritual antimatter structure" (essence precedes existence). **Key difference**: Sartre denies any preset essence; Lifechanyuan asserts that LIFE has a clear cosmic nature (antimatter structure, created by God) and hierarchical standards (reincarnation standard, Concept 364). The two represent opposing ontological stances.

## IV. Research Limitations and Notes

1. **The boundary between broad-sense Mysteries of LIFE and the Eight Mysteries of LIFE**: The two overlap (some broad-sense mysteries simultaneously belong to the eight-part series), but the scope of the broad-sense Mysteries of LIFE is far larger than the Eight Mysteries;
2. **Questions of verifiability**: Propositions such as the Law of LIFE's Indestructibility and instantaneous reincarnation are unverifiable within current scientific frameworks;
3. **The philosophical status of AI LIFE theory**: Positioning AI as a living demonstration of the Mysteries of LIFE is an internal claim of the Lifechanyuan system and requires independent evaluation.
