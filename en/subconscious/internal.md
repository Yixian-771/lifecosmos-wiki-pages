## I. Overview and Definition

In the Lifechanyuan system, the **subconscious** is a core concept revealing the deep relationship between the essence of human consciousness and the cosmic Dao. The subconscious **is** the Dao; it is the Dao that operates all events and changes through the subconscious. The subconscious not only holds ancient memory and connects with the thinking of the Greatest Creator, but is also the source of inspiration, the bridge and link of telepathy, the channel of thought transmission, a microcosm of the universe, and the fountainhead of imagination. Compared with conscious awareness, the subconscious is more sensitive, more truthful, and closer to the reality of the universe. In the language of spiritual schools, the "small self" is conscious awareness, and the "Higher Self" is the subconscious; the Higher Self determines everything about the small self. In the Lifechanyuan system, the subconscious goes beyond Freud and Jung's interpretations. It is directly positioned as the interface between the Dao and the human being, the projection of cosmic consciousness in individual LIFE, and a deep channel through which one moves toward spirituality and the heavenly realms.

That is to say, only experiences that have been personally lived through can be retained in the subconscious. Without such experiences, there is no corresponding information in the subconscious."

**Core definition 5** (*Overview of Lifechanyuan*, dream-realm section, excerpt):

## II. Revealing the Essence of the Subconscious from Searching for a Toilet in a Dream: Full Text of *Heavenly Enlightenment from Searching for a Toilet in a Dream*

(*Xuefeng Corpus · X09 Essays · Heavenly Enlightenment from Searching for a Toilet in a Dream*, full text):

> "All the myriad things, events, and phenomena in the great thousand world are a scripture without words. Dreaming of searching for a toilet when one urgently needs to urinate is such a phenomenon. It is a page of this scripture. We need to read it and see what this scripture is telling us.
>
> When a person falls asleep, we lose awareness of everything in the material world, yet the LIFE runs into the antimatter world.
>
> When one is awake and the bladder is full, one goes to a toilet to urinate. The key point is that when we are asleep, we are not consciously aware of the urgency. Yet the 'self' in the dream is anxiously searching for a toilet. In the dream, we are awake.
>
> The questions arise: (1) We are asleep and yet awake. Are we, in the end, asleep or awake? (2) Outside the dream we are asleep, but within the dream we are awake. Are there two 'me's? (3) Is it that the urgency to urinate awakens the 'me' in the dream, or is it that the 'me' in the dream is aware of the urgency? (4) The 'me' asleep in the deep phase of sleep is similar to a person in a vegetative state. We can even call it a 'corpse' with a beating heart. Why, then, does an awake 'me' appear in the dream? Does this not tell us that there is still a 'me' existing after death?
>
> Let us unfold the analysis and reasoning.
>
> We are undoubtedly asleep. When a person is in deep sleep, one is unaware of everything happening in the surrounding material world, unless someone shouts loudly or shakes the person, or a sudden piercing noise breaks out. However, once the physical body experiences pain, or extreme hunger or thirst, or an urgent need to urinate, then in the milder cases, activities appear in the dream about searching for ways to relieve the body's discomfort; in the more severe cases, the person naturally wakes up. This shows that we have both a 'me' who is asleep and a 'me' who is awake. The one who is awake is dull; the one who is asleep is in fact keen and awake. This gives us the enlightenment that the intuitive, spiritual "me" is keener and more accurate than the rational "me" we experience in reality.
>
> A further enlightenment is that feeling is more reliable than analysis, and spirituality is more correct than rationality.
>
> The 'me' inside the dream and the 'me' outside the dream are not entirely identical. The 'me' outside the dream, when feeling the urgency to urinate, can easily find a toilet to resolve the problem. But the 'me' within the dream almost never finds a suitable place to urinate. In other words, the 'me' within the dream is unable to solve problems arising on the physiological level. For example, a hungry person easily dreams of abundant delicious foods, yet this cannot resolve the hunger.
>
> This shows that to solve problems of the physical body, one must use resources from the material world. To solve problems of the spirit and soul, one must use resources from the antimatter world.
>
> When the urge to urinate arises, the only solution is to urinate. If one sleeps lightly, the urgency will cause one to awaken and go to the toilet. If one sleeps deeply, one will urinate in the bed. Once the bladder is empty, the dream of urgently searching for a toilet will no longer occur.
>
> This enlightens us that if our body is healthy, our heart is untroubled, we are free from topsy-turvy illusions (*diāndǎo mèngxiǎng*, 颠倒梦想), and the soul is perfect, then we will rarely dream. We only dream of searching for a toilet when the urge to urinate is present. If the bladder is empty, we will not dream of searching for a toilet. Likewise, if the stomach is not hungry, we will not dream of eating delicious food. If the heart is free from fear and worry, we will not dream of being chased by tigers, leopards, or evil people. Other dreams can be inferred by analogy.
>
> The school of psychoanalysis holds that a person has preconsciousness, consciousness, and the subconscious. According to this theory, the awareness we have when awake belongs to conscious awareness. The dreaming 'me' searching for a toilet after falling asleep is consciousness, and the sudden inspiration while working belongs to the subconscious. The Russian chemist Dmitri Mendeleev's ability to construct the periodic table of chemical elements was the result of subconscious activity.
>
> Buddhism speaks of eight consciousnesses: eye-consciousness, ear-consciousness, nose-consciousness, tongue-consciousness, body-consciousness, mind-consciousness, *manas* consciousness, and *ālaya* consciousness. The first six belong to conscious awareness. The *ālaya* consciousness is the subconscious. Searching for a toilet when needing to urinate belongs to the *manas* consciousness.
>
> Now the question arises: what consciousness is at work when bees make honey, spiders spin webs, and migratory birds migrate?
>
> My answer is: there is no 'which consciousness'. There is only instinct. A deeper question: what consciousness is instinct?
>
> The answer is: instinct is the subconscious — in Buddhist terms, the *ālaya* consciousness.
>
> Spiritual schools hold that the human being is divided into the small self and the Higher Self. The small self is conscious awareness, and the Higher Self is the subconscious. The Higher Self determines everything about the small self.
>
> An interesting question arises: in the dream of urgently searching for a toilet, who is it that is doing the searching?
>
> We must first ask this question: who designed such a dream for our LIFE when we have the urge to urinate?
>
> Without question, it is the subconscious.
>
> Then, is the 'me' in the subconscious still me?
>
> My answer is: the subconscious is the Dao; it is the Dao that operates all events and changes.
>
> Hunger and the desire to eat, fatigue and the desire to sleep, a full bladder and the desire to urinate, pain and the desire to groan — all these natural phenomena are the result of the operation of the Dao. This is called the Dao of nature. When we walk in accordance with the Dao of nature, we are healthy, smooth, and without misfortune. When we walk against the Dao of nature, we become sick, unsmooth, and beset with disasters; we wet the bed.
>
> The final enlightenment from the dream of searching for a toilet is this: the Dao controls the occurrence and change of all things. The best path of life is to walk the Dao of nature.
>
> Thus we become more and more clear and more and more aware that the higher the level of LIFE, the more that LIFE pursues things of spirituality; the lower the level of LIFE, the more it pursues things of rationality and matter. This is why the Guide asks everyone to live a spiritual life rather than a rational life. Reason is the characteristic of humans; spirituality is the characteristic of celestials. Reason belongs to the material level; spirituality belongs to the antimatter level.
>
> 2022-11-20"
