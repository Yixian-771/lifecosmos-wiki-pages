## I. Overview and Definition

Free will is a core concept in the Lifechanyuan system for revealing the essence of human nature and humanity's relationship with the universe — human beings undoubtedly possess free will; this is the most fundamental attribute of LIFE bestowed by the Greatest Creator on every individual. Free will is the fundamental mark distinguishing humans from biological robots; it is the source of joy and suffering in human life, and it is the inner driving force through which one can move toward the Kingdom of Heaven or fall into Hell. Free will is not absolute freedom; its highest mode of use is to align one's will with the Way of the Greatest Creator, reaching the state of "doing as one wishes without transgressing the Dao" — this is the state of a Celestial Being.

**Core Definition I** (*800 Values for New Era Humanity*, Value 68, complete original text):

> Human beings undoubtedly possess free will — and it is precisely free will that is the root source of hesitation, worry, pain, fear, and sorrow in human life.

**Core Definition II** (*Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom · How to Use Free Will*, excerpt):

**Core Definition III** (*Xuefeng Corpus · X01 Inspiration · Live for Yourself, Not for Others*, excerpt):

> All LIFE created by the Greatest Creator is individual — each individual is given a unique appearance and nature, a free will and a complete biological system, and a mission completely different from that of any other person.

> Human beings undoubtedly possess free will — and it is precisely free will that causes the hesitation, worry, pain, fear, and sorrow of human life.

## II. The Cosmological Premise: Human life Is a Grand Script; Free Will Operates within It

Free will is not unlimited freedom. It operates within the framework of the cosmic grand script — the script's plot is the fixed element, free will is the variable, and the tension between the two is the source of joy and suffering in human life.

(*Chanyuan Corpus · Preaching · The Trajectory of Human life and Free Will*, complete original text):

> Across the vast sky there are far more celestial bodies — stars — than there are people on earth. Every celestial body has its own orbit; every celestial body's orbital path is fixed, not freely chosen by the celestial body itself.
>
> Human life also has a trajectory. Growth, aging, and death are an inescapable trajectory. The eighteen events in human life that one cannot manage oneself are the trajectory of human life. If examined, analyzed, and summarized in detail, every detail on the trajectory of human life has already been determined — including one's name, one's temperament and character at each stage of life, what one will say, what one will do, who one will meet, what misfortune one will encounter, what illness one will suffer, and what one's end will be — all of it has already been determined.
>
> The question arises: if the trajectory and details of human life have already been determined, does a person have free will?
>
> If so, where does free will manifest?
>
> Human beings undoubtedly possess free will — and it is precisely free will that causes the hesitation, worry, pain, fear, and sorrow of human life.
>
> The universe is one; the universe is holographic; the universe is a grand script. Everything that happens in human society is a passage of plot within this grand cosmic script. Every person is an actor on the great stage of human life — performing strictly according to a script's plot that was determined in advance, playing a role stipulated by the script.
>
> The psychological experiences of hesitation, worry, pain, fear, anxiety, dread, and sorrow in human life are caused by the conflict and contradiction between each person's free will and the script's plot. The script asks you to play a mouse, but your free will wants to play an elephant. The script asks you to live in a thatched house, but your free will insists on a five-star hotel. You are supposed to assist and serve someone, but you want that person to assist and serve you. You are supposed to grow vegetables, but you hope to build spacecraft. You are supposed to do evil, but you insist on doing good. These are instances of free will clashing with the script's arrangement. Whenever free will conflicts with the script's arrangement, the heart begins to hesitate, worry, suffer, feel anxious, fear, dread, and sorrow. If free will aligns with the script's arrangement, the heart becomes peaceful, joyful, happy, blissful, and free.
>
> The highest state of practice and cultivation is to eliminate the clinging to self and achieve "no self." Only by relinquishing the clinging to self and reaching the state of "no self" can one align with the Dao. When one aligns with the Dao, one has achieved right results.
>
> Another question arises: if everything has already been destined, what is the value and meaning of personal effort and striving?
>
> First of all, so-called effort and striving are almost meaningless in this sense — when the program is determined and the role to be played is determined, a person's effort and striving are also determined. That is to say, not wanting to make effort and strive is simply not possible. A series of events forces one to make effort and strive. The nature determined by one's LIFE's nonmaterial structure — one's temperament and character — urges and drives one to make effort and strive whether one wishes to or not. My own state has always been like this: it is not that I want to make effort and strive, but that my character and the events and connections occurring around me compel me to do so. This is what is called "once in the rivers and lakes, one cannot be one's own master."
>
> Every person longs for a beautiful future, longs to live in Heaven. So who, in the end, can realize their most beautiful ideals?
>
> If everything is destined, what use is personal longing?
>
> This brings us to certain programs in the cosmic script. The cosmic script contains both fixed elements and variables. The script's plot is a fixed element; who will play the characters in the script is a variable. Those who play their roles well can go on to play roles ever more in alignment with their own free will — for example, playing in Heaven. Those who play poorly will play roles ever more at odds with their own free will — for example, playing in Hell.
>
> So who goes to Heaven?
>
> Those who, following the script's established plot, play their role well without clinging to self — they go to Heaven. Those who conflict with the arranged plot — that is, those whose sense of self is strong and who resist playing their role — go to Hell.
>
> From this we discover a great mystery: whether one goes to Heaven or to Hell has nothing to do with the role one plays, and everything to do with whether one plays the role well.
>
> Take the fascist leader Hitler. From a human perspective, he should go to Hell. But from the perspective of the cosmic script's plot, if he performed his role very well, he would actually go to Heaven.
>
> Whether you play an emperor or a thief, as long as you play the role well, you are rewarded with Heaven. If you play it poorly, you are punished with Hell.
>
> So how can one prove whether one's performance is good or poor?
>
> There is a very simple method of verification: no matter what role you are playing — a role of suffering and hardship, or a role of enjoyment and comfort; a role of commanding the storm, or a role of quiet ordinariness — if at any moment you can be calm and at peace, joyful and happy, free of hesitation, worry, pain, anxiety, dread, and fear, full of gratitude — then you are playing the role well. Otherwise, you are not.
>
> Those who have grasped the essence of the Buddha's teaching understand that the clinging to self is the greatest obstacle to reaching the Pure Land. The clinging to self is caused by one's own free will being too strong — always wanting things to proceed according to one's own thoughts and wishes, always finding reasons to justify oneself, always insisting on one's own views, always believing one is right. Such people cannot play the roles the script has arranged for them well. Such people are people of suffering, people who will not receive the reward of the Dao of Heaven.
>
> Acting in accordance with what comes, following conditions, acting according to one's inner nature, responding to circumstances — this is the supreme method for playing one's role well.
>

## III. The Specific Manifestations of Free Will: Thinking, Choosing, Resisting

Free will is not abstract — it manifests concretely in every act of thinking, weighing, and choosing in daily life.

(*Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom · How to Use Free Will*, complete original text):

> Human beings have free will. When the script's plot and characters are determined, how to perform the role is determined by a person's free will.
>
> A human being cannot achieve full alignment with the Dao. The places where one is not aligned with the Dao are precisely where one has used one's own free will.
>
> Humans think, weigh pros and cons, use schemes, and seek good fortune and avoid misfortune — all of these are expressions of free will.
>
> Humans have worry, pain, anxiety, sorrow, and fear — these are feelings of consciousness, which also proves that humans have free will. When free will conflicts with the script's plot and character arrangements, these feelings are born.
>
> When we go shopping, the store is full of goods, and we browse and select — this selection process is the moment of free will's fullest expression. Television has aired matchmaking programs — the matchmaking process is the process of free will selecting.
>
> If a human being had no free will, they would be nothing but a biological robot — incapable of making a selection.
>
> Those living in the Home should have this kind of experience and feeling: when you are in a one-on-one state, you inwardly resist the Guide's requirement not to form one-on-one relationships — you may even feel you are right and the Guide is wrong. But you dare not openly resist. On one hand you want to follow the Guide's guidance; on the other hand you cannot let go. This internal confusion and pain — this is the contradiction between free will and the script's arrangement.
>
> The Buddha's teaching is "no self." A person with "no self" has no free will. The Buddha's state is "no self" — if there is "a self," it is not the Buddha.
>
> The requirement for aligning with the Dao and being in accord with Heaven and humanity is: make one's free will consistent with the Dao's program and the laws and rules of Heaven — not in opposition to them. In other words, allow oneself, wholeheartedly and joyfully, to align with the script's plot and character arrangements — playing every role to the full, doing every task to the full, step by step in harmony, heart and mind in resonance.
>
> Attachment is the extreme expression of free will: the script says go east, you insist on going west; the script says play, you insist on working; the script says move, you insist on staying still; the script says take it off, you insist on keeping it on. When this happens, conflict intensifies, and hardship and disaster become frequent.
>
> Observe those around you, historical figures, and characters in television dramas — you will certainly find that those who are strongly attached live in difficulty, their lives go poorly, and their endings are bad.
>
> This is why the Buddha taught us to "eliminate the clinging to self."
>
> Every person living in the Home can discover this mystery from their own personal experience: when you uphold Lifechanyuan's values and walk strictly along the Guide's roadmap, you experience happiness, joy, freedom, and bliss. When you violate Lifechanyuan's values and deviate from the Guide's roadmap, you feel suppressed, depressed, and uncomfortable — unable to enjoy happiness or freedom. All of this is the confusion caused by wanting to deploy one's own free will.
>
> "The sage embraces the One and is a model for all under Heaven." Why "embrace the One"?
>
> Because embracing the One brings ease, simplicity, freedom, and joy. If one does not embrace the One, one not only meets obstacles at every step but will surely encounter disaster.
>
> In the entire universe, embrace the One with the Greatest Creator. In a nation, embrace the One with the head of government. In a group, embrace the One with the highest authority. If one insists on opposing, it is nothing but an egg crashing against a stone — ending with one's own head split and bleeding.
>
> To "embrace the One" requires eliminating the clinging to self. Eliminating the clinging to self requires maximally suppressing one's free will that conflicts with the script's plot.
>
> Free will is bestowed by the Greatest Creator. Everything bestowed by the Greatest Creator should be used; not using it is wrong. But how should it be used?
>
> The cosmic grand script is vast and complex. Only those who have opened the dharma eye and Buddha eye can glimpse some of its mysteries — by looking across the long river of history, across centuries and millennia, from tiny details, and from the characteristics and principles of the Dao's operation. Most people see only as far as a mouse's eye, and cannot understand at all what this grand script is expressing or how it is unfolding. The one and only broad road is: walk the Way of the Greatest Creator; listen to and obey the teachings and guidance of divine beings, Buddhas, Celestial Beings, and Sages.
>
> To try to understand the truth of the universe by relying on our own little bit of wisdom is like climbing a tree to catch a fish. To try to find a shortcut to Heaven by relying on our own little bit of free will is like a fool dreaming.
>
