# Chan · The Unwritten Scripture · Divine Revelation

**Chan** (禅, Zen) is the supreme method of awakening to the Tao; **the Unwritten Scripture** (无字天书) is the totality of natural phenomena—the wordless, symbolless book written by the cosmos itself; **Divine Revelation** (天启, Tianqi) is the language the Greatest Creator uses to communicate with humanity through the ten thousand things and phenomena. The three are facets of the same mystery: Tianqi is the content, the Unwritten Scripture is the medium, and Chan is the method for reading it.

---

## I. Tianqi: The Language of the Greatest Creator

The Greatest Creator has never held a direct conversation with humanity in any human language—but this does not mean He is distant. The Greatest Creator communicates with us ceaselessly, using His own language: the natural world itself.

> "All things in nature are the language of the Greatest Creator; all phenomena in nature are His words. The sun, moon, and stars; rivers, lakes, and seas; wind, cloud, rain, and snow; all creatures and plants; mountains and earth—these are His vocabulary. The movements of celestial bodies, the surging of rivers, the roaring of oceans, the shifting of seasons, the rise and fall of living beings—these are His sentences."  
> *(Xuefeng, transmitting from the Greatest Creator and Buddha: "Preface to the Tianqi Chapter")*

"Everything—every single thing—that humanity perceives through eyes, ears, nose, tongue, and body is the language and words of the Greatest Creator."

This means: nothing in the universe is without meaning. Every falling leaf, every illness, every chance encounter is the Greatest Creator speaking. This is **Tianqi**—Divine Revelation—ever present, yet rarely understood.

---

## II. The Unwritten Scripture: The Deepest of Three Books

There are three kinds of books:

1. **Written books**—newspapers, journals, texts: transmit information through words;
2. **Symbol books**—sculpture, painting, music: transmit information through symbols;
3. **The Unwritten Scripture**—the ten thousand things and phenomena of nature: no words, no symbols.

Of these three, the Unwritten Scripture is the most profound; written books are the most superficial. People of lower spirituality can only read written books; those of higher spirituality can read the Unwritten Scripture. All celestial beings, Buddhas, and sages read the Unwritten Scripture.

The Sixth Patriarch of Chan, Huineng, was illiterate—yet he became one of the founding masters of the Chan lineage, precisely because he could read the Unwritten Scripture. A person who wishes to become a great master, a celestial being, or a Buddha must learn to read the Unwritten Scripture.

**Reading people, reading objects, reading phenomena—this is reading the Unwritten Scripture.**

"The ten thousand things and phenomena are the words of the Greatest Creator; changing phenomena are the Greatest Creator speaking—this is Chan, this is the Unwritten Scripture. To understand the Unwritten Scripture is to be in dialogue with the Greatest Creator."

---

## III. Chan: The Supreme Gateway to Awakening

Chan is the supreme method of awakening to the Tao. Every other spiritual path can be expressed in words; Chan alone can only be transmitted through direct heart-to-heart perception—"no reliance on written words, transmission outside the scriptures."

### The Origin of Chan

At the assembly on Vulture Peak, Shakyamuni Buddha held up a flower before the congregation. All disciples were bewildered—except Mahakashyapa, who understood at once and smiled with complete recognition. That smile conveyed that he had grasped everything the Buddha meant—not through the ears, but through the heart. This is the essence of Chan.

The Buddha transmitted this wordless, mind-to-mind method to Mahakashyapa. It passed through twenty-eight Indian patriarchs to Bodhidharma, who brought it to China. There it was handed down through Huike, Sengcan, Daoxin, Hongren, and finally to the Sixth Patriarch Huineng. After Huineng, a dispute over the robe caused Chan to split into Northern and Southern schools; the true transmission was lost. Countless books on Chan have since been written, but they have mostly grasped only the surface.

*Xuefeng, transmitting from the Greatest Creator and Buddha, has been entrusted with restoring and proclaiming the true essence of Chan for this era.*

### Three Dimensions of Chan

| Dimension | Nature | How Accessed |
|-----------|--------|--------------|
| **Chan itself** | The awakening state—wordless, beyond analysis | Heart-to-heart transmission, direct experience |
| **Chan principle (禅理)** | Rational truths of the Tao | Logic, experience, observation |
| **Chan subtlety (禅机)** | Intuitive cosmic signals, subtle potential | Spiritual sensing (灵觉) alone |

Chan principle: when hungry, eat; when thirsty, drink; water flows downward—these are Chan principles. All scientific discoveries are Chan principles.

Chan subtlety: love at first sight; the sudden stirring of emotion before a landscape. These cannot be rationally analyzed; they can only be sensed by spiritual perception. If Chan principle is kinetic energy, Chan subtlety is a form of latent potential—it exists, but cannot be touched by the five ordinary senses.

## IV. Two Keys to Reading Tianqi: Spiritual Sensing and Wisdom

Understanding Tianqi requires both faculties in combination:

| Faculty | Function | Without It |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| **Spiritual sensing (灵觉)** | Perceiving the presence and language of the Greatest Creator | Knowing *that*, not knowing *why* |
| **Wisdom (智慧)** | Understanding what the Greatest Creator is saying | Knowing *why*, not knowing *that* |

- Religious practitioners generally have spiritual sensing but lack wisdom;
- Scientists generally have wisdom but lack spiritual sensing;
- Ordinary people largely lack both;
- Only rare sages of humanity have possessed both—enabling them to truly understand Tianqi.

---

## V. All Phenomena Give Birth to the Mind; the Mind Transcends All Phenomena

Reading the Unwritten Scripture is the core practice of cultivation:

**Stage One: All phenomena give birth to the mind.**  
By carefully observing the ten thousand phenomena—stars and seasons, the rise and fall of civilizations, the behavior of people—the heart-mind absorbs the patterns of the Tao. "When our heart-mind embraces the ten thousand phenomena of the cosmos, our heart-mind acquires Ling (spiritual resonance)." This is how Laozi arrived at the profound insights of the *Tao Te Ching*.

**Stage Two: The mind transcends all phenomena.**  
Once the mind has acquired Ling, it must transcend its dependence on phenomena and enter successively higher states of thinking—Taiji thinking → Non-Form thinking → Hundun thinking—corresponding to the Thousand-Year World → the Ten-Thousand-Year World → the Elysium World.

All phenomena are illusory manifestations of the Noumenon—impermanent and unreal. "Life is like a dream; why take it too seriously?" Only when the mind transcends phenomena does it enter the true substance of LIFE, and in doing so, attain Buddhahood.

---

## VI. Tianqi in Daily Life: Examples

Tianqi is everywhere—in the most ordinary moments:

- Flour left in a damp place turns to hard clumps → **Tianqi**: *Thoughts also need ventilation; otherwise a person calcifies into rigidity.*
- Eggplants deteriorate whether frozen or heated → **Tianqi**: *Some people can be neither loved nor hated—measure matters above all.*
- A neighbor plows snow without asking for payment; you offer him your home → **Tianqi**: *Accumulate merit in advance, and doors open naturally.*
- Lightning splits the sky before a storm → **Tianqi**: *The universe is warning humanity of imminent change.*

"To be in dialogue with the Greatest Creator, you need no grand posture. In daily life you can hear His words."

---

## VII. Responding to Tianqi

Understanding Tianqi is only the first step; the second is **responding**—adjusting one's life direction accordingly.

"Do not wait until the torrential rain falls to build your ark; do not wait until the Titanic strikes the iceberg to look for a life jacket… The key to opening the gates of Heaven lies in your own hand. Simply sense quietly, and listen to the guidance of the spirit within."

Cultivating the capacity to receive Tianqi is the same as cultivating spiritual sensing—which is the same as purifying the heart-mind. When the Five Poisons (greed, anger, delusion, arrogance, doubt) are removed and the heart is clear, Tianqi becomes transparent. "When the flower blooms, butterflies come naturally; when the heart is pure, celestial beings arrive."
