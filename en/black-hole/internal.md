# Black Hole (Internal Reference)

## I. Definition and Nature

"I. 'Black holes' are a type of highly energetic matter. The reason they appear 'formless' accords with the energy-form formula I have identified: the greater the energy, the less the form; the lesser the energy, the more the form. I believe scientists will ultimately confirm this."
*(Xuefeng, Awakening Series — The Mysteries of the Black Hole)*

"II. 'Black holes' are 'gods' created by the Greatest Creator. They possess consciousness. Each black hole is like a traffic officer maintaining cosmic order, and the vast universe is regulated and adjusted by these 'black hole officers.' Without the action of these 'black hole officers,' the Earth would inevitably collide with the Sun. We know that celestial bodies always exert a gravitational pull, 'wanting' to draw each other into their embrace — but the repulsive force of black holes 'cruelly' severs these 'yearning threads,' keeping celestial bodies at their relative distances, not allowing them to cross the line."
*(Xuefeng, Awakening Series — The Mysteries of the Black Hole)*

## II. Black Holes Are Not Dark — Luminous Inside

"III. In ordinary matter, energy is concentrated at the core. Black holes are the opposite: their energy is concentrated at the perimeter. Viewed from outside, nothing can be seen — only darkness. But inside, it is not dark at all; it is a world of brilliant radiance. This is like the special glass on some executive office windows: nothing can be seen from outside, but inside there is ample light and one can clearly observe others at work."
*(Xuefeng, Awakening Series — The Mysteries of the Black Hole)*

"In *Journey to the West*, the scene where Sun Wukong draws a circle on the ground to protect Tang Monk, Sandy, and Pigsy offers a useful analogy. The White Bone Demon, disguised as a young woman, cannot enter because the circle's energy exceeds her own. The structure of a black hole is exactly like that circle and everything within it."
*(Xuefeng, Awakening Series — The Mysteries of the Black Hole)*

"IV. From III, the conclusion follows: black holes are not black."
*(Xuefeng, Awakening Series — The Mysteries of the Black Hole)*

## III. Dynamic Equilibrium — Absorbing Gravity, Emitting Repulsion

"V. Black holes maintain dynamic equilibrium. Many scientists believe a black hole's gravity is so immense that it swallows all energy that comes near, 'only eating, never expelling.' This is not so. For anything to exist, it must accord with dynamic equilibrium — there must be inflow and outflow, reception and release. There is nothing in the universe that only receives and never gives; if there were, the universe would not look as it does."
*(Xuefeng, Awakening Series — The Mysteries of the Black Hole)*

"VI. Black holes 'eat' gravity and 'expel' repulsive force."
*(Xuefeng, Awakening Series — The Mysteries of the Black Hole)*

"VII. Some may worry: since black holes are 'traffic officers,' might they 'doze off' or make errors of judgment, causing 'cosmic traffic chaos'? Rest assured — black hole officers neither doze off nor give wrong directions, because they are themselves subject to the command and constraint of even greater conscious energies. The greater the energy, the higher the wisdom of a life-form, and the more strictly it adheres to the Way."
*(Xuefeng, Awakening Series — The Mysteries of the Black Hole)*
