# Embrace the One: Walk One Path for a Lifetime — This Is the Highest Cultivation Secret

## 1) The one-sentence version

**Embrace the One means holding fast to the Way of the Greatest Creator — no matter how things shift, always bite down on that One, unshaken and unmoved.**

Laozi wrote in the *Tao Te Ching*:

> Heaven attained the One and became clear. Earth attained the One and became still. Spirit attained the One and became efficacious. The valley attained the One and became full. The ten thousand things attained the One and came to life. Therefore the sage embraces the One and becomes the model for all under heaven.

These words have circulated for two thousand years. In Lifechanyuan, they receive their first clear and unambiguous specific meaning: that "One" is not metaphysics, not a number — it is **the Way of the Greatest Creator**.

---

## 2) Most people's frustration comes from "not embracing the One"

Does any of this feel familiar?

- Today this path seems right; tomorrow that one looks better
- Today you're convinced by one teaching; tomorrow someone else talks you out of it
- Every few years you rebuild your life plan from scratch — but you haven't moved
- Your heart holds several goals at once, yet none of them really advances

This is not laziness. This is not stupidity. This is **losing the One** — pursuing too many things simultaneously, energy scattered, nothing accomplished.

Entry 706 states it plainly:

> All of humanity has only one faith, one command; a person can have only one goal in life, one wish, one path of cultivation, one road to walk. Otherwise: difficulty becoming clear, difficulty becoming still, difficulty becoming efficacious, difficulty becoming full. Multiplicity creates disorder; mixture creates complexity.

"Multiplicity creates disorder; mixture creates complexity" — six words that precisely diagnose most of life's confusion.

---

## 3) "The One" doesn't mean becoming a robot

Some people hear "walk one path for a lifetime" and feel constrained. But Embrace the One is not about narrowing your life — it is about finding the root.

Think of a tree. One root system, one main trunk, but branches spreading in every direction and ten thousand flowers opening. The branches are free precisely because the root holds.

Guide Xuefeng uses the image of **one root, ten thousand branches**: one steady main trunk, with ten thousand branches growing from it in all directions, each one more flourishing than the last — but the premise for all of it is that the one root never wavers.

Embrace the One means finding that root and holding it.

## 4) How do Chanyuan Celestials "embrace the One"? Three things

In the daily life of Chanyuan Celestials, Embrace the One becomes three concrete things (*One Root, Ten Thousand Branches*):

**1. Revere only one Master**

> Chanyuan Celestials revere only one Master: the Greatest Creator, possessing the eight characteristics of uniqueness, formlessness, neutrality, unknowability, impartiality, benevolence, supernal power, and wisdom. Other gods, Buddhas, immortals (bodhisattvas, angels), emperors, and sages are all respected — but not revered as master.

This is not about rejecting others — it is about choosing the root. Energy is limited; you can only revere one. This is Embrace the One, not the "pray to any passing deity" approach.

**2. Follow only one government's overall arrangements**

> Chanyuan Celestials follow only one government's overall arrangements: the World Government.

While the world remains a patchwork of competing nations each claiming its own authority, those who embrace the One have already recognized only one source of order — the World Government, all under heaven as one family.

**3. Follow only one Guide**

> Chanyuan Celestials follow only one guide: Xuefeng, the founder of Lifechanyuan.

Once you've found your pathfinder, you walk with them — not swayed by others' three words and two sentences. The *Bible* says "No one can serve two masters" — same principle.

## 5) A striking equation: 1 + 1 = 1

We all learned that 1 + 1 = 2 — that's **quantity** increasing.

But Entry 311 says:

> 1 + 1 = 2 is an increase in quantity. 1 + 1 = 1 is an increase in energy. The sage embraces the One and becomes the model for all under heaven.

When two people both "embrace the One," both holding fast to the same One (the Way of the Greatest Creator), they are not two independent units being added — they **merge** into the same frequency. What forms is an increase in energy, not an accumulation of numbers.

This is the remarkable thing that happens in Lifechanyuan:

> Those whose intentions are upright find resonance everywhere in Lifechanyuan, harmony everywhere, feeling at ease throughout — 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 + … = 1

A hundred Chanyuan Celestials all embracing the One — added together, not a hundred, but the greater One: resonance, harmony, union without seam. This is the community's true power.

## 6) A practical daily method: 10 − 9 = Tao

Entry 458 gives a way of understanding Embrace the One that is immediately usable in everyday life:

> 10 − 9 = "Tao." For a person, the meaning of 10 − 9 is: set aside and clear away the myriad tangled and complex affairs of life, concentrating wholeheartedly on one thing. As long as you take that one thing deep, thorough, complete, and through to the end, you will gain from it everything you need.

Out of 10 things, throw away 9, keep 1. That is the Tao. That is the One.

It is not about doing less — it is about cutting away what scatters your energy, **pouring your whole force into that one thing**.

Water wears through stone; rope saws through wood; the melon ripens when the time comes; the river finds the sea when the conditions are right — all these results come from **sustained focus at one point**. This is the most direct daily practice of Embrace the One.

## 7) What does losing the One look like?

Entry 706 says:

> Only with "Embrace the One" is there harmony, peace, auspiciousness, timely rains, and a life of joy, happiness, freedom, and bliss for all.

The reverse: what happens without Embrace the One?

- Faith in disorder: learning this today, that tomorrow, the soul with nowhere to settle
- Direction wavering: switching one road for another, confusion deepening
- Community fracturing: everyone with their own opinion, resonance impossible
- LIFE scattered: energy leaking into a thousand unfinished things, none taken deep

Guide Xuefeng says: once you deviate from the Guide's leading, you will quickly become "lost, anxious, sorrowful, worried, confused, wandering, depressed, fearful, tense." Not a curse — a pattern. Lose the One, lose the root, and everything comes apart.

## 8) Embrace the One is the key to becoming a celestial being

Entry 456 says:

> Guarding the Tao means … no matter how the times change, no matter what great events occur around you, no matter whether you are in favorable or adverse circumstances, no matter how great the temptations — steadfastly holding faith and conviction: "Tempered by a thousand blows, still tenacious; let the winds come from east, west, south, and north." "Sit steady in the fishing boat while waves rise all around." Unshaken and unmoved.

*Unshaken and unmoved* — these four words are the most beautiful description of Embrace the One.

However the outside changes, that One inside does not. However strong the wind, the boat does not rock. This is not numbness — it is roots sunk deep.

Entry 462 says it even more simply:

> A person who can reach the state of always being joyful, cheerful, and free — has become a celestial being.

Always joyful, cheerful, and free — why is it possible? Because that One is there, not pulled by external circumstances, not carried away by temptations. Naturally joyful. Naturally free.

## 10) A final word: Embrace the One is holding that One and never letting go

Guide Xuefeng says:

> The mystery lies in "the One." The sage embraces the One and becomes the model for all under heaven. Lifechanyuan's "One" is clear and unambiguous — it is the Way of the Greatest Creator. Embrace this One, and no matter how the winds rage or the seas surge, you can "sit steady in the fishing boat while waves rise all around," can "hold firm to the green mountain and let the winds come from every direction."

Embrace the One is not a one-time flash of insight, not something you figure out one day and then it is done. It is a choice made every day:

- Today — am I still holding that One?
- This thing I'm considering — does it move me closer to that One, or pull me away from it?
- Facing this temptation, this pressure, this doubt — am I still biting down on that One?

Every time you answer yes, you are in cultivation.

That is Embrace the One. That is one of Lifechanyuan's highest cultivation secrets.
