# Elementary Refinement · Friendly Introduction

> A plain-language guide to the core ideas of Elementary Refinement and how they apply to everyday life.

---

## What Is Elementary Refinement?

In simple terms, Elementary Refinement is about **learning to see through life clearly**.

Lifechanyuan divides the refinement path into three stages: Elementary refines your *perception*, Intermediate refines your *heart*, and Advanced refines your *nature*. "Refining perception" means working to see what life actually is — beneath the illusions, the comparisons, the anxieties — so that you gradually move from the confused existence of an average person to the purposeful, clear life of a truly wise one.

The goal? **Individual health, abundance, and freedom.**

## The Twenty-Three Guidelines, Made Simple

### Group One: Let Go of the Things That Exhaust You (Guidelines 1–9)

These nine guidelines share one theme: **stop clinging to things that cause suffering**.

- **Natural non-action**: Whether you're a president or a janitor, a billionaire or a farmer — accept it, and stay at peace. Don't force anything. Follow the flow.
- **No scheming**: A clear mind, open as a sunny day, is more powerful than any scheme. All schemes eventually trap their designer.
- **No promises**: Life is unpredictable. Promises you can't keep become yokes around your neck. It's better not to make them.
- **No dependence on power**: Power is like a burning log — it will turn to ash. Leaning on it means losing yourself when it falls.
- **No receiving of favors**: A received favor is a debt, like a mountain pressing on your heart. To live freely, try not to owe.
- **No career ambition**: Seeking the Tao is the only real ambition. Chasing fame, glory, or family honor is just worldly noise.
- **No worry about tomorrow**: Live in this moment only. Tomorrow never ends — worry about it and you'll never have peace.
- **No thought of success or failure**: True success is attaining the Tao. All other measures are just conventions. Act naturally and let go of results.
- **No comparison with others**: You cannot walk anyone else's path. Their success is their story. Ask yourself instead: *Who am I?* That question clears the mist.

### Group Two: Simplify Your Daily Life (Guidelines 10–16)

These seven guidelines are about **how to live day to day**:

- **Seek the mild**: Mild food, mild friendships, mild habits. Less intensity means fewer desires; fewer desires means less suffering.
- **No idle busyness**: Move like the sun and moon — steady and natural. Neither rush nor drift aimlessly.
- **No concern for reputation after death**: Let your posthumous reputation take care of itself. Caring too much about it prevents you from living freely now.
- **No cleverness**: Great wisdom looks ordinary. Cleverness is a trick; it backfires. Real intelligence leaves no trace.
- **Speak little**: Most words are wasted — too deep for some, too shallow for others, unnecessary for the rest. Speaking less means fewer disputes, fewer troubles.
- **No fear of death**: Death is inevitable. Accepting that frees you. When you have no fear of death, you are closer to being truly alive.
- **Be like stone and wood**: Rocks and trees are not moved by storms, slander, or noise. Without stillness, there is no understanding of the Tao.

### Group Three: Become More Like Nature (Guidelines 17–23)

These seven describe **the inner states to cultivate**:

- **Like clouds**: Drifting freely, unattached to any place or form. Present when needed; gone when not.
- **Like ice**: Steady and enduring, unswayed by the push and pull of emotion.
- **Like water**: The highest good is like water. Yielding, yet capable of overcoming anything. Nourishing everything while demanding nothing.
- **No rivals, no enemies**: Don't contend with heaven, earth, or people. When wronged, let karma handle it — the Tao is just and does not shortchange anyone.
- **Do not display ability**: Great ability is like a rare gem — keep it hidden. The moment you show it off, you become a servant to others' expectations.
- **Cherish the minute, the weak, the small**: Great trees begin as tiny sprouts. Fortune and disaster begin as a single thought. Pay attention to the small things; that's where the Tao hides.
- **Give your whole heart**: This is the culmination of all 22 other guidelines.

## The Keystone: Give Your Whole Heart

The twenty-third guideline is the heart of Elementary Refinement:

> *Cultivate the celestial being by first cultivating the person. Cultivate the person through giving one's whole heart. Giving your whole heart resolves the heart. Resolving the heart makes you a celestial being.*

"Giving your whole heart" (*jìnxīn*) doesn't mean perfection. It means giving your best effort:

1. **Honor your parents** — truly and practically, not just in principle
2. **Raise your children** well — put real effort into their growth
3. **Be genuine with everyone** — spouse, siblings, friends, neighbors, even animals and plants
4. **Be honest with yourself** — don't suppress your desires; understand them until they naturally dissolve

When you've done all of this, when nothing feels unfinished or regretted, the heart is *resolved* (*liǎo xīn*) — free from entanglements. That is when you're ready for the next stage.

## Elementary Refinement vs. Elementary Cultivation

A common question: How does this differ from Elementary *Cultivation* (*xiūxíng*)?

| | Elementary Refinement | Elementary Cultivation |
|--|----------------------|----------------------|
| Focus | Inner: clear *perception* | Outer: regulated *behavior* |
| Guidelines | 23 mental principles | 51 behavioral rules |
| Goal | Move from ordinary → wise person | Become a qualified, decent person |

Both matter and work together. Cultivation shapes outward behavior; Refinement trains inward understanding.
