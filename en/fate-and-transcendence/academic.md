# Fate and Transcendence · Academic Version

## Abstract

In Lifechanyuan thought, "fate and transcendence" forms a core dialectical proposition. Xuefeng acknowledges fatalism in a qualified sense: for 98% of ordinary people, the script of life is already written and all things move according to the Greatest Creator's program, inescapably; yet for those Chanyuan Celestials who reach a sufficient level of cultivation, transcendence is possible through knowledge of life's mysteries, abnormal thinking, and consciousness transformation. This position differs both from absolute determinism (fate is entirely fixed) and from pure voluntarism (fate is entirely self-determined); instead, it preserves within the framework of the Greatest Creator's program a narrow "exception channel" accessible to a rare few awakened individuals.

## I. Conceptual Analysis

### 1.1 The Three-Tier Structure of Fate

Xuefeng's conception of fate operates across three tiers:

**Cosmic tier**: The entire universe operates according to "the Tao" — the program — which no being can resist. The Earth orbiting the Sun and the Moon orbiting the Earth are inescapable cosmic fate.

**Species tier**: Every species has a fate it cannot transcend — plants follow seasonal cycles; animals follow the food chain; a pig's fate is the slaughterhouse.

**Individual tier**: Every person's life — birth, illness, wealth, death, and the timing of each — is already written into the "script." The subjective sense of making choices is itself part of the performance.

These three tiers form a complete mapping of the program designed by the Greatest Creator, and are integral to Lifechanyuan's cosmology (see [Life Script](/en/life-script/), [Cosmic Script](/en/cosmic-script/)).

### 1.2 The Structural Exception: 98%/2%

Xuefeng does not arrive at absolute fatalism. He explicitly states that every rule has exceptions: for 1–2% of all beings, the Greatest Creator's program "opens a gap," allowing certain individuals to walk outside the preset trajectory. The conditions for this exception are:

1. **Knowledge** — a thorough understanding of life, time, space, and the nature of consciousness;
2. **Action** — active engagement with the process, finding the passage into a broader domain;
3. **Method** — access to the specific methods that Lifechanyuan's guide holds for transcending fate and time-space.

This framework resonates structurally with the "narrow gate" in Christianity, the Buddha's teaching of liberation from the wheel of rebirth, Lao Tzu's path to "lasting life and enduring vision," and the Sufi concept of *fana* (annihilation of the self-determined ego). However, all of these are integrated within Lifechanyuan's own LIFE-hierarchy framework.

## II. Comparative Positions on Fate

### 2.1 Distinction from Traditional Fatalism

Traditional fatalism is typically **passive**: since everything is decreed, there is no point in effort. Xuefeng explicitly rejects this attitude: "Let fate arrange everything — then what are we cultivating ourselves for? For nothing at all!" 

Lifechanyuan's position is an **active, dialectical fatalism**: first acknowledge fate (do not fight the Greatest Creator's program), then use that acknowledgment as the foundation from which to seek transcendence. "The path guided by the Lifechanyuan guide is one of counter-fate — guiding people first to acknowledge fate, to recognize fate, and then to transcend fate."

### 2.2 Distinction from Pure Voluntarism

Pure voluntarism holds that individuals freely determine their own destinies. Lifechanyuan's position is that for the vast majority, what feels like free choice is still an enactment of the script — "although each person subjectively senses that they are making choices, the actual situation is that all their words and actions have already been arranged in advance."

True freedom is only available to the rare few who break through the program.

## III. Mechanisms of Transcendence

### 3.1 Consciousness Transformation

"When consciousness does not change, the trajectory of life does not change, fate does not change... But if consciousness changes, the trajectory changes, fate changes."

Consciousness is identified as the root cause of fate. Shifting consciousness — particularly by establishing the intention to walk the path of the Greatest Creator and pursue joy, happiness, freedom, and bliss — can alter one's trajectory at its source, preventing bitter outcomes that would otherwise inevitably ripen.

### 3.2 Abnormal Thinking

Abnormal thinking (see [Abnormal Thinking](/en/abnormal-thinking/)) is the essential cognitive instrument for exiting the fate-program: "One must engage in abnormal thinking, otherwise fate cannot be escaped... one absolutely cannot resonate on the same frequency as the thinking and consciousness of the vast majority." This parallels structurally with Lao Tzu's *wei wu wei* (acting through non-action), Kierkegaard's "leap of faith," and Osho's teaching that the invisible path is the only true path.

### 3.3 The Lifechanyuan Methods

Xuefeng claims to possess "methods for transcending fate, for transcending time and space," without fully disclosing their content in public writings. These methods are treated as the inner core of Lifechanyuan cultivation, with access contingent upon genuinely entering the Chanyuan Celestial community and walking the path of the Greatest Creator.

## V. Position in the Lifechanyuan Cultivation System

Fate and transcendence form the motivational foundation of Lifechanyuan's cultivation framework: precisely because most people are bound by fate while the cultivating few have the opportunity to break free, cultivation is meaningful. The concept is systematically linked to [Awakening](/en/awakening/), [Free Will](/en/free-will/), [Abnormal Thinking](/en/abnormal-thinking/), and [Consciousness](/en/consciousness/).
