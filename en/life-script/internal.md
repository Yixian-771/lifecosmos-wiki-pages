## II. The Complete Exposition of the Script of Human Life: *Screenwriter, Director, Actor* (Full Text)

(*Xuefeng Corpus · X01 Warning · Screenwriter, Director, Actor*, complete original text):

> In the prime of youth, one feels the world can be arranged according to one's own wishes. After passing through vicissitudes and tasting bitterness, weary in body and heart, when reaching the age of "knowing Heaven's mandate" and "having ears that obey," one finally realizes it is Heaven and Earth that arrange oneself.
>
> "Everything is fated; not half a point is up to the person" — this is the lament of many people. They tried, struggled, fought, and gave their all, but little came of their own will. At such moments, only "gazing at the vastness of Heaven and Earth, alone with tears flowing down" remains.
>
> This makes me think of the relationship between screenwriters, directors, and actors. The screenwriter sits at a desk, smoking, constructing and arranging characters, locations, times, and plot elements. The appearance, personality, encounters, and fate of all characters in the story are under the screenwriter's careful arrangement. The director, following the screenwriter's arrangement, gathers the characters of the story — the actors — and directs them in how to best portray the story's people. The actor can only perform according to the director's requirements, enacting the story's plot. Let alone controlling one's own fate — even one's lines are not freely chosen. When to be born, when to die — the screenwriter arranged it long ago. The actor has no say; they cannot even choose to live a few extra hours.
>
> From this we learn: a character's fate is arranged by the screenwriter. The director has the right to make appropriate adjustments, even large ones, but the fundamental fate cannot be adjusted by the director — the right to adjust fate lies with the screenwriter.
>
> Though the actor is alive, everything about them has already been "set in stone." An actor is, in effect, a living puppet.
>
> Then — are we, ordinary people of the human world, not all actors?
>
> Are kings, presidents, chairmen, religious masters, CEOs, general managers, and so forth not all directors, one by one?
>
> Where is the screenwriter who arranges our fate?
>
> Undoubtedly, Jin Yong is a screenwriter. Whether it is the fate of characters in *Demi-Gods and Semi-Devils* or *The Heaven Sword and Dragon Saber*, it was all arranged by Jin Yong.
>
> Wait — can Jin Yong truly arrange characters' fates as he pleases?
>
> He cannot!
>
> Setting aside historical threads, anyone who arranges characters' fates in a story must follow many rules — at minimum, things must be arranged plausibly. Once a character's personality is set, the screenwriter is compelled to respect that character's nature and follow the story's development. The screenwriter will find that making someone fall in love with another, or making one person hate another, is not easy. Making someone love desperately or hate bitterly is no easy feat. Making someone live or die is absolutely impossible to do as one pleases — if the timing of birth or death is too early or too late, it won't work; it must be exactly right. Otherwise, the entire story cannot be arranged.
>
> Once arrangement begins, in most cases the screenwriter is also not in control of themselves.
>
> The screenwriter cannot arbitrarily make people laugh, or arbitrarily make people cry; cannot arbitrarily send people to fight others, or arbitrarily fabricate false cases.
>
> This means: behind the screenwriter, there is another screenwriter.
>
> There is one ultimate screenwriter in the universe, and that screenwriter is the Greatest Creator. The story unfolds according to the will of the Greatest Creator — the Dao.
>
> In the story arranged by the Greatest Creator, we are all actors. Jesus, Shakyamuni, Muhammad, Hundun Yuanchu, and others are all directors — and of course there are many, many small directors too.
>
> Since we are actors, the question we face is: which role to play?
>
> To play Yang Guo in Jin Yong's novels, one arm cannot be kept. To play Xiaolongnü, one must live in the ancient tomb for a period. To play Zhang Wuji, one must accept the strike of the Xuanming Palm.
>
> Perhaps someone thinks: I will play no role at all; I will design my own fate according to my own will.
>
> The wish is good, but unfortunately it is impossible. If it were possible, Laozi's saying "Heaven's net is vast; though with wide meshes, nothing slips through" would be nonsense. The regularities discovered by scientists would no longer be regularities. "Once in the rivers and lakes, one cannot help oneself" would be absurd.
>
> One must be an actor — and moreover, one must follow the director's arrangement.
>
> The great director directs according to the requirements of the ultimate screenwriter, the Greatest Creator.
>
> There are many kinds of scripts; different directors direct different content. Hitler, Tojo Hideki, Stalin, Churchill, Roosevelt, Chiang Kai-shek, Mao Zedong, and others during World War II are also directors.
>
> Li Hongzhi, undoubtedly, is also a director.
>
> Each director directs a different script. Each person has more or less the right to choose which role to play.
>
> When we recognize a director, that director will then direct us according to the script's requirements.
>
> So choosing a director directly relates to our fate.
>
> Lifechanyuan's Hundun Yuanchu directs according to the script of the Thousand-Year World, Ten-Thousand-Year World, and the Celestial Islands Continent of the Elysium World. The actors he needs are all those who hope to become celestial beings — who wish to eternally enjoy the joyful, happy, free, and blissful life of Heaven.
>
> To be a good actor, one must perform according to the director's requirements. If one does not perform as the director requires, the director will certainly choose someone else. This is the actor's freedom, and the director's freedom.
>
> Everyone subconsciously wants to be a director — wants to direct others — and is unwilling to be directed by others.
>
> Even within a family, the husband wants to direct the wife, and the wife wants to direct the husband.
>
> Everyone subconsciously wants to control their own fate — wants to become the Greatest Creator. But this is impossible. This is called plotting mischief, disrupting the program.
>
> Many modern "masters" teach this: "You yourself are God." "You create your own God." And so forth. Jesus Christ, the Buddha Shakyamuni, the Prophet Muhammad, and the Sage Laozi do not teach people this way.
>
> Satan was originally an archangel, but he wanted to become God. As a result, Satan became a devil.
>
> Anyone who wants to become the Greatest Creator themselves is an incarnation or representative of the devil. Anyone who incites others to want to become the Greatest Creator is a representative and accomplice of the devil.
>
> Is inciting ministers, governors, premiers, leaders, and religious figures to seize the presidency not the work of the devil?
>
> Therefore, I advocate that people "be at ease with whatever comes, go with the flow, act naturally, act in the moment." This is called "following the Dao's nature."
>
> Every person has "nature (xing)." This nature is Heaven's mandate. Following one's nature is following Heaven's mandate. Following Heaven's mandate is aligning with the Dao. Aligning with the Dao is being a celestial being.
>
> A screenwriter's script is a kind of program. Once an actor has chosen a director, they enter a kind of program and begin performing life according to the program.
>
> If we don't choose this program, we must choose another program. It is impossible not to operate within a program — unless we ourselves are the Greatest Creator. But can we become the Greatest Creator?
>
> By no means entertain the delusion of becoming the incarnation or accomplice of the devil.
>
> There are thousands upon thousands of programs. A family is one program; once you enter the family program, you must play the roles of father, child, husband, wife, brother, sister, and so on.
>
> A nation is a program; once you enter a certain nation, you must endure or enjoy everything that nation gives.
>
> Political parties and religious organizations are likewise programs; once you enter these programs, you must observe them and endure their constraints.
>
> Of course, we can choose not to enter any "human-made" programs. But if we do, we will enter the program of "wandering soul."
>
> Of course, we can strive to become directors — but becoming a director requires a very clear understanding of the script and thorough knowledge of everything in it.
>
> Of course, we can strive to become screenwriters — but becoming a screenwriter requires corresponding capacity.
>
> Since the ultimate screenwriter is the Greatest Creator, we choose to be actors in the Greatest Creator's script. Whoever understands the Greatest Creator and the Greatest Creator's script best — we let them be our director.
>
> Moreover, since we must be actors either way, we might as well choose the most ideal role to play.
>
