# The Script of Human Life: Core Category of Lifechanyuan's Individual LIFE Cosmology and Its Philosophical Significance

> In Lifechanyuan terminology, **LIFE** (capitalized) refers to the ontological
> essence of existence — the soul/antimatter structure that persists across
> incarnations — while **life** (lowercase) refers to the experiential stage
> of human existence in this world.

## Abstract

"The Script of Human Life" (人生剧本) is the core category in Lifechanyuan's cosmology that focuses on the level of individual LIFE — the complete individual-dimension elaboration of the "Cosmic Script" proposition. This category takes "the essence of life is to perform according to the requirements of the cosmic script" as its central axiom. It reveals the structure of human life through the "screenwriter–director–actor" role system; establishes an individual karmic cosmology through the mechanism of "this life fixed / next life alive"; delineates the most profound prohibition of human life through "the prohibition against self-scripting"; and establishes the only genuine space for personal effort through "intentions and perfection of role performance." This article systematically examines the textual sources, internal logic, and comparative significance of this category.

## II. Internal Logical Structure

### 2.1 The Core Proposition on the Nature of Life: Experience, Perceive, Ascend, Serve

The first core proposition of the "Script of Human Life" category:

> The process of LIFE traveling through the human world is called life. The meaning of life is to experience, perceive, and ascend — serving the Greatest Creator. The essence of life is to perform according to the requirements of the cosmic script.

This proposition positions life as a **service-oriented existence within the cosmic script** — not autonomous creation, but experiencing and ascending within the cosmic script's framework. "Serving the Greatest Creator" and "experiencing and ascending" are listed in parallel, meaning the growth of individual LIFE is itself a component of the cosmic script's operation, not something opposed to it.

### 2.2 The Role System at the Individual Level

Unlike the macroscopic perspective of "The Cosmic Script," "The Script of Human Life" focuses on the **specific situation of the individual actor**:

- **Individual-level script**: Birthplace, family, personality, encounters, even one's name — all within the script's arrangement
- **Sub-scripts within the grand script**: Within the cosmic grand script, each person can also write personal sub-scripts (marriage, children, business, etc.) — but these sub-scripts cannot transcend the grand script's overall arrangement
- **The Guide as the concrete earthly director**: Hundun Yuanchu directs Chanyuan Celestials toward the Celestial Islands Continent — this is an actionable director-choice within the script of human life

### 2.3 The Prohibition Against Self-Scripting: The Most Profound Taboo in the Script of Human Life

The naming of life's greatest trap: **self-scripting, self-directing, self-acting**.

> Harboring the thought of self-scripting and self-directing is doing what the devil does — it is self-destruction, bringing disaster upon oneself, walking the road of no return toward catastrophe and ruin.

Satan wanted to become the Greatest Creator and fell — this is the most stunning cosmic-scale case of "self-scripting" in the script of human life. Anyone who tries to design their own fate according to their own will is retracing Satan's path.

The philosophical function of this proposition: elevating "acceptance of fate" from passive resignation into **active recognition of cosmic order** — not helplessness, but awakening.

### 2.4 The Operational Standard for "Playing One's Role Well"

"The Script of Human Life" provides the most operational cultivation standard:

| Dimension | Content |
|-----------|---------|
| Space for effort | Intentions (good/evil) + perfection of role performance |
| Verification standard | Calm and at ease at all times, joyful and happy, grateful |
| Failure signals | Hesitation, worry, pain, anxiety, dread, fear |
| Reward/punishment logic | Good performance → Heaven; poor performance → Hell (regardless of role's morality) |

Actors cannot blame the script or the director — how well the role is played is entirely the actor's own affair.

## III. Comparative Philosophical Research

### 3.1 Comparison with Western Existentialism

Sartre's existentialism proclaims "existence precedes essence" — the person first exists, then creates their own essence and meaning. "The Script of Human Life" is the complete opposite: **essence (script) precedes existence (performance)** — the script is written first; the person is born and performs according to it. These are fundamentally opposed cosmological views. But both share one common ground: both emphasize **attitudinal responsibility** for one's existential state — Sartre's "authenticity" corresponds to Lifechanyuan's "playing one's role well."

### 3.2 Comparison with Confucian "Tianming" (Heaven's Mandate)

The Confucian view of Heaven's mandate holds that human LIFE trajectories are arranged by Heaven, but the exemplary person must still "know Heaven's mandate, establish human affairs" — doing one's utmost while accepting Heaven's will. "The Script of Human Life" aligns closely: accept the determined fate (this life's script), focus on human affairs (intentions and role performance). The difference: Confucian Heaven's mandate leans toward the ethical (practice benevolence and righteousness); "The Script of Human Life" leans toward the inner-state theory (calm, joyful, grateful).

### 3.3 Comparison with Strict Determinism in Indian Vedic Tradition

The early strict determinism of Hindu thought holds that everything is fixed and humans are powerless. "The Script of Human Life" acknowledges that the plot is fixed, but explicitly preserves **intention as the only free space** — this is more humanistically compassionate and practically valuable than strict determinism.

### 3.4 Comparison with the Christian "Life as Pilgrimage" View

The Christian tradition views life as a pilgrimage to eternal Heaven — this life is a brief trial. "The Script of Human Life" shares structural similarities (life is a process, Heaven is the destination), but differs in mechanism: Christian Heaven is entered through faith and grace; "The Script of Human Life"'s Heaven is entered through playing one's role well (perfecting the antimatter structure of LIFE).

## IV. Theoretical Limitations and Open Questions

1. **Blurred boundary between grand script and sub-scripts**: *The Value and Meaning of LIFE* notes that within the grand script, humans may write personal sub-scripts (marriage, children, etc.). However, the systematic criteria for distinguishing necessary nodes in the grand script from free space in sub-scripts remain underdeveloped.

2. **Relationship between "playing one's role well" and "forming good intentions"**: The texts list both as spaces for effort, but in extreme situations (e.g., being assigned to play an abuser), the relationship between "playing well" and "forming good intentions" still awaits more nuanced elaboration.
