# Wuji (Internal English Version)

## I. Overview and Definition

Wuji is the most fundamental starting-point concept in the Lifechanyuan cosmological system. It refers to the primordial state before the birth of the universe, before the emergence of LIFE, time, space, matter, and consciousness. It is also treated as a key for understanding “Wuji gives rise to Taiji,” the origin of the universe, and core meanings in the *Diamond Sutra* and the *Dao De Jing*.

Core definition (New Era Human 800 Concepts, Article 416):

All entities within the range of time, including the celestial universe, have an origin, a birth time, and a process of development. The universe is no exception. Before the universe was born, there was “Wuji.” The state of Wuji is: no outside and no inside, no large and no small, boundless, neither void nor solid, no time, no space, no matter, no spirit—clear and yet chaotic; all being and all non-being.

Supplementary definition (The Eight Connotations of the Way of the Greatest Creator):

At the very beginning, the universe was empty: Wuji, chaos, boundless, no large and no small, formless, no spirit and no soul, with only unordered movement of invisible energy.

## II. The Essence of Wuji: “No-thing that contains all”

Wuji is not “nothing” in ordinary language. It is a special mode of existence: the “no” that contains all possibilities.

In the Chanyuan writings, this is expressed as follows: if one says it exists, nothing formed can be pointed to; if one says it does not exist, it contains all potential emergence.

This is used to explain why key *Diamond Sutra* formulations are difficult for ordinary logical thinking:

- “No dharma-form, and no non-dharma-form.”
- “What Tathagata speaks of as Dharma is neither graspable nor sayable—neither dharma nor non-dharma.”
- “What is called Buddhadharma is not Buddhadharma.”
- “What Tathagata calls world is no-world; therefore it is named world.”

In this interpretive line, without understanding Wuji, these formulations remain opaque.

## III. Space in Wuji: Beyond fixed spatial form

Wuji has no fixed spatial concept. It transcends ordinary spatial determinacy.

A classical metaphor used is the golden cymbal story from *Journey to the West*: expand greatly and the enclosure expands with you; shrink to a tiny seed and it shrinks with you. This is used to illustrate Wuji-space as non-fixed, non-container-like, and non-objectifiable.

The internal critique of naive spatial realism is explicit: space is not an empty box independent of all content. When material distribution vanishes, corresponding space relation vanishes as well. Space is linked to distribution and energy conditions of existence.

Therefore, in Wuji—where no formed matter exists—ordinary space does not exist.

## IV. Time in Wuji: no movement, no time

Time is described as the recorder of movement-state. Where movement does not occur, time does not arise.

Illustrative reasoning in the text:

- biological age depends on cellular movement;
- slowing/stopping movement delays decay-signature;
- if there is no birth event, no personal timeline appears;
- if Earth is not born, Earth-time does not appear;
- if universe is not born, universe-time does not appear.

Hence, in Wuji there is no time in the ordinary temporal sense.

## V. Energy in Wuji: invisible and unordered motion

Although Wuji has no formed matter, no time, no space, and no consciousness, it is not absolute emptiness. Invisible energy exists in Wuji and moves in unordered ways.

This is the key to “Wuji gives rise to Taiji”: through unordered movement of invisible energy, a specific sequence may accidentally form at a point, triggering cosmic emergence.

Four characteristics of Wuji-energy:

1. invisible (not directly form-bound),
2. unordered (no prior law-pattern sequence),
3. non-spiritual (Wuji is not the Greatest Creator),
4. non-conscious (consciousness appears after structure emerges).

## VI. Three essential clarifications

### (1) Wuji is not the Greatest Creator

Wuji is chaotic pre-conscious state. It has no spirituality and therefore is not the Greatest Creator.

### (2) Wuji is not “absolute nothingness”

Wuji is “no” in a special sense: no formed order, yet containing energetic potential for emergence.

### (3) Relation between Wuji and Dao

A key distinction is drawn: Dao is interpreted as the spirit-expression of the Greatest Creator after Creator-emergence, not as a pre-Creator independent principle in strict sequence terms.

An analogy used: constitution/law appears after social subjectivity appears, not before social existence itself.

## VII. Wuji and chaos: relation to hundun distinction

Wuji is equated with chaos-state before universe formation.

The framework distinguishes two terms:

- **chaos (混沌)**: unordered pre-formation state,
- **hundun order (浑沌)**: ordered-whole post-formation state.

Hence:

- Wuji = chaos (unordered, non-spiritual, non-conscious),
- Taiji = hundun order (ordered, spiritual, conscious after Creator emergence).

Core proposition: Wuji gives rise to Taiji; chaos transforms into hundun order.

## VIII. Wuji gives rise to Taiji: mechanism of universe emergence

This transition is treated as the single cosmic contingency; afterward, process unfolds by necessity.

Mechanism narrative:

- invisible energy in Wuji moves unordered,
- at a point, accidental sequencing appears,
- a structured tendency forms (described as potential-vortex pattern),
- this is Taiji in potential-state,
- structure gives rise to consciousness,
- consciousness is identified as the Greatest Creator,
- then cosmic order expands and time-space layers appear.

A compact internal formula:

unordered energy → accidental order → structure → consciousness (Greatest Creator) → Dao-governed unfolding.

## IX. Wuji and Taiji: fundamental contrast

Wuji and Taiji form the two most basic phases in cosmic history:

- Wuji: no inside/outside, no large/small, no time, no space, no formed matter, no consciousness, unordered energy movement;
- Taiji: unity-state, formless-yet-structured, immense energy whole, consciousness-present source-state.

Core transformation:

- from unordered chaos to ordered hundun whole,
- from non-conscious to conscious.

A classical in-text summary:

Wuji gives rise to Taiji; Taiji is the Greatest Creator; Taiji arises naturally, therefore nature is the Greatest Creator.

## X. Wuji and the Diamond Sutra relation

In this framework, understanding Wuji helps explain why *Diamond Sutra* language appears paradoxical under ordinary logic.

Wuji as “all-being and all-non-being” provides cosmological background for “neither dharma nor non-dharma” formulations. Before formed emergence, all possibilities are included without formed objecthood.

Thus, understanding Wuji is treated as a key to deep-level Buddhadharma interpretation.

## XI. Position of Wuji in universe-origin theory

Wuji occupies the most fundamental starting position in this cosmology:

1. Without Wuji, no Taiji; without Taiji, no Creator-emergence; without Creator-emergence, no cosmic unfolding.
2. Wuji is the field in which the single cosmic contingency occurs (Creator-emergence).
3. Wuji serves as interpretive key for both Dao-origin and deep sutra logic.
4. “Wuji gives rise to Taiji; chaos transforms into hundun order” is treated as a core new concept in Lifechanyuan universe-origin discourse.

## XII. Historical significance (in-system assessment)

Within this framework, Wuji is considered historically significant because it:

1. defines Wuji precisely as the “no that contains all,”
2. explains absence of time and space in pre-formation state through movement-relation logic,
3. identifies invisible unordered energy movement as dynamic basis for Taiji emergence,
4. clarifies Wuji is not the Creator and resolves conceptual confusions,
5. links Wuji to deep *Diamond Sutra* interpretive structure,
6. condenses universe-origin mechanism into a highly compact proposition.

Concise internal summary:

Wuji is unordered chaotic energy; accidental ordering generates structure; structure gives rise to consciousness; that consciousness is the Greatest Creator; after Creator emergence, Wuji-chaos transforms into Taiji-hundun order.
