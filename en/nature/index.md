# Nature (性 Xìng)

[Gateway Page]

> In Lifechanyuan terminology, **LIFE** (capitalized) refers to the ontological essence of existence — the soul/antimatter structure that persists across incarnations — while **life** (lowercase) refers to the experiential stage of human existence in this world.

**Nature** (性, Xìng) is the deepest, most fundamental concept in the Lifechanyuan theoretical system. It is the characteristic of **Structure** — one of the Three Cosmic Elements — one of the three essential qualities of the Way of the Greatest Creator, the ultimate destination of all cultivation practice, another name for Buddha, the foundational basis of all existence and distinction among the ten thousand things, and the shortest path to understanding the universe, LIFE, and oneself.

> The true transmission contains only one word: **Nature** (性).
>
> — Guide Xuefeng, *New Era Human 800 Concepts*, Article 799

> The essence of the Way of the Greatest Creator is Nature, Love, and the Way. Nature is the characteristic of Structure; Love is the characteristic of Energy; the Way is the characteristic of Consciousness.
>
> — Article 516

---

## Core Positioning

In the Lifechanyuan theoretical framework, **Nature** (Xìng) simultaneously operates on five levels:

- **Cosmological level**: Nature is the fundamental characteristic of Structure, one of the Three Cosmic Elements (Article 516)
- **Ontological level**: Buddha is Nature; Nature is Buddha — seeing one's Nature is becoming Buddha (Article 675)
- **Cultivation level**: The true transmission is one word: Nature; cultivating Nature is cultivating Buddhahood (Article 799)
- **LIFE-classification level**: Each structure produces its corresponding Nature; Nature determines the essence and level of LIFE
- **Civilizational level**: From sensibility → rationality → intellectuality → spirituality, Civilization 3.0 is the spiritual age of living within Nature
