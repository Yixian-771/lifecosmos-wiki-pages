---

## I. One Word, Everything

If you could only transmit all of Lifechanyuan's wisdom in one single word — what would it be?

> **The true transmission contains only one word: Nature (性).**
>
> — Guide Xuefeng, *New Era Human 800 Concepts*, Article 799

One word, "Nature" (性, Xìng) — encompassing the Three Cosmic Elements, the ultimate path of cultivation, the essence of Buddha, the foundational basis of all existence — and the core of Civilization 3.0.

This single word is cleaner and more direct than the Bible, the Diamond Sutra, and the Tao Te Ching combined.

## II. What Is "Nature" (性)?

"Nature" (Xìng) in Lifechanyuan doesn't mean gender, personality, or sexuality (though all are involved). 

**Nature is the characteristic of Structure.**

> Mountains have mountain-Nature, water has water-Nature, flowers have flower-Nature, trees have tree-Nature, people have human-Nature, beasts have beast-Nature — everything with a specific structure has a Nature; different structures, different Natures.
>
> — *Xuefeng Corpus · "Xuefeng on Nature"*

This sounds simple — but the implication is vast: as long as something exists, it has structure; as long as it has structure, it has Nature. Nothing without Nature exists at all.

The universe is constituted by three basic elements: Consciousness, Structure, and Energy.
- Characteristic of Consciousness → **the Way**
- Characteristic of Structure → **Nature**
- Characteristic of Energy → **Love**

*(Article 516)*

"Nature" is therefore not a moral concept, not a psychological concept — but a cosmological-level ontological concept, standing alongside "the Way" and "Love" as one of the three fundamental characteristics of cosmic existence.

## III. Nature Is Buddha — The Most Stunning Equation

Lifechanyuan's most important revelation about "Nature" is these words:

> **Buddha is Nature; Nature is Buddha.** (Article 675)

> **Clarify the mind and see the Nature; seeing the Nature is seeing the Buddha.** (Article 687)

> **If one does not see one's Nature, one wanders aimlessly all day, seeking outwardly, unable to find Buddha.** (Article 688)

Where is Buddha? Buddha is not in a statue, not in a scripture, not in a temple. Buddha is Nature. Your own Nature is Buddha.

The moment you see your own true Nature — you see Buddha.

This isn't Xuefeng's invention. Bodhidharma's *Treatise on Blood Veins* already said:

> If one does not see one's Nature, all speech is demonic speech; all action is demonic action.

And the Sixth Patriarch Huineng:
> How remarkable — self-nature is originally pure; how remarkable — self-nature is originally unborn and undying; how remarkable — self-nature is originally self-sufficient and complete.

What Xuefeng does is connect this highest Chan wisdom to the Three Cosmic Elements theory — "Nature is the characteristic of Structure" provides the cosmological basis for why "Buddha is Nature." Chan Buddhism's seeing-Nature-and-becoming-Buddha here acquires a foundation in structural theory.

## IV. You Cannot Escape Nature — Because You *Are* Nature

> **People dwell within Nature, just as fish dwell within water. People come from Nature, live within Nature, and return to Nature. Without Nature, there are no people.**
>
> **Living within Nature is living within Buddha. To become Buddha, one must live fully within Nature. People can transcend everything — but can never transcend Nature.**
>
> — *"Nature Formation"*

> **What is the universe? The universe is Nature. The ten thousand things of the great thousand worlds are all Nature — all the blossoming of Nature.**
>
> — *"Sensibility, Rationality, Intellectuality, Spirituality"*

You cannot escape Nature. Because you yourself are Nature.

The question is not "how to obtain Nature" — but "which Nature are you living within?"

## V. Three Kinds of Nature: Heavenly, Innate, and Habitual

Lifechanyuan divides "Nature" into three layers:

| Level | Name | Meaning | Corresponds to |
|-------|------|---------|----------------|
| Highest | **Heavenly Nature** (天性) | The Creator's essential attribute; shared by all of the same kind | Way-Nature; emptiness-spirit-beauty; no distinction |
| Middle | **Innate Nature** (秉性) | A specific characteristic given to distinguish types | Virtue-Nature; Taiji yin-yang; distinction |
| Lowest | **Habitual Nature** (习性) | Individual habits formed by environment and experience | Rationality; humanity-righteousness-ritual-wisdom-faithfulness |

What does cultivation do? Three things:

> **Overcome Habitual Nature; discard Innate Nature; restore Heavenly Nature.**

Or equivalently: transcend the rationality of humanity-righteousness-ritual-wisdom-faithfulness → sublimate beyond Virtue-Nature → return to Way-Nature. That is "escaping the Nature Formation."

## VI. Universal Holography: You Have Six Kinds of Nature

Lifechanyuan has an astonishing proposition:

> **The universe is holographic. People are born with divine-Nature, Buddha-Nature, immortal-Nature, human-Nature, beast-Nature, and matter-Nature.** (Article 53)

You are not just human-Nature. You carry all six kinds simultaneously — it's just that whichever dominates determines what you are.

> Divine-Nature fills it — it is a god; Buddha-Nature is sufficient — it is a Buddha; demonic-Nature dominates — it is a demon; human-Nature is full — it is a person.

So cultivation's essence is changing structure — determining which Nature dominates.

**What are human-Nature's characteristics?** (*"Continuing to Discuss LIFE Quality"*)

Selfish, shortsighted, pursuing money, power, position, reputation; maximizing resource possession; lying, deceiving, scheming; jealousy, anger, greed, fear…

**What are immortal-Nature's eight characteristics?** (*"Immortal Nature"*)

1. Following conditions with broad ease; acting freely according to one's nature
2. Coming and going without fixed measure; responding according to circumstance
3. Leaving feeling everywhere; never clinging heavily to feeling
4. Possessing nothing; completely unbound
5. Acting through non-action; moving along the Way
6. Neither merciful nor sorrowful; prioritizing ease and grace
7. Not contending, not debating; walking together with celestials
8. Immortal bearing and Way-bones; laughing and speaking with ease

From human-Nature to immortal-Nature — this is the entire content of cultivation.

## VII. Cultivating Nature: The Shortest Path

How do you cultivate? 

> **Cultivating Buddhahood is cultivating Nature; cultivating Nature is cultivating Buddhahood; seeing one's Nature is becoming Buddha.** (*"Sensibility, Rationality, Intellectuality, Spirituality"*)

> **If cultivation practice does not face Nature, does not cultivate Nature — it is scratching the boot from the outside; it is wildly off-topic; it is putting the cart before the horse.**

> **If one does not see one's Nature — chanting Buddha's name, reciting sutras, holding mantras, giving alms, observing precepts, building temples, making offerings, releasing animals, prostrating, burning incense — one cannot become Buddha.** (Article 688)

This one sentence directly nullifies countless religious cultivation methods.

Where is the shortcut?

> **For people who wish to become celestials, there is a shortcut: acting according to immortal-Nature. Even a person who is illiterate — as long as they act according to immortal-Nature, gradually and over time, becoming a celestial is hopeful; becoming a celestial can be achieved.** (*"Immortal Nature"*)

No need for vast tomes; no need for meditation and elixir refining; no need for daily sutra recitation — **act according to immortal-Nature**. That is enough.

> Follow circumstances as they are; transform along conditions; act according to Nature; work according to opportunity. (Article 157)

"Nature" (性) also involves the Nature of sexuality — Lifechanyuan's understanding is very distinctive:

> Sexual love is produced by Innate Nature (Taiji principle: all things bear yin and embrace yang).

The direction of cultivation: transcend Taiji yin-yang, return to Heavenly Nature, achieve self-consistency, transcend sexual love — this is the state of Buddha, the state of a celestial.

But —

> **Do not flee from Nature. The more one flees from Nature, the further one moves from Heavenly Nature-Way-Nature — Buddha-Nature.**

> **Only through Nature can one return to Nature. Only when yin and yang achieve balanced saturation can one return to the sea of Nature — Heavenly Nature-Way-Nature.** (*"Nature Formation"*)

This is a profound paradox: fleeing sexuality is not the path to Buddhahood. Experiencing Nature, experiencing yin-yang, achieving balanced saturation — only then can one truly return to Heavenly Nature.
