## I. Overview and Definition

Rational Thinking is the collective name for the four rungs of Material Thinking, Imagery Thinking, Associative Thinking, and Illusory Thinking. It is the collective name for "human thinking" and encompasses all thinking modes below the watershed between humans and celestial beings. Rational Thinking's core is: basing on large quantities of facts and phenomena, observing, comparing, analyzing, and inducting events or problems, then synthesizing to find patterns, then ascending to abstraction, generalization, and judgment, thus finding the direction of development or solving problems. Rational Thinking is the thinking of the Worthy (*xianren*) — humanity's highest thinking — yet as human thinking it has limitations: no matter how rigorous, how objective, one's recognition of the vast world and of human nature remains a view through a narrow tube, unable to make universal connections; relying on human ingenuity and cleverness, a civilized society cannot be built.

**Core Definition I** (*Xuefeng Corpus · Vol. X09 Miscellaneous Essays · Critique of Rational Thinking*, complete original text):

> Using large quantities of facts and phenomena as the basis, the conscious activity of observing, comparing, analyzing, and inducting events or problems, then synthesizing to find patterns, then ascending to abstraction, generalization, and judgment, thus finding the direction of development or solving problems — this is Rational Thinking.

**Core Definition II** (*Xuefeng Corpus · Vol. X09 Miscellaneous Essays · Critique of Rational Thinking*, complete original text):

> Rational Thinking is the thinking of the Worthy. It belongs to humanity's highest thinking. The majority of humanity does not possess Rational Thinking; most people think by instinct alone, fixing their thinking on wealth, power, reputation, and status. The reason humanity has not yet entered a civilized society is that the thinking of the masses is too materialistic, while the thinking of the Worthy is too rational. One might say that Rational Thinking is one of the great obstacles to humanity entering a civilized society.

**Core Definition III** (*Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom Chapter · The Way of Thinking and Doing of Spiritual Thinkers*, complete original text):

> Material Thinking, Imagery Thinking, Associative Thinking, and Illusory Thinking all belong to Rational Thinking — this is human thinking. Heart-Image Thinking, Taiji Thinking, Non-Form Thinking, and Hundun Thinking all belong to celestial thinking. In other words, Rational Thinking and Spiritual Thinking are the watershed between humans and celestial beings. If your thinking is Rational Thinking, do not doubt it — you are definitely human.

**Core Definition IV** (*Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom Chapter · The Way of Thinking and Doing of Spiritual Thinkers*, complete original text):

> To put it plainly: if the starting point of our thinking about problems comes from material, imagery, associative, or illusory angles — if what we focus on is how to solve food, clothing, shelter, transportation, birth, aging, illness, and death; how to ensure safety; how to gain respect, dignity, and a good reputation; how to live in a way that makes people envious or even worshipful; how to navigate with ease through complex social strata and interpersonal relationships — this is Rational Thinking, the thinking of humans.

**Core Definition V** (*Chanyuan Corpus · Human Life Chapter · A Spiritual Life Is Higher Than a Rational Life*, complete original text):

> A rational life is a life of using knowledge, facts, logic, and science to pursue a beautiful life. A rational life does not rely on slogans, stances, promises, or emotions to seek the best path in life. Therefore, a rational life is the life philosophy of the Worthy, not the life philosophy of the Muddle-headed, the Worldly, or the Common.
>
> The core of rationality is calculation through logical reasoning based on knowledge and evidence; the core of spirituality is the naturalness and spontaneity based on faith and intuition.

**Core Definition VI** (*Chanyuan Corpus · Cultivation of Immortality Chapter · A Brief Introduction to the Key Points of Spiritual Thinking*, complete original text excerpt):

> Rational Thinking is the hallmark of the Worthy; Spiritual Thinking is the hallmark of the celestial.

**Limitation III: Unable to Design a System That Makes 99% of People Happy** (*Xuefeng Corpus · Vol. X09 Miscellaneous Essays · Critique of Rational Thinking*, complete original text):

## VII. The Fundamental Differences Between Rational Thinking and Spiritual Thinking

(*Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom Chapter · The Way of Thinking and Doing of Spiritual Thinkers*, complete original text):

**Difference I: Macro vs. Micro**

> I. Spiritual thinkers first grasp the macro; Rational thinkers grasp the micro. For example, in finding a job: Spiritual thinkers focus on whether the work brings joy and happiness; Rational thinkers focus on whether the salary is high, whether the environment and conditions are good, what the prospects for development are. Why is this? Because Spiritual thinkers consider the direction of their LIFE — whether they can ultimately reach heaven; as long as what they are doing is inconsistent with their destination, they are unwilling to do it. Whereas Rational thinkers consider whether the work can resolve current living needs; they do not think about whether what they are currently doing will affect their ultimate goal. In this sense, Spiritual thinkers consider how to live; Rational thinkers consider how to survive.

**Difference II: LIFE vs. Life**

> II. Spiritual thinkers consider LIFE; Rational thinkers consider life. Those who consider LIFE must understand the principles, mysteries, and relationship with time and space of LIFE. Those who consider life only need to learn the knowledge and experience accumulated by humanity and understand the operating laws of human society.

**Difference III: Simplification vs. Complication**

> III. Spiritual thinkers simplify complex matters to the greatest extent; Rational thinkers always complicate simple matters as much as possible.

**Difference IV: Heavenly Fortune vs. Helplessness** (*Chanyuan Corpus · Human Life Chapter · A Spiritual Life Is Higher Than a Rational Life*, complete original text):

> Mudslide pouring down a slope instantly stopped for a minivan, and after the minivan escaped, the mudslide again rushed downward with overwhelming force. How does rationality analyze and explain this phenomenon? Rationality is helpless — but the Spiritual Thinking explanation is: the people in the minivan had heavenly fortune; it was Dao that stopped the mudslide and rescued the people with heavenly fortune. The Israelites fleeing Egypt had to cross the Red Sea, and the Red Sea stopped flowing to make way for the Israelites — how can Rational Thinking explain this? Also helpless.
