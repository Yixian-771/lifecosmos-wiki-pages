# Elysian Bliss · Peak Experience (Internal Version)

## Source Quotations

### I. Peak Experience Text: The Eighteen Characteristics

**Source: Xuefeng, "The Peak Experience and Elysian Bliss of Life" (September 30, 2011)**

> American psychologist Abraham Maslow, through extensive research across five continents, confirmed the highest experiential state that human life can reach — the Peak Experience. Xuefeng studied Maslow's Peak Experience and aligned it with Buddhism's "Pure Land" and Taoism's "union with the Tao," reaching this conclusion: Maslow's Peak Experience is the blissful realm of which Buddhism speaks — Lifechanyuan's Elysian Bliss.

> Xuefeng summarized the characteristics of peak experience into 18 points:
> 1. Harmony and wholeness — feeling complete and unified
> 2. Self-dissolution — merging with heaven and earth as one
> 3. Everything flowing — being at one's absolute best
> 4. Effortless mastery — moving with ease and grace
> 5. Boundless confidence — nothing feels impossible
> 6. Free of pain — seeing everything through optimistic eyes
> 7. Innocent simplicity — pure as a child
> 8. Fluid and spontaneous — improvising with natural ease
> 9. Uniquely oneself — free, uninhibited expression
> 10. Fully present — absorbed entirely in the now
> 11. Transcendence — spirit rising above the mundane
> 12. Vitality — full of life force and inner completeness
> 13. Poetic rapture — mysterious, ecstatic delight
> 14. Complete release — unburdened and liberated
> 15. Clear and transparent — free from enemies, free from hatred
> 16. Serene detachment — extreme inner peace
> 17. Enchanted wonder — inexplicably beautiful
> 18. Joyful gratitude — overwhelmed with thankfulness to the divine

> There are three pathways to temporarily enter the Zero-State: one is to stand within vast nature, with mind undistracted, concentrated and present, ego dissolved; [the second involves content from a restricted text — omitted here]; third is sitting meditation, contemplation — when deep meditation is reached, consciousness returns to zero.

> To remain in the Zero-State permanently and forever is to remain in the formless state permanently and forever. No-Form Thinking is the only path to the eternal Zero-State. The Diamond Sutra is the finest scripture for cultivating No-Form Thinking.

### VI. The Complete Definition of Self-Coherence

**Source: Xuefeng, "The Peak Experience and Elysian Bliss of Life" (September 30, 2011)**

> Self-Coherence (*自洽*) means a life possesses complete yin-yang wholeness within itself — achieving inner harmony and balance without needing to draw on external sources — like the Taiji symbol where yin and yang are perfectly merged.

> Four conditions for Self-Coherence: first, no weeds in the Soul Garden; second, repay the karmic debts of lifetimes, achieving freedom from all attachment and hindrance; third, accumulate sufficient heavenly merit; fourth, consciousness enters the perfect celestial-Buddha state, undergo bone-deep transformation, truly embody celestial consciousness.

> The construction of the Second Home is designed precisely to accomplish these four tasks — paving the road toward eternal Elysian Bliss.

## Practitioner Notes

1. The essay *"Climbing Boldly Toward Elysian Bliss"* (2016) is marked "**This text is prohibited from external circulation.**" It addresses the deep mechanism of Elysian Bliss and sexual energy cultivation. Its content is not included in this entry. Advanced practitioners should consult the original in the internal library.

2. The permanent path to Elysian Bliss is closely linked to Diamond Sutra practice. A dedicated entry on the Diamond Sutra (*《金刚经》*) exists separately; practitioners are encouraged to cross-reference.

3. Xuefeng's 18 characteristics depart somewhat from Maslow's original — Xuefeng reorganized and elevated them through a Chinese cultivation lens, emphasizing their significance for practice rather than presenting purely psychological description.
