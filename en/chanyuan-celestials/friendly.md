## Why "Celestials" (Grass)?

This name sounds a bit strange at first—why not "Chanyuan Flowers" or "Chanyuan Trees," but specifically "Celestials" (草, literally "grass")?

Actually, this name contains deep meaning:

**Surface Humility**: Grass is the weakest life in nature, trampled upon, swaying in the wind, with no aggression. Chanyuan Celestials say: "We are the weakest group in the world, not confronting any power."

**Inner Magic**: But think carefully—though grass is weak, it's the foundation of all life. Without grass, the earth would be barren. Moreover, the "celestials" in Chanyuan are special—they are rare and precious grass, seeds of celestial beings.

Like the rose in "The Little Prince"—it looks ordinary, yet it's unique.
