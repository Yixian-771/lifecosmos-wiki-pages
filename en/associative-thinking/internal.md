## I. Overview and Definition

Associative Thinking is the third rung of the Eight Thinking Ladders, between Imagery Thinking and Illusory Thinking. Associative Thinking refers to the capacity to grasp a phenomenon in nature, then through that phenomenon, reason by analogy, draw inferences by extension, and link all related factors together. Associative Thinking possesses primacy, inventiveness, pioneering spirit, and originality — it is a form of divergent thinking that breaks through the fixed patterns of Material Thinking, transcends the imitation and copying of Imagery Thinking, and extends its reach directly into unknown territories, leading to new discoveries, new inventions, and new pathways. Scientists and inventors are the typical representatives of Associative Thinking; those who have driven human civilization slowly forward are primarily people with Associative Thinking capacity.

**Core Definition I** (*Chanyuan Corpus · Revelation Chapter · Associative Thinking*, complete original text):

> The thinking that can grasp a phenomenon in nature, and then, through that phenomenon, reason by analogy, draw inferences by extension, and link all related factors together is called Associative Thinking.

Sakyamuni attained the realm of Non-Form Thinking; Laozi attained the realm of Taiji Thinking; the thinking of celestials is Heart-Image Thinking; the thinking Zhuge Liang, Einstein, and others reached is Associative Thinking; the realm attained by artists such as Beethoven is Imagery Thinking; ordinary people's thinking is Material Thinking; religious practitioners possess Illusory Thinking.

This is the most precise and incisive revelation of Associative Thinking in the Lifechanyuan system — the vast majority of people believe they have Associative Thinking; in reality they are merely applying others' knowledge and experience.

(*Chanyuan Corpus · Revelation Chapter · Associative Thinking*, complete original text):

> Associative Thinking possesses primacy, inventiveness, pioneering spirit, and originality — it is a form of divergent thinking. Many people appear to have Associative Thinking but actually do not; they are merely applying the associative knowledge of others, or the experience distilled from prolonged practice. Many farmers can think of spring plowing, summer growth, autumn harvest, and winter storage from the changing of the seasons — but this is not the result of their own Associative Thinking; it is the reflection of accumulated experience in consciousness. Many people can think of weather changes from arthritic pain — this too is the effect of knowledge and experience, not the result of Associative Thinking. Many merchants can think of what goods to buy and sell based on regional, temporal, and seasonal differentials — this too is the result of experience (with a few exceptions). They are merely applying others' knowledge or experience: knowledge learned in school or experience accumulated through work. For example, Newton associating from the falling apple to universal gravitation — that is the result of Associative Thinking. But if a university student says he can associate from a falling apple to universal gravitation, he is only recounting what he has learned, narrating Newton's discovery — not articulating his own Associative Thinking — unless he had never heard of Newton and never knew there was such a thing as gravity.

> Strictly speaking, only thinking that leads to new discoveries, new inventions, new creations, or new theories is Associative Thinking. In this sense, those with Associative Thinking are primarily scientists, inventors, forensic specialists, traditional Chinese physicians, physiognomists, feng shui masters, outstanding politicians and military strategists, and so on. Engineers and technicians who can develop genuinely new products may also be marginally included.
