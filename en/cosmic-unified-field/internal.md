## IV. The Eight Forces of the Universe

How many forces does the universe actually contain?

Answer: Eight — magnetic force, gravitational force, strong force, weak force, constructive force, repulsive force, intentional force, and spiritual force.

Brief descriptions of these eight forces:

**Magnetic force:** The interaction between charged particles.

**Gravitational force:** The mutual attraction between masses produced by the curvature of spacetime caused by motion.

**Strong force:** The force that binds quarks together inside protons and neutrons, and binds protons and neutrons together to form atoms.

**Weak force:** The force that acts on all matter particles but not on force-carrying particles.

**Constructive force:** The force produced by the structure of matter and antimatter — every kind of structure has its own unique capacity to absorb energy.

**Repulsive force:** The force of like repelling like.

**Intentional force:** The force analogous to the bladder and urethra beginning to work the moment the brain thinks about urination.

**Spiritual force:** The force that enables mutual sensing between things.

As I have said, the fundamental reason scientists cannot establish the Cosmic Unified Field theory is that they are unable to measure and calculate the latter four forces. Although these four forces cannot be measured and calculated, we cannot deny or ignore their existence. Since they exist, and you cannot bring them into a single formula or theoretical system, you cannot establish the Cosmic Unified Field. This is precisely why Einstein labored in vain.
*(Chanyuan Corpus · Preaching Chapter · "The Preaching Chapter," Part Six: The Cosmic Unified Field, 2005-12-30)*
