## Abstract

Lifechanyuan's treatment of the Cosmic Unified Field begins as a critique of modern physics: scientists have failed to establish a unified field theory because they study only the material world and acknowledge only four forces. Xuefeng argues that the universe contains eight forces — four recognized by science, and four in the antimatter/consciousness/spiritual domain that science cannot measure. On this basis, Lifechanyuan declares that the true Cosmic Unified Field already exists: it is the Tao of the Greatest Creator, expressed as the Hundun system of three cosmic elements (Consciousness, Structure, Energy), which holographically integrates all eight forces across matter and antimatter alike.

## II. Problem Statement: Physics' Unfulfilled Quest

Einstein devoted his final thirty years to seeking a "terminal formula" capable of unifying all known forces — without success. Contemporary physics has pursued Grand Unified Theories (GUT), Superstring Theory, and M-Theory, all operating within the framework of matter and the four known forces. Lifechanyuan's diagnosis: this is not merely a technical shortfall but a **fundamental error of direction** — the deliberate or unconscious exclusion of the antimatter domain.

## V. The Parallel Lines Corollary

Euclid's parallel postulate holds in two- and three-dimensional space; it is also upheld under quantum mechanics and holographic models. But within the Cosmic Unified Field framework — which incorporates lateral (横向) spacetime and intentional force — two parallel lines will eventually intersect. This is because lateral spacetime shifts and intentional/spiritual forces introduce asymmetric perturbations that no purely material model can capture.
