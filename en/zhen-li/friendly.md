# Truth (Zhen Li) · Friendly Version

## What Is Truth, Really?

For thousands of years, humanity has wrestled with one question: What is Truth?

Socrates, one of the greatest minds in history, spent his life searching — and concluded that the only thing he knew for certain was that he knew nothing. Descartes retreated into his own mind and could confirm only "I think, therefore I am." Einstein's relativity theory led many to believe Truth is relative — that what's true depends on your frame of reference.

Lifechanyuan offers a direct and unambiguous answer:

> What is Truth? The Greatest Creator is Truth.

More specifically: **Truth is the Tao, and the Tao is the consciousness and spirit of the Greatest Creator.**

Jesus said, "The Tao of the Greatest Creator is Truth." Laozi wrote the *Tao Te Ching* — five thousand characters about a single concept: the Tao. Confucius sighed, "If I hear the Tao in the morning, I can die in peace that evening." Shakyamuni taught for 49 years and at the center of everything was one word: Tao. They were all pointing at the same thing. They were all pointing at Truth.

## Eight Windows into Truth

Xuefeng describes eight fundamental attributes of Truth — eight angles from which we can begin to understand it:

**1. Nothingness**  
Truth comes from "nothing." The Buddhist teaching "Form is emptiness, emptiness is form" expresses this: being arises from non-being, and that non-being is the Tao — the source of Truth.

**2. Totality**  
Everything in the universe — every mountain, every tree, every living being — originates from the Tao and is an expression of Truth. Truth is not hiding somewhere else; it's right in front of you, in everything.

**3. Trustworthiness**  
Natural laws are Truth in action: apples fall, seasons turn, tides rise and fall — none of these consult your feelings before operating. Truth is utterly reliable. A person who breaks their word, lies, or makes promises they don't keep is, in this sense, acting against Truth.

**4. Naturalness**  
Truth flows naturally — like ripe fruit falling from a branch, like water finding its level. You cannot force Truth into being; you can only align with it. Living without excessive striving, accepting whatever life brings — this is living in accord with Truth.

**5. Justice**  
Truth plays no favorites. Whether you're a PhD or never finished school, a billionaire or a street vendor, a head of state or a shepherd — before Truth, you stand on exactly equal ground. Everyone has the same opportunity to know Truth.

**6. Warmth**  
Here is the surprising one: Truth feels *warm*. When you draw near to it, you don't feel dread or strangeness — you feel like you're coming home after a long journey. Settled. Calm. At ease.

**7. Uniqueness**  
There are countless paths to Truth, but only one Truth. There's no "roughly true" or "kind of true." The destination is singular.

**8. Eternity**  
Even if the Earth vanishes and humanity disappears, Truth will still be there. It existed before us and will exist after us. Truth does not age.

## Truth Is Alive — Don't Freeze It

There's an important nuance: Truth is alive, not frozen.

> Three-dimensional Truth may be error in four-dimensional space. Yesterday's Truth may become tomorrow's error… Whoever believes they've captured Truth and grips it tightly forever is someone drilling blindly into a dead end.

This doesn't mean Truth changes — it means our *understanding* of Truth must grow as we grow. Someone who stops at the level of understanding they reached decades ago and refuses to let it expand isn't honoring Truth; they're imprisoning it.

## Why Seeking Truth Is Life's Greatest Priority

> Truth is a priceless treasure. Seeking Truth, knowing Truth, and honoring Truth is the highest priority of human life — for whoever does so gains eternal LIFE and can reach the Thousand-Year World, the Ten-Thousand-Year World, or the Elysium World.

This is the ultimate promise of Truth-seeking in Lifechanyuan: it's not just intellectually satisfying — it determines where your soul goes after this life.

---

## Truth Isn't Found in the Crowd

The *American Encyclopedia* once observed: "Truth often provokes resentment, because Truth neither supports bias nor confirms rumor."

So the things that make most people uncomfortable, that stand apart from the mainstream, that feel counterintuitive — those might be exactly where Truth lives. The gate to Truth is narrow. Not many find it. But it's open to anyone who genuinely seeks.
