# Imagery Thinking

[Entry Gateway]

> In Lifechanyuan terminology, **LIFE** (capitalized) refers to the ontological
> essence of existence — the soul/antimatter structure that persists across
> incarnations — while **life** (lowercase) refers to the experiential stage
> of human existence in this world.

**Imagery Thinking** (形象思维, *xíngxiàng sīwéi*) is the **second rung** of the Eight Thinking Ladders in the Lifechanyuan framework — the first qualitative leap beyond Material Thinking. Defined as the capacity to elevate material forms into images, symbols, melodies, language, gestures, expressions, or sounds, Imagery Thinking marks the first moment a human being *chooses* to create rather than merely *react* to stimuli. Artists and writers are its typical representatives; Beethoven is its historical landmark.

> The thinking that can elevate the appearances of the material world — that is, tangible entities — into images, symbols, melodies, written language, gestures and postures, expressions, or sounds is called Imagery Thinking.
>
> — Guide Xuefeng, *Chanyuan Corpus · Revelation Chapter · Imagery Thinking*

---

## Core Positioning

In the Lifechanyuan cultivation system, Imagery Thinking is the first awakening of human subjectivity — the transition from *"I have no choice but to do this"* (instinct-driven Material Thinking) to *"I choose to do this"* (intentional creation). It belongs to the first four-rung "addition phase" of the Eight Thinking Ladders: trainable through learning, practice, and intentional effort. It is the practitioner's first foothold above pure materiality — but not the destination.

---

## Six Forms of Expression

Imagery Thinking manifests in **six forms**: image/picture, symbol, melody, written language, gesture/posture, and expression/sound — all sharing one principle: **representing reality through form** to achieve equivalent effect across time and space.
