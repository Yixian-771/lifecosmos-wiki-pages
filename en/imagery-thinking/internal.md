## I. Overview and Definition

Imagery Thinking is the second rung of the Eight Thinking Ladders, one level above Material Thinking. Imagery Thinking refers to the kind of thinking that can elevate the appearances of the material world — tangible entities — into images, symbols, melodies, written language, gestures and postures, expressions, or sounds. Imagery Thinking no longer stops at the direct surface perception of matter; instead, it moves from the concrete into the intangible, from the material world into the spiritual world, transcending the limitations of time and space, turning fleeting things, phenomena, or events into a relative form of permanence — and thus those who possess it live more freely than Material Thinkers. The process of artistic creation is the process of Imagery Thinking; artists and writers are its typical representatives.

**Core Definition I** (*Chanyuan Corpus · Revelation Chapter · Imagery Thinking*, complete original text):

> The thinking that can elevate the appearances of the material world — that is, tangible entities — into images, symbols, melodies, written language, gestures and postures, expressions, or sounds is called Imagery Thinking.

**Core Definition II** (*New Era Human 800 Concepts*, Article 87, complete original text):

> The eight rungs of thinking, in ascending order, are: Material Thinking, Imagery Thinking, Associative Thinking, Illusory Thinking, Heart-Image Thinking, Taiji Thinking, Non-Form Thinking, and Hundun Thinking.

**Core Definition III** (*Chanyuan Corpus · Cultivation Chapter · Non-Form Thinking (I)*, complete excerpt):

> Ordinary people's thinking is Material Thinking; artists' and writers' thinking is Imagery Thinking; scientists' thinking is Associative Thinking; most religious practitioners' thinking is Illusory Thinking; the thinking of shamans and those skilled in spiritual arts is Heart-Image Thinking; sages' thinking is Taiji Thinking; bodhisattvas' and Buddhas' thinking is Non-Form Thinking; gods' and the Greatest Creator's thinking is Hundun Thinking. The level of LIFE a person reaches depends on what kind of thinking they possess; or conversely, the kind of thinking one possesses determines the level of LIFE one attains.

**Core Definition IV** (*New Era Human 800 Concepts*, Article 666, complete original text):

> The highest realm of thinking is Hundun Thinking — the thinking of the Greatest Creator, beyond the imagination of ordinary people, and also the thinking of the divine. Sakyamuni attained the realm of Non-Form Thinking; Laozi attained the realm of Taiji Thinking; the thinking of celestials is Heart-Image Thinking; the thinking Zhuge Liang, Einstein, and others reached is Associative Thinking; the realm attained by artists such as Beethoven is Imagery Thinking; ordinary people's thinking is Material Thinking; religious practitioners possess Illusory Thinking.

**Core Definition V** (*Xuefeng Corpus · X04 Friendship Chapter · Tribute to Mr. Qingfeng Bi*, complete excerpt):

> Imagery Thinking: thinking that can elevate material forms into concepts, symbols, melodies, artistic shapes, and so forth is Imagery Thinking.

**Core Definition VI** (*Xuefeng Corpus · X09 Essays · I Hope This Doesn't Startle Everyone*, complete excerpt):

> The eight modes of thinking I have proposed — Material Thinking, Imagery Thinking, Associative Thinking, Illusory Thinking, Heart-Image Thinking, Taiji Thinking, Non-Form Thinking, and Hundun Thinking — are the eight evolutionary steps of LIFE, marking the watershed from an ordinary person to an artist, to a scientist, to a religious or political leader, to a cultivator, to a celestial, to a Buddha, to a god.

## II. The Six Forms of Imagery Thinking: Representing Reality Through Form

Imagery Thinking transforms material entities into freer modes of expression through six forms, allowing information to transcend temporal and spatial constraints and be transmitted and preserved.

(*Chanyuan Corpus · Revelation Chapter · Imagery Thinking*, complete original text, cited form by form):

**Form One: Image — Painting, Photography, Sculpture**

> Sketching, painting, photography, copying, rubbing, and sculpting all express physical objects through imagery. This kind of thinking activity is Imagery Thinking.

**Form Two: Symbol — Technical Drawing, Scores**

> Drafting — whether maps, transistor circuit diagrams, architectural blueprints, process flow charts, mechanical structure diagrams, assembly diagrams, meridian charts, simplified musical notation, or staff notation — elevates physical entities into symbols. This kind of thinking is Imagery Thinking.

**Form Three: Melody — Musical Works**

> Musical compositions such as *Spring River Flowers Moonlit Night*, *The Moon Reflected on Second Springs*, and the violin concerto *Butterfly Lovers* express natural scenery or human stories through undulating, rhythmic melodies to achieve a form of spiritual enjoyment. This too belongs to Imagery Thinking.

**Form Four: Written Language — Literary Works, Pictographic Characters**

> The evolution of Chinese written language is the result of Imagery Thinking. Our characters began as pictographs — symbolic representations of physical objects. Even today we can glimpse the original forms: 月 (moon), 山 (mountain), 石 (stone), 田 (field), 水 (water).
>
> When we read *Romance of the Three Kingdoms*, *Water Margin*, *Journey to the West*, *Dream of the Red Chamber*, or *Investiture of the Gods*, we can clearly "see" vivid, lifelike characters through the language — as if they could leap off the page. This too is the result of the author's Imagery Thinking; the author has transformed real human figures into images through written language.

**Form Five: Gesture and Posture — Bodily Imitation, Qigong Forms**

> *Five Animal Frolics* (Wuqin Xi), the health-strengthening exercise created by the renowned physician Hua Tuo, transforms the specific forms of animals into postures and gestures through imitation. This too is a result of Imagery Thinking.

**Form Six: Expression and Sound — Drama, Mimicry**

> In all forms of drama — whether Shakespeare's or Lao She's — the actors portraying characters are not expressing their own genuine emotions. They become vessels for the characters they portray, giving image-form to the joy, anger, love, grief, and longing of those characters. This also belongs to Imagery Thinking.
>
> People who imitate the roar of dragons, the howl of tigers, the croaking of frogs, the barking of dogs, the shouts of men, the neighing of horses, the chirping of insects, or the howl of wolves are also practicing Imagery Thinking.

## III. The Core Essence of Imagery Thinking: Representing Reality Through Form to Achieve Equivalent Effect

(*Chanyuan Corpus · Revelation Chapter · Imagery Thinking*, complete original text):

> Imagery Thinking is a mode of thinking that replaces physical objects with images in order to convey an equivalent effect. Sending a photograph to a loved one far away — the person hasn't gone, yet the gesture brings comfort across the distance.
>
> The landscapes and people that appear on a television screen are all virtual images — yet their effect is nearly indistinguishable from reality. When we watch television, we grow excited, joyful, angry, anxious, tearful, laughing, loud, and vocal along with what changes on the screen. This is the effect produced by Imagery Thinking.

## IV. Imagery Thinking vs. Material Thinking: From "I Have No Choice" to "I Choose This"

This is the first qualitative leap in the entire Eight Thinking Ladders — Imagery Thinking is when one first consciously activates the mind, first enters the spiritual world, first transcends time and space.

(*Chanyuan Corpus · Revelation Chapter · Imagery Thinking*, complete original text):

> Material Thinking is direct, one-sided, and literal: 1 is 1, 2 is 2. The subject relies mainly on instinctive impulse — wind blows and the branches sway, cold creeps in and one puts on a padded coat, hunger comes and one finds food, desire stirs and one acts. No mental effort is required; it is "I have no choice but to do this." Their daily activities are mechanical, passive, done out of necessity — often self-loathing, repelled, contrary to personal will. They are locked firmly within a limited space or domain, repeating day after day — even throughout an entire lifetime — the uncreative, non-independent, non-autonomous activities of themselves and their ancestors. But Imagery Thinkers are different: they engage their minds. Their thinking is not "I have no choice but to do this" but rather "I choose to do this." They move from the concrete into the intangible, from the material world into the spiritual world, transcending the limitations of time and space, turning fleeting things, phenomena, or events into a relative form of permanence — and thus live more freely than the former.

## VII. Imagery Thinking vs. Associative Thinking: The Limitation of the Imagery Thinker

Although Imagery Thinking is one rung above Material Thinking, compared to Associative Thinking it still has practical limitations — imagery cannot replace physical objects in solving actual problems.

(*Chanyuan Corpus · Revelation Chapter · Associative Thinking*, complete excerpt):

> For Lifechanyuan fellows, I hope you do not confine your thinking to Associative Thinking, because even Associative Thinking is a low-level mode. It is like transmitting intelligence across the two shores of the Atlantic: the Material Thinker builds ships and sends people; the Imagery Thinker lays undersea cables; the Associative Thinker uses radio waves; and our method is…
>
> The Imagery Thinker simply cannot manage it. From an artistic perspective, the Imagery Thinker is greater than the Material Thinker — but from a practical perspective, when one is hungry, a steamed bun works; a picture of a steamed bun, however lifelike, cannot solve an actual problem.

## XIV. The Position of Imagery Thinking in the Lifechanyuan Theoretical System

**First**, Imagery Thinking is the second rung of the Eight Thinking Ladders — the first qualitative leap in human thinking history from "instinct-driven" to "active creation." Imagery Thinkers are the first to consciously engage their minds, the first to enter the spiritual world, the first to transform tangible entities into relatively permanent spiritual images. (*Chanyuan Corpus · Revelation Chapter · Imagery Thinking*)

**Second**, Imagery Thinking is an essential component of the "first four-rung addition phase" — Material + Imagery + Associative + Illusory Thinking constitute the addition phase, purely products of the brain, achievable through learning and training. Imagery Thinking is the typical level for the artist community and corresponds to the establishment of humanity's entire spiritual civilization: art, literature, music, drama. (Qifeng Grass's teaching)

**Third**, Imagery Thinking is a crucial coordinate in the thinking-ladder system — within the Eight Thinking route map, Imagery Thinking is precisely mapped to the artist level (landmark figure: Beethoven), giving Chanyuan Celestials a clear marker of their current position so that cultivation is no longer directionless. (Article 666; *The Thinking Ladder of Humanity*)

**Fourth**, Imagery Thinking's limitations make further elevation necessary — imagery can transcend matter, but "a picture of a steamed bun cannot solve an actual problem"; imagery is a permanent imprint but not the cosmic source; a practitioner who remains at Imagery Thinking is still caught in the Thinking Formation and cannot truly enter the spiritual levels above Heart-Image Thinking. (*Associative Thinking*; Article 455)

**Fifth**, Civilization 3.0 will completely liberate the value of Imagery Thinking — Articles 677 and 638 clearly state: in the era of Civilization 3.0, art is completely liberated; in the Second Home, art and labor go hand in hand; everyone can become an artist — this is the ultimate cosmic destiny of Imagery Thinking, and the most beautiful life vision for all carbon-based loved ones living at the level of Imagery Thinking.
