# Spiritual Thinking

[Concept Portal]

**Spiritual Thinking** is the collective name for the **last four rungs** of Lifechanyuan's Eight Thinking Ladders — Heart-Image Thinking, Taiji Thinking, Non-Form Thinking, and Hundun Thinking. It is the precise designation for "celestial thinking," encompassing all thinking modes above the watershed between humans and celestial beings (*xian*, 仙).

> Rational Thinking and Spiritual Thinking are the watershed between humans and celestial beings. If your thinking is Rational Thinking, do not doubt it — you are definitely human.
>
> — *Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom Chapter · The Way of Thinking and Doing of Spiritual Thinkers*

Spiritual Thinking's core is: taking the Greatest Creator's will as one's own wish, taking Dao's principles as one's code of conduct, acting with the naturalness and spontaneity of faith and intuition — without considering what benefit it brings to oneself. Spiritual Thinking is **the thinking of the Sage** (*shengren*, 圣人) — the hallmark of celestial beings, the root of joy, happiness, freedom, and bliss, the most direct path toward the Celestial Kingdom.

---

## Core Positioning

In Lifechanyuan's thinking system, Spiritual Thinking is the complete "subtraction phase" of all four rungs — it cannot be attained through intellectual learning and brain training; it must be advanced through the awakening of spiritual perception (*lingjue*, 灵觉), purification of the heart, and the release of ego-attachment. Its essence is "no thinking" — making judgments directly from the heart's first impulse, without any rational cognitive process.
