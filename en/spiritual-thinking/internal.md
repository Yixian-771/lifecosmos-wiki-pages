## I. Overview and Definition

Spiritual Thinking is the collective name for the four rungs of Heart-Image Thinking, Taiji Thinking, Non-Form Thinking, and Hundun Thinking. It is the collective name for "celestial thinking" and encompasses all thinking modes above the watershed between humans and celestial beings formed by Rational Thinking (first four rungs) and Spiritual Thinking (last four rungs). Spiritual Thinking's core is: taking the Greatest Creator's will as one's own wish, taking Dao's principles as one's code of conduct, acting with the naturalness and spontaneity of faith and intuition — without considering what benefit it brings to oneself. Spiritual Thinking is the thinking of the Sage — the hallmark of celestial beings, the root of joy, happiness, freedom, and bliss, the most direct path toward the Celestial Kingdom, the thinking-level guarantee for LIFE quality ascending to higher-dimensional space.

**Core Definition I** (*Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom Chapter · The Way of Thinking and Doing of Spiritual Thinkers*, complete original text):

> Material Thinking, Imagery Thinking, Associative Thinking, and Illusory Thinking all belong to Rational Thinking — this is human thinking. Heart-Image Thinking, Taiji Thinking, Non-Form Thinking, and Hundun Thinking all belong to celestial thinking. In other words, Rational Thinking and Spiritual Thinking are the watershed between humans and celestial beings. If your thinking is Rational Thinking, do not doubt it — you are definitely human.

**Core Definition II** (*Chanyuan Corpus · Cultivation of Immortality Chapter · A Brief Introduction to the Key Points of Spiritual Thinking*, complete original text excerpt):

> Rational Thinking is the hallmark of the Worthy; Spiritual Thinking is the hallmark of the celestial.

**Core Definition III** (*Chanyuan Corpus · Human Life Chapter · A Spiritual Life Is Higher Than a Rational Life*, complete original text):

> The core of rationality is calculation through logical reasoning based on knowledge and evidence; the core of spirituality is the naturalness and spontaneity based on faith and intuition.

**Core Definition IV** (*Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom Chapter · The Way of Thinking and Doing of Spiritual Thinkers*, complete original text):

> To put it plainly: if the starting point of our thinking about problems comes from material, imagery, associative, or illusory angles — if what we focus on is how to solve food, clothing, shelter, transportation, birth, aging, illness, and death; how to ensure safety; how to gain respect, dignity, and a good reputation; how to live in a way that makes people envious or even worshipful; how to navigate with ease through complex social strata and interpersonal relationships — this is Rational Thinking, the thinking of humans. ## IV. The Way of Thinking and Doing of Spiritual Thinkers: Three Great Differences

(*Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom Chapter · The Way of Thinking and Doing of Spiritual Thinkers*, complete original text):

**Difference I: Macro vs. Micro**

> Spiritual thinkers first grasp the macro; Rational thinkers grasp the micro. For example, in finding a job: Spiritual thinkers focus on whether the work brings joy and happiness; Rational thinkers focus on whether the salary is high, whether the environment and conditions are good, what the prospects for development are. Why is this? Because Spiritual thinkers consider the direction of their LIFE — whether they can ultimately reach heaven; as long as what they are doing is inconsistent with their destination, they are unwilling to do it. Whereas Rational thinkers consider whether the work can resolve current living needs; they do not think about whether what they are currently doing will affect their ultimate goal. In this sense, Spiritual thinkers consider how to live; Rational thinkers consider how to survive.

**Difference II: LIFE vs. Life**

> Spiritual thinkers consider LIFE; Rational thinkers consider life. Those who consider LIFE must understand the principles, mysteries, and relationship with time and space of LIFE. Those who consider life only need to learn the knowledge and experience accumulated by humanity and understand the operating laws of human society.

**Difference III: Simplification vs. Complication**

> Spiritual thinkers simplify complex matters to the greatest extent; Rational thinkers always complicate simple matters as much as possible.
