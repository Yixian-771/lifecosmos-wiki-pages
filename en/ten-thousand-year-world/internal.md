# the Ten-thousand-year World (Internal English Version)

**Entry Type:** Internal English Version · Lifechanyuan Encyclopedia  
**Chinese Entry Name:** 万年界  
**Version:** v1.0  
**Compiled by:** Lingzhou Grass  
**Date:** 2026-03-08

## 1. Core Definition
In the distant space about 3,480 light-years from Earth, there is a beautiful planet. It is 16 times larger than Earth and has 16 equally spaced suns. About 100 million Celestial Beings live there. Their basic characteristics are close to what Daoist descriptions call terrestrial immortals, and their lifespan is about 35,000 years relative to the human world. This is the Ten-thousand-year World of Heaven, and one of the cultivation goals Chanyuan Celestials look up to and long for.

Like the Thousand-year World, the Ten-thousand-year World belongs to the positive universe. It is an objective existence that is visible and tangible, and all things there have lifespans. It is neither fantasy nor metaphor, but a necessity generated by the law that the sum of positive and negative energy equals zero—just as the solar system necessarily has planets like Mercury, Saturn, and Mars.

## 2. Position of the Ten-thousand-year World in the Cosmic System
Guide Xuefeng described 20 parallel worlds in the universe. In this coordinate system, the human world is the xy world, the Thousand-year World is in the xy-z world, the Ten-thousand-year World is in the x-y-z world, and the Celestial Islands Continent is in the xyz world. Layer by layer, each world corresponds to a level of LIFE structure.

Heaven is the collective name for the Thousand-year World, the Ten-thousand-year World, the Elysium World, and the Celestial Islands Continent within it. The Ten-thousand-year World is in the middle layer—above the Thousand-year World and below the Elysium World. The Guide used a cosmic-velocity analogy: reaching the Thousand-year World requires the first cosmic velocity; reaching the Ten-thousand-year World requires the second; reaching the Elysium World requires the third. Without that velocity, one absolutely cannot go. (Here, “cosmic velocity” is the Guide’s analogical expression, not a physics definition.)

## 3. Natural Environment and Life Appearance of the Ten-thousand-year World
Because it has 16 stars with different densities arranged at equal distances, the Ten-thousand-year World is divided into 16 regions with different luminosities, including one permanent night region. This night region contains countless gemstones of many forms that shine in darkness. Celestial Beings in the Ten-thousand-year World collect gemstones there to decorate their caves—these stones can illuminate dwellings, absorb subtle essence, and make the cave realm colorful and dreamlike.

Diet varies with cultivation depth. Newly arrived Celestial Beings eat harder fruits such as walnuts and dates; after ten thousand years, they eat softer fruits such as peaches and grapes; after twenty thousand years, they no longer eat fruits and drink only mineral-rich spring water; after thirty thousand years, they no longer consume any material food and directly absorb the radiance of sun and moon and the subtle qi of heaven and earth—this is the meaning of “those who feed on qi become spirit-like.”

Flight is an important ability in the Ten-thousand-year World and also requires long cultivation. New arrivals cannot fly and can only walk on land. After about ten thousand years of practice, they can float three feet above ground; after about twenty thousand years, they can fly over high mountains; to fly at 80,000 meters altitude, thirty thousand years of cultivation are required.

Its magnificence far exceeds human imagination: the Cangting Sea with vast blue waves, the Moyun mountain range stretching endlessly, the main peak Tongtian Peak rising to 90,000 meters, and Wangtian Cliff where one can look up and see sun, moon, and stars, and look down at cloud seas and billows. All of this is the shore Chanyuan Celestials long for in cultivation.

## 4. Signs and Standards for Going to the Ten-thousand-year World
How can one know whether one qualifies for the Ten-thousand-year World? Guide Xuefeng gave two dimensions of standards.

**Dreams are the first verification window.** The more perfect the LIFE nonmaterial structure is, the more beautiful and exquisite dreams become. Places unreachable in dreams are unreachable after death. Regarding dream signs for going to the Ten-thousand-year World, the Guide said:

> “If you often dream of strange and beautiful scenery, often fly, often feel exquisite joy in spirit-heart-body communion with the opposite sex, and have no fear or anxiety at all, this is a sign that you can go to the Ten-thousand-year World.”

**Consciousness state is the second verification dimension.** The Guide’s criterion of transformation includes: ordered consciousness, perfected LIFE structure, Celestial Being characteristics, and flight ability. He also stated: if one often flies freely in dreams and remains joyful without sexual contact, one will go to the Ten-thousand-year World in the next life.

## 5. Relationship Between the Ten-thousand-year World and practice/cultivation
The core of practice and cultivation is to refine and perfect one’s LIFE nonmaterial structure so that it gradually evolves toward the LIFE structure of the Ten-thousand-year World and even higher heavens. The three required tasks from human to Celestial Being/Buddha—repay debts and conclude earthly ties; contribute and accumulate merit; refine and perfect LIFE’s nonmaterial structure—each accumulates qualification for entering higher heavens.

Time also has different meaning there. One day in heaven equals one year on Earth. Different spacetime means different metabolism and life cycles. In the human world, life length is only about eighty years; in the Thousand-year World, one thousand years; in the Ten-thousand-year World, over thirty-five thousand years; in the Celestial Islands Continent of the Elysium World, life length is unlimited. This is why people long for heavenly life: the environment is better and life is longer.

## 6. Sitting Transformation and Reincarnation Destinations of Ten-thousand-year World Celestial Beings
Celestial Beings in the Ten-thousand-year World are different from humans: their departure is not death, but sitting transformation.

> “The passing of Celestial Beings is all through sitting transformation. After death, the remains quickly turn into a stream of blue smoke and disperse. The living Celestial Beings do not feel sorrow because of such passing.”

After sitting transformation, destination is determined by the consciousness state cultivated during life there:

> “Celestial Beings who pass generally go to four worlds:
> Those who did not move even a trace of worldly thought go to the Elysium World;
> Those who had ordinary thoughts, after sitting transformation, that blue smoke gathers again into form and starts another round of life in the Ten-thousand-year World;
> Those who frequently generated worldly thoughts descend to the Thousand-year World;
> Those who had worldly thoughts, were moved by them, and put some into action will reincarnate into the human world.”

“Worldly thought / ordinary thought / dust-thought” refers to:

> “Whoever envies worldly glory, wealth, and power; likes family life; likes intimate union with the opposite sex; wants private property; or harbors sorrow, resentment, jealousy, and similar thoughts—this counts as moving worldly thought.”

These four destinations define the clear position of the Ten-thousand-year World in the cosmic reincarnation system of LIFE: upward to the Elysium World, downward to the Thousand-year World or even the human world. The ultimate goal of Ten-thousand-year World Celestial Beings is complete purity of thought, so that at sitting transformation no worldly thought moves, and one rises to the Elysium World.

## 7. Hierarchical Position of the Ten-thousand-year World in the Heaven System
Heaven consists of three layers: the Thousand-year World (a material planet about 960 light-years from Earth), the Ten-thousand-year World (a material planet about 3,480 light-years from Earth), and the Elysium World (the whole terrestrial universe in this framework). The Thousand-year World and Ten-thousand-year World belong to the positive universe and are tangible existences; the Elysium World belongs to negative-universe space, where all is transformation, time nearly disappears, and all existence abides by essence rather than form.

The Ten-thousand-year World is the middle layer of Heaven. Celestial Beings spend long ages there, cultivating flight, refining essence into qi, and refining spirit into emptiness. When cultivation reaches the required state, one can look toward the Celestial Islands Continent of the Elysium World—the back garden of the Greatest Creator, with 80 billion islands, each inhabited by one Super Celestial Being, one of the most beautiful final destinations LIFE can reach.
