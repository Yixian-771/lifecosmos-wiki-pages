# Repentance — Friendly Version

## That Heavy Feeling After You've Done Something Wrong

You said something you shouldn't have. You acted in a way that wasn't right. You apologized to the person involved, time passed, and yet — something still lingers. A weight. A quiet discomfort that won't quite leave.

The Lifechanyuan system has a name for what that feeling is asking for: **repentance**.

---

## What Is Repentance?

In the Lifechanyuan system, repentance has a clear and specific meaning:

> **Repentance is a private matter between a person and the Greatest Creator — the Creator.**

Not a confession to a priest. Not a public admission in front of a crowd. Not a social media post. Just you, and the Greatest Creator, in silence — communicating through the inner Spirit.

The essence of repentance is recognizing that you've deviated from the Greatest Creator's will, honestly acknowledging it in your heart, and committing to change.

---

## Why Repent?

Here's an analogy that makes it immediately clear:

> Repentance is of the same nature as the daily act of washing your face, brushing your teeth, washing your clothes, bathing, and cleaning your room.

Our bodies accumulate sweat and dust every day — so we clean them every day. Our inner Spirits accumulate the residue of mistakes, misjudgments, and misdeeds. If we don't clear them out, they build up.

Repentance is daily hygiene for the soul.

---

## What Does Repentance Actually Do?

The Lifechanyuan system identifies four concrete effects:

**1. It purifies the inner Spirit and elevates your LIFE**

> "Repentance can purify the inner Spirit, can elevate the quality of LIFE, can make a person tranquil and harmonious, can bring a person closer to the Greatest Creator, and can make the path of one's life relatively smooth and auspicious."

And the boldest claim:

> "Repentance is the necessary condition for the elevation of LIFE quality. Without repentance, there is no elevation."

**2. It clears karma and breaks you out of stuck situations**

The Lifechanyuan teaching uses a farming metaphor: karma is like a seed. Once planted, it will grow, flower, and bear fruit — whether you like it or not. But you can dig up the seed before it does.

> "Repentance serves precisely the function of digging up and pulling out."

And the direct practical promise:

> "The primary method for escaping hardship is repentance."

**3. It opens a direct line to the Greatest Creator**

The universe is holographic — the Spirit-Force of the Greatest Creator permeates all spaces. Every thought you have is already connected to the Greatest Creator. When you repent:

> "Repentance and prayer are in fact acts of communication and exchange with the Greatest Creator. Our thought-waves spread like a broadcasting transmitter into the vast negative-universe space; divine beings and Buddhas can sense them and will come to our aid effectively."

**4. You will be forgiven**

> "As long as one repents, the Greatest Creator will certainly forgive, and will certainly love the one who repents."

> "Before the Greatest Creator, there is no transgression or wrongdoing that cannot be forgiven."

The ultimate stakes:

> "Heaven belongs to those who have erred or committed wrongdoing and repent devoutly. Hell belongs to those who have erred or committed wrongdoing and refuse to repent."

---

## How to Repent

The method is simple. The requirements are real.

**Who do you repent to?**
Only the Greatest Creator. You can apologize to other people — but you repent only to the Greatest Creator. No priest, no teacher, no guru has the authority to forgive your transgressions.

**How?**
Through the inner Spirit alone. No writing, no voice, no audience.

> "Any form of repentance not conducted through the inner Spirit is performed for others to see, not directed at the Greatest Creator, and the Greatest Creator will not accept such repentance."

**Where?**
Anywhere quiet. Your room. A forest. A field by the water. Gently close your eyes, calm your Heart, and communicate honestly with the Greatest Creator — acknowledge your transgressions, express your genuine intention to change, and ask for forgiveness.

**The most important part: follow through**

> "After repentance, one must take action — that is, concretely improve in the practice of daily life. If one repents every day but continues as before every day, repentance has little meaning."

Repentance is the beginning of change, not a substitute for it.

---

## What Repentance Is Not

**Not a performance**: There's no audience. Don't make it visible to others.

**Not an excuse**: Never let repentance become a way of explaining yourself or avoiding responsibility.

**Not an obsession**: Someone once asked whether repenting daily might itself become an "attachment." The answer: calling repentance an attachment is "the equivalent of calling daily face-washing and tooth-brushing an attachment — a misuse of the concept."

---

## One Line to Remember

**Repentance is a private conversation with the Greatest Creator — honest, silent, and followed by genuine change. It is the first step out of hardship and the necessary door to LIFE elevation.**
