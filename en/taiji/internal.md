## I. Overview and Definition

Taiji is one of the most central concepts in the Lifechanyuan cosmological system. It is the first form of existence the universe took after the Greatest Creator was born, the turning point at which the universe moved from disorder to order, and the cosmological foundation of the entire theoretical architecture of Lifechanyuan. Taiji is the Greatest Creator — the source and sovereign of all phenomena in the universe.

Core definition (New Era Human 800 Concepts, Article 503, full original):

> Wuji gives rise to Taiji; Taiji is the Greatest Creator. Taiji arises naturally; therefore nature is the Greatest Creator.

*Chanyuan Anthology · Eight Characteristics of God 1* (full original):

> "Taiji" is the Greatest Creator, and the Greatest Creator is "Taiji." "Wuji gives rise to Taiji, Taiji gives rise to Two Principles (Liangyi)." All phenomena of the universe come from the Two Principles; the Two Principles are in oppositional unity; the Two Principles come from "Taiji"; "Taiji" is unique and singular — it is not in oppositional unity. Therefore, the Greatest Creator is one. The Greatest Creator has no opposite.

**The multi-layered expressions of Taiji:**

- First layer — Taiji is the spirit of the Greatest Creator. Before the celestial universe appeared, a "thing" formed first; this "thing" is "Taiji," the spirit of the Greatest Creator. (*Chanyuan Anthology · Universe-Time-Space Section · The Origin of the Universe*)

- Third layer — Taiji is Tathagata. What is called Dao is Tathagata; what is called Tathagata is Dao. Taiji, Tathagata, and Dao are different linguistic expressions of the same ontological entity.
- Fourth layer — Taiji is nature. Taiji arises naturally; therefore nature is the Greatest Creator. (Article 503)
- Fifth layer — Taiji is the cosmic One. The meaning of Dao in brief is principle, order, and 1; hundun contains Taiji — the "one" of oppositional unity, with two poles and a middle way. (Article 417)
