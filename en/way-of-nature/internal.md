## II. Reinterpreting "Dao Follows Nature" (*Dào fǎ zìrán*)

**Xuefeng Corpus · Admonitory Essays · On the Mistaken Understanding of "Dao Follows Nature"**

In Chapter 25 of the *Tao Te Ching*, Laozi wrote: "Man follows Earth, Earth follows Heaven, Heaven follows the Tao, the Tao follows nature (*zìrán*)." Countless interpretations and commentaries have been written about this passage, and having surveyed them broadly, I find that the overwhelming majority are mistaken.

Where does the error lie? It lies in treating the character *fǎ* (法) as a verb meaning "to emulate, to follow, to obey," reading "Tao follows nature" as "the Tao emulates nature" or "the Tao is subordinate to nature."

What is the Tao? The Tao is the consciousness of the Greatest Creator.

What is nature (*zìrán*)? Nature is the laws and patterns through which all things and all phenomena operate and change.

The relationship between the Tao and nature is like that between the brain and the body's organs. To say "the Tao emulates nature" would be like saying the brain must operate according to the demands of the hands and feet — in other words, that the brain should take orders from the limbs rather than the limbs obeying the brain.

The correct reading is not a verb-object construction but a predicate structure: "Man's law (*rén fǎ*) — Earth; Earth's law — Heaven; Heaven's law — the Tao; the Tao's law — nature." This means: what man does is revealed by Earth; what happens on Earth is revealed by Heaven; what Heaven does is revealed by the Tao; how the Tao operates is revealed through nature.

"The Tao's law — nature." The state of nature *is* the content of the Tao. Whatever accords with nature belongs to the Tao; whatever departs from nature falls outside the Tao.

A human being who eats, drinks, sleeps, and loves — that accords with the Tao. A human being who refuses to eat, drink, sleep, or love — that departs from the Tao.

When all things proceed according to natural law, there is harmony and abundance. When something departs from natural law, calamity and suffering follow. A naturally raised chicken lays eggs worth more at market; its meat tastes richer. Why? Because it grew according to natural laws — while the factory-farmed chicken was artificially forced to grow faster.

Cultivation works the same way. Strictly following natural law in cultivation yields authentic fruit; departing from it yields warped results.

The Way of the Greatest Creator is the Way of Nature. Why do so many people who have devoted enormous effort to seeking the Tao or seeking Buddha ultimately fail to attain it? Because they did not strictly follow the Way of Nature and objective law.

Laozi said: "The great Tao is level and smooth, but the people love shortcuts." The true method for attaining the Tao is actually simple — just proceed according to natural law. Those who seek exotic techniques are loving shortcuts, and naturally they cannot enter the Tao.

Everything in Lifechanyuan proceeds strictly according to the teachings of Jesus Christ, Buddha Shakyamuni, and Immortal Laozi — following the Way of the Greatest Creator and natural law. What has been the result? Those Chanyuan Celestials who have sincerely walked the Guide's Route Map according to Lifechanyuan's principles should know clearly: are you not approaching the state of Celestials? The fulfillment of your aspirations and ideals is within sight.

Remember: "The Tao's law — nature."
