## [I] The Nature of the Inner Spirit — Definition and Structure

The inner Spirit is the union of Heart and Spirit-Force.

On the "Heart": The Heart is a creation of heaven and earth; in essence, the Heart is empty. The Heart is the mirror that reflects all phenomena of the great universe — one can say it is a mirror that reflects the ten thousand things. In ordinary circumstances, the Heart follows conditions: when a condition arises, the Heart arises; when a condition ceases, the Heart ceases. A Heart that has attachments is an ordinary Heart; a Heart with no attachments is the Buddha Heart. (Source: Xuefeng Corpus · Essays · *The Spiritual World and the Inner-Spirit World*)

The capacity of the Heart is determined by the magnitude of the Spirit-Force that dwells within it: when the Spirit-Force of the Greatest Creator dwells in the Heart, the Heart becomes the entire universe; when a divine Spirit-Force dwells in the Heart, the Heart possesses half of Taiji; when a Buddha's Spirit-Force dwells in the Heart, the Heart encompasses the entire nonmaterial world; when a celestial's Spirit-Force dwells in the Heart, the Heart holds all the high-dimensional spaces of LIFE… When the Spirit-Force of an ordinary person dwells in the Heart, money, power, status, fame, lust, resentment, and hatred fill the Heart; when the Spirit-Force of a muddled person dwells in the Heart, the Heart desires nothing but the instincts of eating, wearing, dwelling, traveling, and relieving itself. (Source: Chanyuan Corpus · Cultivation Practice · *Heart, Inner Spirit, Thinking, Consciousness*)

On the "Spirit-Force": The Spirit-Force is the consciousness of the Greatest Creator, the very source of LIFE, pervading all time and space in the universe. (Source: Xuefeng Corpus · Essays · *The Spiritual World and the Inner-Spirit World*) The Spirit-Force is like a great transmitter, continuously broadcasting information from the Divine to every space and corner of the universe; the Heart is like a small receiver, capable of continuously receiving messages sent by the cosmic Spirit-Force. But if the Heart is polluted and impure — like a rusted receiver — it will be unable to receive the instructions from the Spirit-Force. (Source: Chanyuan Corpus · Cultivation Practice · *Intermediate Cultivation*)

The inner Spirit is "the information entering and exiting the space of thinking." (Source: Chanyuan Corpus · Cultivation Practice · *Heart, Inner Spirit, Thinking, Consciousness*) What is ordinarily called the inner Spirit refers to the information entering and exiting the "Heart" of LIFE. (Source: Xuefeng Corpus · Q&A · *The Principle of Telepathy*)

There is another concise definition of the inner Spirit: The meaning of the inner Spirit is a state of clarity and joy experienced when a person's consciousness resonates in unison with the consciousness of the Greatest Creator. (Source: Xuefeng Corpus · Satire · *Finding Fault with "Farewell Samsara"*)

The inner Spirit is a Heart that possesses spiritual nature. When a person's Heart yearns for Truth, Goodness, and Beauty, the Heart and the Spirit-Force can immediately resonate and vibrate together; by yearning for Truth, Goodness, and Beauty over a long period, one can attain a perfect inner Spirit. A perfect inner Spirit enables one's thinking and consciousness to commune with the thinking and consciousness of the negative universe — you are the universe, and the universe is you — and spiritual illumination will arise at every moment, leading to the unity of Heaven and humanity. (Source: Chanyuan Corpus · Cultivation Practice · *VI. Intermediate Cultivation*)

## [II] Distinguishing the Inner Spirit from the Spirit — Consciousness vs. Energy

The Spirit and the inner Spirit are two different concepts. Their common ground is that both belong to the nonmaterial world and share nonmaterial attributes; their difference is: the Spirit belongs to the realm of energy, while the inner Spirit belongs to the realm of consciousness.

Expressions such as "a beautiful inner Spirit," "a rich inner Spirit," "an ugly inner Spirit," and "a dull inner Spirit" describe states of consciousness. Expressions such as "full of spirit," "invigorated spirit," "vigorous spirit," and "listless spirit" describe states of energy. (Source: Xuefeng Corpus · Essays · *The Spiritual World and the Inner-Spirit World*)

The Spirit embodies the energy characteristics of LIFE; the inner Spirit embodies the structural characteristics of LIFE. (Source: Xuefeng Corpus · Q&A · *The Difference Between Spiritual Wealth and Inner-Spirit Wealth*)

The inner Spirit is the root; the Spirit is a kind of vitality that grows from the root. The quality of the inner Spirit determines the quality of the Spirit. (Source: Xuefeng Corpus · Warnings · *Hoping Governments of All Nations Will Consider Humanity's Inner-Spirit Construction*)

Material, spiritual, and inner-Spirit wealth can all be seen as forms of life's riches: a person who has abundant material wealth but is poor in spirit and inner Spirit is like a walking corpse; a person who has abundant spiritual wealth but is poor in material and inner-Spirit wealth is like a ghost or specter; a person who has abundant inner-Spirit wealth but is poor in material and spiritual wealth is like a demonic creature. Only when all three are present — material, spiritual, and inner-Spirit — can a person be called truly complete. (Source: Xuefeng Corpus · Essays · *The Spiritual World and the Inner-Spirit World*)
