# New Era Consciousness (Friendly Version)

## Are You Dreaming — or Awake?

Imagine you've lived your entire life in a sealed room. You've never seen what's beyond the walls. You assume the room is everything. You assume what you see is real.

New Era Consciousness wants to open a window — or better yet, knock down the wall.

> Our souls are in a state of slumber in most circumstances. What we consider the real world is actually a dream… New Era Consciousness asks us to awaken from this slumber, enter a more genuinely real reality, and enjoy a life that is freer, more comfortable, and free from fear.
>
> — Xuefeng, *Fully Embrace New Era Consciousness*

## Where Does New Era Consciousness Come From?

Throughout history, there have been many pioneers of inner exploration — the Seth Material, Orin, *The Law of One*, *Conversations with God*, *A Course in Miracles*. Each opened a different door into the nature of consciousness, the soul, and the cosmos. Together they form the source material for New Era Consciousness, spanning science, philosophy, psychology, spirituality, cosmology, and religion.

Life Chanyuan strings all these "pearls" together with one thread: **the path of the Greatest Creator**.

## What Does New Era Consciousness Actually Say?

Here are the core ideas in plain language:

**1. Consciousness is the foundation of everything**
The universe is built of consciousness, energy, and structure. The world you see is a projection of your own consciousness. Change your consciousness — change your world.

**2. Life never ends**
The soul is eternal. What we call "death" is simply the start of a new journey, not an ending. Heaven is real — it consists of the Thousand-Year World, the Ten-Thousand-Year World, and the Elysium World.

**3. You create your own destiny**
No blaming circumstances, other people, or fate. Your future is shaped by the quality of your inner life — your antimatter structure. You are responsible for all of it.

**4. Love is the master key**
Love purifies the soul and perfects life's structure. Love yourself, love others, love nature, love the Greatest Creator. Give love freely, feel grateful for everything — that's the key to joy.

**5. Joy and growth aren't opposites**
Pursuing health, freedom, and material wellbeing doesn't conflict with spiritual growth. Human nature doesn't need to be suppressed. Follow your nature — and direct it upward.

## 18 Things to Unlearn

Xuefeng laid out 18 specific shifts in consciousness needed to enter the "new homeland." A few examples:

- **Stop**: believing the universe is random and godless. **Start**: walking the path of the Greatest Creator.
- **Stop**: thinking "the world makes me who I am." **Start**: knowing "my consciousness shapes my world."
- **Stop**: clinging to family, party, religion, or nation as ultimate identities. **Start**: thinking of yourself as a citizen of a global new homeland.
- **Stop**: fearing death as the end. **Start**: understanding it as the beginning of a new journey.
- **Stop**: trying to get as much as possible. **Start**: giving more, receiving less, accumulating merit.

Think of it like preparing for a long journey. You need a passport, a visa, the right luggage. Entering the new homeland requires a new kind of inner preparation.

## The Infant Who Won't Grow Up

New Era Consciousness has a striking social insight: most adults are still emotionally infants.

As children we clung to our mothers for safety and nourishment. As we grew, we transferred that clinging to other "mothers" — the nation, the religion, the political party, the family unit. These institutions give us a sense of security and belonging.

But New Era Consciousness challenges this:

> New Era Consciousness requires people to leave the infant stage. Those who cannot leave infant-stage thinking have no connection with heaven.
>
> — *The Infant Who Cannot Grow Up*, Chanyuan Corpus

Outgrowing these attachments isn't coldness or ingratitude. It's the maturity required to enter a freer, higher life.

## The Internet Was Made for This

Long before the age of social media and AI, Xuefeng wrote:

> The computer network is a vast reservoir of resources and energy. Its significance and value will far exceed the combined value of all earth's coal, oil, and mineral resources.

And:

> Networking combined with New Era Consciousness is a catalyst for transforming human destiny.

The point: the fastest, most efficient way to spread consciousness change has always been through open networks — not temples, not political movements, not institutions.

## Western New Age Is a Waypoint, Not the Destination

Books like Seth, *A Course in Miracles*, and *Conversations with God* are genuinely valuable — but they're not the end of the road:

> Western New Era consciousness is only humanity's intermediate wisdom — definitely not higher wisdom.

Buddhism points to the riverbank but isn't the crossing itself. Western New Age opens doors but doesn't reveal the final corridor.

Life Chanyuan describes itself as humanity's "last course" — the destination that all other spiritual paths are preparing for.

> If one clings to the old ways, one will inevitably be eliminated by the New Era — just as television stations and newspapers are being gradually eliminated.
