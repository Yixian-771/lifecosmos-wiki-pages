## I. Definition and Mathematical Derivation

**The twenty worlds are derived from the three-dimensional coordinate system**

The dense network of positive and negative time spreading throughout cosmic space is called the time tunnel. The channels connecting the Thirty-Six-Dimensional Space to one another are called space tunnels. Time tunnels are always vertical; space tunnels are always horizontal. To travel to the past or future, one uses the time tunnel; to travel across the Thirty-Six-Dimensional Space, one uses the space tunnel. Time and space are interlaced, producing twenty quadrants. We know that time has positive and negative directions, and so does space. Considering only positive time and positive space yields a single quadrant — the first quadrant of the plane coordinate system. When we include negative time and negative space, we get four quadrants (xy, x-y, -xy, -x-y), formed by real numbers. Upon entering the antimatter world, we discover an additional imaginary unit i; substituting z for i and combining with xy generates a three-dimensional coordinate system with twenty quadrants: xyz, xy-z, x-y-z, x-yz, -x-y-z, -xyz, -x-yz, -xy-z, xz, x-z, -xz, -x-z, yz, y-z, -yz, -y-z, xy, x-y, -xy, -x-y. Thus the whole universe contains twenty worlds, called the twenty parallel worlds because they coexist simultaneously.

*(Chanyuan Corpus · Antimatter World Chapter · Space-Time Tunnels and the Twenty Parallel Worlds)*

The Greatest Creator is the supreme mathematician. Everything in the universe can be expressed and derived through mathematics (numbers, arithmetic, algebra, geometry, trigonometry, calculus, imaginary functions, and more).

Adding a Z-axis to the four quadrants of the plane coordinate system yields twenty quadrants via factorial calculation:

(5×4/2) + (5×4×3/3×2×1) = 20
