# Demonic Nature (Moxing) · Internal Version

> This version is for practitioners and researchers. All source texts are presented in full, organized within a seven-section logical framework. Texts are translated faithfully without embellishment; sources are cited after each passage.

---

## Entry Overview

Demonic nature (*moxing*) is a quality inherent in the nonmaterial structure of every LIFE — rooted in the principle of cosmic holography: because the universe contains demonic beings, and because the universe is holographic, every LIFE carries demonic nature, just as it carries divine, celestial, and Buddha nature. Whether a LIFE becomes a demon or a Buddha depends on which nature holds the dominant position. Human beings are particularly susceptible to demonic nature taking over, because human nature itself contains demonic nature as a component. Subduing demonic nature and purging the demonic gene from the nonmaterial structure is a key threshold on the path toward becoming a Celestial Being or Buddha.

## I. Definition and Essence

There are demons in the universe. Demons stand at the same level as divine beings, Buddhas, and Celestial Beings — they are second only to the Greatest Creator. Because the universe is holographic, all LIFE carries demonic nature, just as all LIFE carries divine nature, Buddha nature, and celestial nature.

*(the Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom · Subduing Demonic Nature, 2012/11/7)*

---

What determines whether a being is a divine being, a Buddha, a demon, a Celestial Being, a spirit, a specter, a human being, or an animal — is which type of nature dominates that LIFE. When divine nature is full, that being is divine; when Buddha nature is abundant, that being is a Buddha; when demonic nature dominates, that being is a demon; when human nature prevails, that being is human — and so on.

*(the Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom · Subduing Demonic Nature, 2012/11/7)*

---

Human beings easily become demonic, because human nature contains demonic nature.

*(the Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom · Subduing Demonic Nature, 2012/11/7)*

---

Nature is the quality specific to the structure of a LIFE or an object. Whatever structure exists, that structure produces that nature. Every category of LIFE has its own inherent nature: divine beings have divine nature, Buddhas have Buddha nature, demons have demonic nature, Celestial Beings have celestial nature, humans have human nature, animals have animal nature.

*(the Chanyuan Corpus · Cultivation · Celestial Nature, 2019-11-22)*

All things have nature: mountains have mountain nature, water has water nature, divine beings have divine nature, demons have demonic nature, humans have human nature, animals have animal nature. Where does the nature of all things come from? Answer: the nature of all things comes from their structure. Whatever structure exists, that structure determines that nature.

## III. Signs and Standards — The Eight Conditions for Becoming Demonic

Just as grass does not grow on stone and insects are scarce on snowy mountains — because the conditions for their existence are absent — a person dominated by divine and Buddha nature does not have the conditions for becoming demonic. But if a person exhibits the following qualities, that person is very easily controlled by demonic nature and may become demonic:

**1. Arrogance:** Arrogance is the ideal condition for becoming demonic. Once a person becomes arrogant, that person is already resonating with the same frequency as demons — already a son or daughter of demons.

**2. Self-centeredness:** When a person makes everything about themselves — disregarding nature, society, and the feelings of others — endlessly speaking about their own opinions, experiences, pain, and grievances, always pushing blame onto others while believing themselves to be right, always putting themselves first, enjoying argument and conflict, and easily becoming angry — that person is moving toward the demonic.

**3. Darkness:** When a person acts furtively, lacks the courage to act openly, is always afraid of being seen or discovered, enjoys gossiping behind others' backs, speaking ill of others in private, or prying into the private affairs of others — that person has drawn close to the demonic.

**4. Greed and cunning:** Boundless desire is demonic. Wanting to possess as much wealth and power as possible, coveting everything good in the world, driven by inflated private desires that are never satisfied — pursuing goals without limit on the means, being ruthless, sinister, deceptive, presenting one face and hiding another, committing every manner of evil — this is the demonic.

**5. False spiritualism:** Without truth, science, logic, or genuine spiritual perception, and in order to achieve secret, self-serving ends — using claims of communicating with divine beings, Buddhas, or high spirits as a cover; using alleged supernatural powers, sorcery, witchcraft, or fabricated dream-visions to deceive others and compel their belief or obedience — this is the demonic.

**6. Intense jealousy:** In response to everything beautiful, responding not with admiration but with slander and attack; in response to others' strengths or possessions, not encouraging and affirming but scheming to destroy and condemn; in response to others' merits and virtue, not learning and emulating but endlessly finding fault and blaming — harboring hatred in the heart, then acting to destroy — this is the manifestation of the demonic.

**7. Extreme selfishness:** Never feeling gratitude or appreciation; making everything revolve around satisfying oneself; when desires go unmet, either screaming hysterically or silently harboring resentment; immediately discarding all gratitude for the Greatest Creator's grace and others' kindness at the slightest dissatisfaction — this is what is colloquially called the "ungrateful wolf." Such a person refuses to take responsibility, always attributing every mistake to others rather than looking within, always thinking of one's own feelings while never considering those of others, being unreasonable and impossible to reason with, always believing that others are treating one badly without considering whether one is treating others well — this is the sign that demonic nature is erupting.

**8. Threatening with death:** At the slightest frustration or dissatisfaction, using the threat of death to coerce others into satisfying one's demands or desires — this is the behavior of sons and daughters of demons.

These eight conditions — arrogance, self-centeredness, darkness, greed and cunning, false spiritualism, intense jealousy, extreme selfishness, and threatening with death — are the soil and conditions that generate demonic nature. Once a person falls into these states, that person walks the demonic path, becomes controlled by demonic forces, and becomes a son or daughter of demons.

*(the Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom · Subduing Demonic Nature, 2012/11/7)*

## IV. Cultivation Practice — How to Subdue Demonic Nature

How does one subdue demonic nature?

Answer: Revere the Greatest Creator, revere LIFE, revere nature. Let the spiritual light of the Greatest Creator fill your inner being. Strengthen your conviction in walking the Way of the Greatest Creator. Be humble. Make offering and contribution your first duty in life. Act with an open and upright heart in all you do. Do not pursue alleged supernatural communication, do not perform or demonstrate alleged supernatural powers, sorcery, or witchcraft, do not fabricate dream-visions. Always look for the excellent and luminous side of others and of society. Feel joy in the happiness and well-being of others. Seek to analyze right and wrong from the standpoint of others. Remain calm in the face of events. Maintain inner peace. Never threaten others with death. Do not take extreme paths. In this way, you can subdue your demonic nature, set foot on the path toward light and happiness, and ultimately attain the highest enlightenment and reach the Elysium World.

*(the Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom · Subduing Demonic Nature, 2012/11/7)*

---

As the red dye reddens and the black ink blackens — worship the Buddha and receive Buddha nature; worship demons and receive demonic nature. Walk by the river enough and your feet will get wet … Understand these truths early, and separate yourself early from troubled places and troubled people — otherwise, regret will come too late.

*(the Guide's Other Articles · 2006 · Dissolving Worldly Ties and Cultivating Celestial Ties)*

---

The waves wash the shore — in the storm, what is sand and what is gold becomes clear. Hold firmly to the Dao. Hold firmly to the Values. Suppress the demonic selfishness within.

*(the Guide's Other Articles · 2015 · Chanyuan Celestials Are Known for Their Selflessness)*

---

The Guide's authority is needed to subdue demonic nature. Demonic nature is high in energy-level and endlessly shifting in form. The path of practice therefore requires someone of great wisdom, possessing Hundun thinking, to provide timely wake-up calls and sharp interventions. For example, with arrogance — feeling oneself superior to others — the Guide's authority achieves the effect of using arrogance to counter arrogance, using the demonic to grind away the demonic.

*(the Guide's Other Articles · 2012 · These Brothers and Sisters Have Put Real Work into Understanding the Guide)*
