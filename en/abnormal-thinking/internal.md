## III. The Most Classic Proof of Abnormal Thinking: The Möbius Strip

(*Chanyuan Corpus, Section 02: Abnormal Thinking, “I. Starting from the Möbius Strip,” excerpt from the complete original text):*

> A sheet of paper has a front and a back. > From the viewpoint of conventional thinking, this is absolutely impossible. But someone solved this difficult problem. He was the German mathematician August Ferdinand Möbius.

> Möbius's method was to twist one end of the strip of paper by 180 degrees and paste it to the other end.

> At that point, the ant can freely crawl from the front side to the back side, and from the back side to the front side.

> This is the “Möbius strip.”

> The “Möbius strip” is the result of abnormal thinking. What conventional thinking considers impossible is easy for abnormal thinking.

> …

> Let us look again at the “Möbius strip.” It turns a paper with two sides into a strip with no front and no back. The key is that one of its ends is turned over by 180 degrees.
