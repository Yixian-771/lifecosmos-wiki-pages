This article examines the internal argumentative structure and its structural analogies and key differences with: Christian heaven and the Last Judgement, Buddhist Pure Land rebirth theory, Plato's Realm of Forms, Daoist immortality cultivation, and Dante's *Divine Comedy* heaven structure.

## II. Comparative Analysis with Philosophical and Religious Traditions

### 2.1 Christian Heaven and the Last Judgement: The Most Direct Contrast

The Christian Last Judgement holds that after death, God judges; those who believe in Christ are saved and ascend to heaven; unbelievers enter hell. Lifechanyuan similarly has a three-tier structure of heaven / human world / hell; similarly has a highest place where God resides (the Serene Realm); similarly has a place of punishment (the Fire-Tempering Layer / the Yin-Pole Black Hole).

**Key difference**: The Christian judgement is external (God judges you); Lifechanyuan's "judgement" is internal (your LIFE structure determines the space you inhabit). The Christian heaven is a vague spiritual state; Lifechanyuan's six higher LIFE spaces have precise cosmic coordinates (960 light-years / 3,480 light-years / the entire Earth universe / 80 billion islands) and specific LIFE-characteristic descriptions.

### 2.2 Buddhist Pure Land Rebirth Theory: The Closest Eastern Analogy

Buddhist Pure Land school holds that through reciting the Buddha's name (Amitābha) one can be reborn in the Western Pure Land (Sukhāvatī). Lifechanyuan explicitly states that "the Elysium World is what Buddhism calls the Western Pure Land (Sukhāvatī)" — directly adopting and reinterpreting this concept.

**Key difference**: Buddhist Pure Land rebirth relies on "other-power" (the vow-power of Amitābha to receive and guide); Lifechanyuan's entry into the Elysium World relies on "the degree of perfection of the LIFE structure" (self-power). The Buddhist Pure Land is an object of faith; Lifechanyuan's Elysium World is a cosmic geographic entity (the entire Earth universe — the Dharma-Spiral System — containing 3,000 spiral-galaxy systems).

### 2.3 Plato's Realm of Forms: The Closest Western Philosophical Analogy

Plato held that the world of the senses is shadow; the Realm of Forms is real — the most real being the "Form of the Good." This is highly analogous to the essentialist cosmological view of Lifechanyuan's higher LIFE spaces (the Elysium World belongs to negative-universe space; objects exist by their essential nature, not their form; eternal and undying).

**Key difference**: Plato's Realm of Forms is a purely abstract philosophical construction with no specific inhabitants; Lifechanyuan's higher spaces are cosmic entities with inhabitants (immortals / Buddhas / divine beings), specific characteristic descriptions, and precise coordinates. Plato's metempsychosis (transmigration) is a philosophical pursuit toward the Realm of Forms; Lifechanyuan's reincarnation is a cultivation project of elevation toward the higher LIFE spaces.

### 2.4 Daoist Immortality Cultivation: The Closest Chinese Traditional Analogy

The Daoist cultivation tradition has five levels of immortals — celestial immortals, divine immortals, terrestrial immortals, human immortals, and ghost immortals — and Lifechanyuan directly adopts this classificatory framework, embedding it in a cosmic coordinate system: human immortals → Thousand-Year World; terrestrial immortals → Ten-Thousand-Year World; divine immortals → roaming the four seas; celestial immortals → Celestial Islands Continent.

**Key difference**: Daoist immortal realms are typically secluded cultivation grounds in nature (grotto-heavens and blessed lands) with no cosmic coordinates; Lifechanyuan's immortal realms have precise light-year distances and cosmic structure descriptions. The Daoist cultivation path varies enormously across schools (inner alchemy / outer alchemy / talisman-and-register…); Lifechanyuan provides a clear three-level route map (Second Home → eight modes of thinking → eight mysteries of LIFE).

### 2.5 Dante's *Divine Comedy* Heaven Structure: The Closest Cosmic Hierarchy Analogy

Dante in the *Paradiso* of the *Divine Comedy* describes a structure of nine heavens, from the Moon Heaven to the Primum Mobile, with God's dwelling at the highest point (the Empyrean / Rosa Candida). Lifechanyuan's six higher LIFE spaces are similarly a tiered cosmic structure, with God's abode (the Serene Realm) at the highest point — a highly similar structure.

**Key difference**: Dante's nine heavens combine medieval Christian cosmological view with Aristotelian astronomy; heavenly inhabitants are determined by divine grace. Lifechanyuan's six higher LIFE spaces are based on the internalised logic of "LIFE structure determines the space one inhabits"; admission is determined by one's own cultivation. Dante's heaven is a theological-poetic construction; Lifechanyuan's higher spaces use "the law that the sum of positive and negative energy equals zero" (Concept 478) as a physics-analogy basis.
