# When Flowers Emit Fragrance, Butterflies Come; When the Mind Is Still, Meaning Flows

---

## Overview

"When flowers emit fragrance, butterflies come; when the mind is still, meaning flows; when the body is clean, illness departs; when the heart is pure, immortals arrive." — This is a core teaching of Guide Xuefeng of Lifechanyuan, originating from *The Apocalypse Section* of the Chanyuan Anthology, in the essay titled *When Flowers Emit Fragrance, Immortals Arrive with a Pure Heart*.

This teaching runs through multiple works in both the Chanyuan Anthology and the Xuefeng Anthology. It is a highly condensed expression of the cultivation philosophy of Lifechanyuan and is frequently cited by Chanyuan Celestials in daily life practice.

These four parallel statements form a progressive structure that reveals the profound correspondence between inner state and outer reality — the quality of one’s heart determines the environment of one’s life.

---

## The Four Principles

### 1. When Flowers Emit Fragrance, Butterflies Come

Flowers do not chase butterflies; they simply bloom into their most fragrant form, and butterflies naturally arrive.

This reveals a universal law: whatever kind of environment exists will attract corresponding forms of life. Flowers attract bees and butterflies; filth attracts flies and insects. The phoenix does not land in barren places; worthy ministers choose their sovereign.

For individuals, the people, events, and circumstances encountered ultimately correspond to one’s inner environment. The root of problems lies not in the external world, but in the garden of one’s own heart.

Therefore, the most important task in life is not the pursuit of fame or advantage, but the cultivation of a beautiful inner environment.

---

### 2. When the Mind Is Still, Meaning Flows

Stillness does not mean suppressing thoughts; it means a natural state free from disturbance, desire, and attachment.

In this state, consciousness becomes clear and thinking flows smoothly. Inspiration arises naturally. When the mind is chaotic and burdened with trivialities, deep thinking becomes difficult.

This principle applies beyond writing: higher-quality life prefers order; lower-quality life gravitates toward disorder. Order nourishes life; disorder consumes it.

Even amid turbulence, a Chanyuan Celestial within Lifechanyuan may remain steady — not as a matter of morality, but as a matter of life quality.

---

### 3. When the Body Is Clean, Illness Departs

Cleanliness refers not only to external hygiene but to moderation in diet, regularity in daily life, restraint of desire, and avoidance of exhausting one’s vitality.

When the life state is properly regulated, illness finds little ground to attach itself. Relying solely on medication without adjusting the fundamental life state treats symptoms rather than causes.

---

### 4. When the Heart Is Pure, Immortals Arrive

This is the highest state among the four.

Purity of heart means a state of harmony, order, and clarity. In such a state, life prospers, and resonance with higher realms of life becomes possible.

The words of Jesus, Shakyamuni Buddha, and Laozi are understood as expressions spoken from a purified heart. When one reaches this state, entry into higher modes of understanding becomes possible.

---

## Sixteen States of Impurity

In *Intermediate Cultivation*, Guide Xuefeng lists sixteen conditions that prevent purity of heart:

> A heart with evil thoughts is impure;  
> A heart lost in delusion is impure;  
> A heart driven by greed is impure;  
> Seeking petty advantage makes the heart impure;  
> Deception and cunning make the heart impure;  
> Reckless speech makes the heart impure;  
> Insulting divine beings makes the heart impure;  
> Being unfilial to parents makes the heart impure;  
> Violence and theft make the heart impure;  
> Indecent thoughts make the heart impure;  
> An unclean body makes the heart impure;  
> A filthy living space makes the heart impure;  
> Fraud makes the heart impure;  
> Lack of compassion makes the heart impure;  
> Ignorance of principle makes the heart impure;  
> Blasphemy against the Greatest Creator makes the heart impure.

A pure heart is described as a state of immortality. Upon reaching such a state, higher beings naturally resonate and respond.
