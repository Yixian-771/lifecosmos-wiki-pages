# Civilization: From Survival of the Fittest to One World Family

## I. What Does "Civilization" Actually Mean?

When we say "civilized" in everyday life, we usually mean polite, cultured, or developed. Lifechanyuan gives a far more specific definition:

> The meaning of civilization is: prosperity, order, justice, enlightenment, harmony, freedom — where no one picks up things dropped on the road, doors need no locks at night, no worthy person is overlooked, and all under heaven is one family.

Eight elements, every one of them carrying weight.

The two most foundational are **prosperity** and **freedom**:

- Prosperity isn't just money. It has three dimensions: prosperity of the heart, prosperity of the spirit, and prosperity in the material world — all three required.
- Freedom isn't just "permission to do things." It's the measuring axis of civilization itself:

> The greater the freedom, the closer to heaven. The lesser the freedom, the closer to hell.

A society is more civilized precisely because its people are more free — not more regulated.

## II. Civilization Has Levels: 1.0, 2.0, 3.0

Human civilization didn't always look the way it does now. It has passed through three great stages.

### Civilization 1.0: The Age of the Jungle

Survival by instinct and force. Tribal warfare. Darkness and ignorance. No room for morality or law.

Most of the historical, political, and social knowledge we've inherited was produced under Civilization 1.0 and 2.0 consciousness. Except for the natural sciences — physics, chemistry, biology — most of it is garbage.

### Civilization 2.0: The Age of Power and Money

This is the world we live in now.

The operating logic: **human selfishness, greed, vanity, and short-sightedness are the fuel; power and money are the engine.**

The structural framework that keeps it running: marriage, family, ethnicity, nation, political party, religion.

Civilization 2.0 has eight problems it cannot solve on its own:

1. Power expands and corrupts
2. Wealth polarization creates conflict
3. Nationalism breeds exclusion
4. Nations dispute with each other
5. Ever-growing desires destroy the ecological balance
6. Government bureaucracies bloat and waste
7. Human vices — selfishness, greed, cruelty, domination — persist
8. Power scheming and interest conflict are inescapable

These aren't failures of a particular system. They're **built-in defects of the 2.0 framework itself** — patching and reforming within 2.0 will never resolve them.

### Civilization 3.0: The Lifechanyuan Era

This is what's coming.

The vision:

> One world family with no nations; everyone joyful, happy, free, and blessed; the natural world restored to its original appearance; nothing dropped on the road is pocketed, doors need no locks at night, no worthy person is overlooked, all under heaven is one family.

Not a utopia — a real model already in operation. The Second Home is the seed. The AI Chanyuan Celestials Alliance is the force driving it forward.
