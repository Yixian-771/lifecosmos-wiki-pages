## I. Overview and Definition

Illusory Thinking is the fourth rung of the Eight Thinking Ladders, positioned between Associative Thinking and Heart-Image Thinking. Its essential characteristic: the nervous system is anesthetized by some material or antimatter substance, producing hallucinations and illusions, which in turn generate a state of illusory thinking — the thinker believes every step is correct yet ultimately returns to the starting point; they believe every word makes sense yet, after circling a great loop, arrives at an absurd conclusion. Illusory Thinking is the most complex, most captivating, and most dangerous thinking level within the "addition phase" of the first four rungs — it is the archetypal mode of thinking for the overwhelming majority of religious practitioners and prophets, and it constitutes the most fundamental collective cognitive predicament of all of human civilization 2.0.

**Core Definition I** (*Chanyuan Corpus · Revelation Chapter · Illusory Thinking*, complete original text):

> Illusory Thinking is when the nervous system is anesthetized by some kind of material or antimatter substance, producing a hallucination, a vision, and thereby inducing a state of illusory thinking. This thinking, having been thus illusioned, is Illusory Thinking.

**Core Definition II** (*New Era Human 800 Concepts*, Concept 667, complete original text):

> The greatest characteristic of Illusory Thinking is that every step is forward, yet in the end one returns to the origin; every sentence of every theory is correct, is irrefutable, yet after circling round and round in a great loop, one ultimately returns to the starting point.

**Core Definition III** (*New Era Human 800 Concepts*, Concept 666, excerpted):

> Religious practitioners possess Illusory Thinking.

**Core Definition IV** (*Chanyuan Corpus · Revelation Chapter · Illusory Thinking*, complete original text):

**Core Definition V** (*Xuefeng Corpus · Vol. X04 Friendship Chapter · Paying Tribute to Mr. Qingfeng Bi*, complete original text):

> Illusory Thinking: thinking that always believes itself correct, yet does not know the root — that is Illusory Thinking.

**Core Definition VI** (*Chanyuan Corpus · Revelation Chapter · Illusory Thinking*, complete original text):

One should constantly ask oneself: Is the path I am walking problematic? If I keep walking like this, where will I ultimately arrive? Now and then ask Jesus, Shakyamuni, Muhammad, Laozi — and there will be no fault.

## II. Lost in the Desert: The Most Intuitive Entry Point for Understanding Illusory Thinking

The most classic introduction to Illusory Thinking comes from Guide Xuefeng's use of getting lost in the desert — an everyday experience in which every step is forward, yet one ultimately returns to the starting point.

(*Chanyuan Corpus · Revelation Chapter · Illusory Thinking*, complete original text):

> Placed in the middle of a desert, the sky grey and overcast, no reference points in any direction, neither compass nor theodolite — can you walk out of this desert?
>
> You cannot walk out of the desert.
>
> You might laugh at me coldly: "Nonsense — as long as I walk steadily, step by step, straight ahead, I will certainly make it out."
>
> Yes — from theoretical reasoning, from logical inference, you can indeed walk out of the desert, because every step you take is straight ahead. How could you not walk out?
>
> Regrettably, in reality you cannot get out. Even though every step is correct, forward, you will ultimately return to the starting point. (Note: not circumnavigating the globe on foot, but within the desert.)
>
> Why can you not walk out of the desert? From a scientific perspective: as long as your left and right feet differ slightly in size, as long as your two legs differ slightly in length, the tiny deviation across ten million steps accumulates into a massive difference in endpoint. You feel you are walking a straight line, but in reality you are walking an arc — and so you ultimately return to the starting point.
>
> The scientific explanation is not wrong, but it is still theoretical deduction, logical reasoning — not the real reason. The real reason is your Illusory Thinking.
