## [II] The Form and Content of Prayer

**The complete procedure for prayer**

"Regarding how to pray — the manner and content of prayer — Jesus Christ has already made it very clear to us. We simply follow his teaching. In general terms, prayer must come from sincere devotion; it must never be perfunctory or halfhearted. The content of prayer should begin with wholehearted gratitude for the Greatest Creator's grace, benevolence, and care, then proceed to praise and glorify the Greatest Creator's wisdom, supreme power, and greatness. Next, express your willingness to act in accordance with the Greatest Creator's will, without concern for personal success or failure. Then engage in deep, bone-cleansing repentance for your sins and errors, asking the Greatest Creator to be merciful and forgive your transgressions. Finally, seek the Greatest Creator's help in walking the righteous path so that your life may enter a higher realm of existence in the next lifetime. The closing should indicate that your prayer is offered in the name of Jesus, humanity's Father in Heaven."

(Source: Chanyuan Corpus · Chapter on the Greatest Creator · The Eight Relationships between the Greatest Creator and Humanity · The Corresponding Relationship)

---

**Prayer is a personal act — silent and interior**

"Prayer is a personal act, not a social performance. The kind of prayer that involves shouting aloud, deliberately letting others hear, is false and useless. We must carry it out quietly, silently, without speaking aloud, because our mental activity transforms into thought-waves — like an EEG or ECG — broadcasting our heartfelt intentions into the vast universe. The Greatest Creator will surely respond. Furthermore, since we ourselves are a component of the Greatest Creator, when our prayer is heard by us, that marks the Greatest Creator having heard it."

(Source: Chanyuan Corpus · Chapter on the Greatest Creator · The Eight Relationships between the Greatest Creator and Humanity · The Corresponding Relationship)

---

**The detailed steps for prayer (from the perspective of transcending suffering)**

"The third step is prayer. The best approach to prayer follows Jesus's teaching. The main content should be praising the Greatest Creator — if this day passed in peace, whether we dined on fine food or plain fare, as long as we ate our fill and had a place to sleep sheltered from wind and rain, we should be grateful and thank the Greatest Creator. Next, in prayer, thank all persons and things that brought benefit to us, ask the Greatest Creator to care for and bless them, and forgive those who harmed us, while hoping those who hurt us may attain a beautiful life. Finally, ask the Greatest Creator to grant us a peaceful, happy life and guide us into higher realms of existence."

"Repentance and prayer primarily serve to purify our souls, causing the antimatter structure of our lives to gradually evolve toward perfection. Never let repentance become sophistry or perfunctory excuses, or let prayer become greed or curses."

(Source: Chanyuan Corpus · Tiqi Chapter · Transcending Suffering (Part II) — Tiqi Chapter No. 46)

---

**Prayer as the highest form of spiritual communication**

"Humans are capable of communicating with divine beings. Seeking blessings, burning incense, prostrating — these are all forms of communication with divine beings. But the best method is not through the body, posture, or language — it is through the soul. So, as long as one holds the divine in the heart and regularly repents and prays inwardly, this constitutes direct communication with divine beings, and the ways in which divine beings reciprocate are varied and wondrous beyond description."

(Source: Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom Chapter · The Highest Form of Communication)

---

**An actual example of prayer**

"O Almighty God, my beloved is of utmost importance to me — please do not allow the bus she is riding to have a serious accident. I beg you, God! Amen!"

(Source: Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom Chapter · Follow Your Feelings When Necessary)

"When you encounter danger, be sure to pray immediately and seek help from the Greatest Creator. Because the Tao possesses instantaneous resonance, our inner activities will be perceived by the Greatest Creator in an instant, and protective measures will be deployed at the critical moment. When facing a dilemma, communicate your most beautiful heartfelt wish to the Greatest Creator, then entrust your life to the Greatest Creator, let the Tao govern your path, then adapt to circumstances, act naturally, and respond to the moment — and you will not go wrong."

(Source: Chanyuan Corpus · Transmission Chapter · Xuefeng's Teachings (1–8))

## [V] Distinctions and Boundaries

**Walking the Tao is not the same as religious rote prayer**

"Walking the path of the Greatest Creator is the most effortless, simplest, most direct, and most effective approach. There is no need to abstain from this or that, no need to chant scriptures and pray every day, no need to specially cultivate supernatural powers or divine abilities, no need to exhaust yourself searching for sages — simply follow nature and all will be accomplished."

(Source: New Era Human 800 Concepts, 4th Edition · Concept 539)

---

**Do not pray for miracles — in normal circumstances**

"Miracles do occur frequently, but we must not pray for their appearance. A hen needs 21 days to hatch an egg; the chick breaking out of its shell is itself a miracle. If we wish it to hatch in a few days, we are disrupting the program's operation — this is acting against the Tao."

"Both sides in a war devoutly pray for God to side with them and defeat the other — what is God to do? Everyone prays for God to make everything go their way and make them prosper — what is God to do? The Greatest Creator absolutely does not take sides. The sun shines on good and bad alike; what determines the outcome is the program. Whoever follows the program is the ultimate winner."

"Therefore, praying for miracles to occur is harmful. Miracles must be allowed to happen naturally and imperceptibly; do not force growth or kill the goose for the golden eggs."

"Do not pray for miracles. If you pray for a miracle and it occurs, you will forever disregard process and program, growing habitually lucky-minded — you may become demonic or monstrous. If you pray for a miracle and it does not occur, you will lose faith, curse the heavens, cause your beliefs to collapse, and descend into psychological and spiritual chaos."

"But there are exceptions to every rule. When we are in a truly dangerous and perilous situation, it is time to 'spend a thousand days training troops for use in an emergency' — and pray for a miracle. In ordinary circumstances use ordinary methods; in extraordinary circumstances use extraordinary methods. This is the ordinary and wondrous use of the Tao."

(Source: Chanyuan Corpus · Transmission Chapter · Transmission No. 33: Do Not Pray for Miracles)

---

**The forbidden content of prayer: evil intentions align with demons, not God**

"When praying, be absolutely certain never to harbor evil intentions. Praying for someone's downfall, praying for promotions and wealth, praying for family glory, complaining about God's injustice — these will be received by demons, and the one who prays will be the ultimate victim. Because evil intentions are not on the same frequency as the Greatest Creator — they only resonate with demons."

(Source: Chanyuan Corpus · Chapter on the Greatest Creator · The Corresponding Relationship)

---

**Prayer does not substitute for personal effort**

"You should certainly enjoy what you are entitled to enjoy. If you make no effort to enjoy or protect your right to enjoy — just waiting day after day for the world to turn peaceful, praying every day for the Greatest Creator to take care of you, making no effort yourself — such a person is certainly not pleasing to the Greatest Creator."

(Source: Xuefeng Corpus · Essays · You Must Take Action)
