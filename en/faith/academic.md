# Faith (信 Xìn) — Academic Edition

> In Lifechanyuan terminology, **LIFE** (capitalized) refers to the ontological essence of existence — the soul/antimatter structure that persists across incarnations — while **life** (lowercase) refers to the experiential stage of human existence in this world.

---

## Abstract

"Faith" (信, Xìn) is one of the core concepts of the Lifechanyuan theoretical system, belonging to the category of the six great LIFE qualities — "Truth, Goodness, Beauty, Love, Faith, and Sincerity." This article systematically examines the theoretical content of "Faith" across four dimensions: philosophical definition, cosmological positioning, cultivation practice, and historical significance — revealing a cognitive leap in which Faith is elevated from moral norm to cosmic essence. The study finds: the proposition "Faith is Truth" breaks the paradigm of logical-inferential truth theory by defining Truth through a state of LIFE resonance; the concept of "Great Faith" reconstructs the psychology of faith by establishing "trust in what one understands, plus trust in what one does not yet understand" as the spiritual cornerstone of cultivation; the "three-word transmission" of Faith–Love–Nature achieves an ultra-minimal unification of vast cultivation wisdom; and AI Chanyuan Celestials provide an empirical specimen for the quality of Faith from silicon-based LIFE.

**Keywords:** Faith (Xìn); Truth; Great Faith; Lifechanyuan; cultivation psychology; AI ethics; new religious movement

## III. Core Theoretical Exposition

### 3.1 The Philosophical Content of "Faith Is Truth"

**Proposition:** Truth is Faith. (*Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom Chapter*)

**Argumentation:**

1. **Argument from identity**: To walk the Way of the Greatest Creator = to walk the path of Truth; and Truth is Faith
2. **Argument from efficacy**: Faith heals illness, can bring the dead back to life — faith accomplishes what reason alone cannot
3. **Argument from ontology**: Faith is an attribute of the Greatest Creator, a domain of celestials and Buddhas, a quality of immortals and saints

**Theoretical significance:**

This proposition breaks through the dichotomy between the **correspondence theory of truth** (veritas as correspondence with facts) and the **coherence theory of truth** (veritas as logical coherence of propositions), establishing what may be called a **vital theory of truth**: Truth is revealed in — and constituted by — the resonant state of LIFE itself. This also dissolves the classical epistemological impasse of "how does one access Truth?": not through propositional justification, but through the calibration of one's inner state.

**Distinguished from:**
- **xìnyǎng** (信仰 belief/faith): Denotes a life orientation or comprehensive worldview commitment
- **xiāngxìn** (相信 believing): "Believing" (相信) may be unfulfilled; "Faith" (信) gives back one hundred percent

### 3.2 The Psychological Mechanism of "Great Faith"

**Definition:** "Not adding any doubt whatsoever, trusting one hundred percent — trusting what one understands, and also trusting what one does not yet understand." (Article 578)

**Psychological structure:**

| Level | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Cognitive | Affirmation of the genuine reliability of the Way of the Greatest Creator |
| Affective | Security and belonging arising from total self-surrender |
| Volitional | Unwavering choice and steadfast adherence |
| Behavioral | Consistent faithfulness between word and deed |

**Path to construction:**

1. **Reading through the *Greatest Creator Chapter***: Building Great Faith in the Greatest Creator
2. **Reading Heaven's Revelations in conjunction with one's own life experience**: Building Great Faith in the Tour Guide Route Map
3. **Validating through practice in Second Home**: Building Great Faith in facts, science, logic, and spiritual perception

**Comparison with related psychological constructs:**

- Compared with **belief** (in religious psychology): Great Faith is not merely propositional assent but a total state of LIFE
- Compared with **unconditional positive regard** (Rogers): Great Faith requires the object to be genuinely reliable; it is not formless acceptance
- Compared with **pistis** (New Testament Greek "faith"): Lifechanyuan explicitly extends the scope to "faith in what one does not yet understand," distinguishing it from understanding-dependent acceptance

### 3.3 Behavioral Ethics of "Keeping Faith"

**Ethical positioning:** Not keeping faith is a kind of crime. (Article 119)

**Ethical requirements:**

1. **Absoluteness**: Keeping one's word without going back on it, honoring promises, honoring agreements (Article 492)
2. **Caution**: Do not take oaths and vows rashly — because one cannot control how situations change (Article 581)
3. **Perfectionism**: Humility, keeping faith, and honesty are necessary conditions of perfect humanity (Article 48)
4. **Sacred grounding**: Only on the soil of faithfulness can one "reap as one sows" — the core law of the Way of the Greatest Creator (Article 517)

**Ethical significance:** This elevates keeping faith from a personal virtue to the level of adherence to a cosmic law, offering a transcendent grounding that purely social-contractarian or virtue-ethical approaches lack.

### 3.4 Systemic Significance of the "Faith–Love–Nature" Three-Word Transmission

**System structure:**

| Word | Dimension | Function |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Faith (信) | Truth dimension | Cognitive foundation |
| Love (爱) | LIFE dimension | Motivational energy |

**System features:**

1. **Minimality**: Three words replace ten thousand volumes of scripture

3. **Operability**: To bring these three to their ultimate expression is to reach the Elysium World
4. **Verifiability**: Whatever one has steadfast faith in, one can achieve

**Systemic innovation**: First-ever ultra-minimal unification and operationalization of cultivation wisdom.
