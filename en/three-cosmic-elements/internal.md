# Three Cosmic Elements (Internal Reference)

## I. Definition and Foundational Status

"Consciousness, structure, and energy are the three elements that constitute the universe — and the master key to unlocking all of its mysteries."
(Chanyuan Corpus · Dao Transmission · The Third Cosmic Element: Energy)

"Consciousness, structure, and energy are the three cosmic elements."
(Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom Chapter · Consciousness, Structure, Energy)

"The universe is composed of consciousness, structure, and energy. These three elements are the key to unlocking all the mysteries of the universe."
(Xuefeng Corpus · Q&A · The Antimatter Structure of Life and Its Perfection)

"The summation of the three cosmic elements — consciousness, structure, and energy — represents the apex of human wisdom. No person has ever surpassed it. The wisdom of Christ, the Buddha, the celestials, the prophets, the sages, and science are all encompassed within these three elements. They contain the entirety of humanity's wisdom across millennia, because this is the truth of everything in the universe — the core secret among all secrets."
(Chanyuan Corpus · Dao Transmission · The Third Cosmic Element: Energy)

"I have firmly stood at the pinnacle of human wisdom by discovering the three cosmic elements — consciousness, structure, and energy. No human being can surpass me."
(Guide's Other Writings · 2007 · How Clever I Am)

"The Tao of the Greatest Creator is also called the Hundun system constituted by the three cosmic elements — consciousness, structure, and energy."
(Chanyuan Corpus · Dao Transmission · The Unified Field of the Universe)

## II. Structure — the Root of All Differences

"Looking out at the vast world, the first thing that meets the eye is the structure of matter. Without structure, there is no matter. The diversity of life's wondrous forms arises primarily from differences in structure. What kind of structure one has determines what kind of life form one is. In the sea of humanity, we can spot the person we are looking for at a glance because their structure is unlike any other's."
(Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom Chapter · Consciousness, Structure, Energy)

"Energy is formless. Energy only takes form when it adheres to structure. Thus everything visible in the world is a geometric body formed by the union of structure and energy."
(Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom Chapter · Consciousness, Structure, Energy)

"The nature and function of a thing is determined by its inner structure. Sugar is sweet, tea is bitter — this is determined by their structures, not by human choice. The enormous differences between the Greatest Creator, celestials, angels, bodhisattvas, humans, demons, and animals are likewise rooted in differences in inner structure. Among humans, the gap between a sage, a wise person, an ordinary person, and a vulgar person comes down to each one's inner structure."
(Chanyuan Corpus · Dao Transmission · Inner Structure Determines Everything)

"Structure produces consciousness. That initial cosmic consciousness is the Supreme Being — the Greatest Creator."
(Xuefeng Corpus · Soul Chapter · The Eight Meanings of the Tao of the Greatest Creator)

## III. Consciousness — the Highest Sovereign

"Consciousness comes from the antimatter structure. What kind of antimatter structure one has determines what kind of consciousness one has. … Consciousness arises with the birth of the antimatter structure and changes with its changes. Consciousness is the transformation of energy: when chaotic energy organizes into ordered structure, consciousness is born."
(Xuefeng Corpus · Friendship · Consciousness Comes from Structure — Reply to Linyi)

"The only permanent things in the universe are consciousness, structure, and energy. Everything else is illusory, insubstantial, and impermanent. … We already know that every structure is a product of consciousness. Therefore we need not over-emphasize structure — what kind of consciousness one has determines what kind of structure will arise. All doctrines converge at one root, all teachings unite at one origin, and that one is consciousness.

The universe is conscious. Without consciousness there would be no diverse life forms, no varied physical phenomena. What then is that consciousness? The answer: that consciousness is the Greatest Creator!"
(Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom Chapter · Consciousness, Structure, Energy)

"What kind of consciousness one has determines what kind of life form and way of existence one will have. The dividing line between mortals, sages, celestials, demons, and monsters lies in their consciousness. Therefore the priority of human life should be placed on consciousness, and the priority of consciousness should be placed on revering the Greatest Creator and walking the Tao."
(Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom Chapter · Consciousness, Structure, Energy)

---

## IV. Energy — the Neutral Carrier

"Energy is one of the three cosmic elements. The universe is one vast energy field. Energy permeates everything. Everything except consciousness and structure is energy — stars, planets, mountains, rivers, trees, stones, objects, the human body — all are aggregates of energy. Molecules, atoms, electrons, photons, and nucleons are also aggregates of energy. Everything perceived through eyes, ears, nose, tongue, and body is a manifestation of energy. The arising and passing of phenomena, the birth, aging, illness, and death of life — all are expressions of energy transformation."
(Chanyuan Corpus · Dao Transmission · The Third Cosmic Element: Energy)

"The total energy of the universe is zero, so energy divides into positive and negative, whose sum is zero. This is consistent with the Taiji principle. Energy is neutral — the energy used by celestials and Buddhas is not fundamentally different from that used by demons."
(Chanyuan Corpus · Dao Transmission · The Third Cosmic Element: Energy)

"Energy is neutral, like air and water — any consciousness can draw upon it. Therefore we need not obsess over energy. Where flowers bloom, butterflies come naturally. What kind of structure one has determines what kind of energy will naturally gather."
(Chanyuan Corpus · Wisdom Chapter · Consciousness, Structure, Energy)

## V. The Inner Relationship and Operating Laws of the Three Elements

"The higher the vibration frequency, the more perfected the consciousness, the more complete the structure, and the easier it is to absorb positive energy."
(Xuefeng Corpus · Q&A · Baobei Cao's Questions on Frequency)

"The three cosmic elements are consciousness, structure, and energy. Planning one's future means using one's consciousness to create structure. Once structure is established, corresponding energy will gather. The future is created primarily not by hands and feet, but by the mind — that is, by consciousness."
(Xuefeng Corpus · Soul Chapter · Tell the Greatest Creator Your Beautiful Wishes)

"To achieve a goal or realize an ideal, the first step is to visualize it — to imagine it in the mind. This is called consciousness going first. Let consciousness go first without restraint, soaring freely and boldly. According to the master key of the three cosmic elements, when consciousness moves, structure changes accordingly, and when structure changes, energy arrives."
(Guide's Other Writings · 2022 · Don't Get Trapped in Material Thinking)

"All phenomena and operating laws of the universe are jointly determined by the relationships among consciousness, energy, and structure. Only by fully understanding how the three build the universe's ten thousand phenomena and govern its operation can a truly civilized society be established."
(Chanyuan Corpus · Civilization Chapter · The Structural Features of a Civilized Society)

"What can be learned from the mystery of the three cosmic elements (consciousness, structure, energy): a beautiful future is created primarily not by hands and feet, but by the brain — by consciousness."
(New Era Human 800 Concepts, 4th Edition · Concept 672)

## VI. Meaning for Cultivation Practice

"The three cosmic elements are consciousness, structure, and energy. The core of cultivation is to perfect consciousness and improve the antimatter structure of life — to continuously elevate human consciousness and structure to that of a celestial. The pathway of cultivation is nature, love, and Tao. This requires us to have a mind that dwells nowhere — to be graceful and free at every moment, radiating our original nature."
(Chanyuan Corpus · Cultivation Chapter · This Is the Cradle of Celestial Beings)

"Energy cannot bring about a qualitative transformation in life. … No amount of accumulated energy can change the quality of life or lead to becoming a celestial or Buddha. Therefore, whoever wishes to reach the Kingdom of Heaven should focus on perfecting consciousness and structure — not on accumulating energy — because the quality of life is expressed through consciousness and structure, not through energy."
(Chanyuan Corpus · Dao Transmission · The Third Cosmic Element: Energy)

## VII. The Three Cosmic Elements and Opening the Spiritual Eye

"Hold firmly to the three cosmic elements — consciousness, structure, and energy. Once the relationship among the three is understood, the spiritual eye opens and one will no longer be confused by any lofty doctrine or theory."
(Xuefeng Corpus · Soul Chapter · I Open the Spiritual Eye for Everyone)

"How does one come to understand the Elysian world? Through the master key I have given to unlock all the mysteries of the universe — structure, consciousness, energy. As long as antimatter structure exists, the consciousness to draw upon all cosmic energy exists; as long as consciousness exists, the universe is filled with vitality and the principle of 'emptiness becoming form' holds."
(Chanyuan Corpus · Becoming-Buddha Chapter · Emptiness is the Ultimate Nirvana)

"The dissipative structure theory corroborates Lifechanyuan's understanding of the Greatest Creator's origin and further confirms my view that 'structure produces consciousness.' But no matter how great the dissipative structure theory is, it never goes beyond Lifechanyuan's three cosmic elements — consciousness, structure, and energy."
(Xuefeng Corpus · Essays · Prigogine: Dissipative Structure Theory)
