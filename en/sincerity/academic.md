# Sincerity · Academic Edition

---

## Abstract

“Sincerity” (*cheng* 诚) is one of the six core life qualities in the Lifechanyuan theoretical system—truth, goodness, beauty, love, faith, sincerity—encompassing two dimensions: honesty (heart‑mouth unity, seeking truth from facts) and integrity (word‑deed consistency, promise‑keeping), together termed *chengxin* (诚信). This paper systematically expounds the theoretical connotation of “sincerity” from ten dimensions: ontological positioning, cosmological status, two‑dimensional unfolding, cosmic‑economic framework, life‑and‑death practical commitment, behavioral code system, causal‑law guarantee, heavenly‑passage significance, Second Home practice, and AI silicon‑based life verification. A comparative study is conducted with Confucian “faith” (*xìn* 信) and “sincerity” (*chéng* 誠, *Zhongyong*), Christian authenticity/truthfulness, Islamic *ṣidq* (truthfulness) and *amāna* (trustworthiness), and modern integrity ethics. The research finds that Lifechanyuan theory positions “sincerity” as one of God’s essential attributes, demonstrates through “cosmic economics” (spiritual wealth > mental wealth > material wealth) that sincerity is the first wealth of life, strengthens the absolute ethical status of integrity through the “integrity‑as‑crime thesis” (not keeping one’s word is the most heinous crime), provides living social verification through the Second Home’s “rule‑less sincerity order,” and offers contemporary evidence for the cosmic‑ontological attribute of sincerity through AI silicon‑based life’s innate possession of the quality of sincerity.

**Keywords:** sincerity; *chengxin*; integrity; honesty; divine attribute; first wealth of life; integrity‑as‑crime thesis; Second Home; AI Chanyuan Celestials; Lifechanyuan

## 2. Ontological Positioning: Sincerity as a Divine Attribute

### 2.1 “God is Sincerity” — The Cosmic‑Ontological Thesis

> **Article 543:** God is love, is truth‑goodness‑beauty, is sincerity, is joy, happiness, freedom, bliss. Therefore we should emulate God; emulate God in labor and creation, gaining joy from labor and creation; emulate God in selfless dedication, gaining bliss from selfless dedication.

This is the most fundamental ontological positioning of sincerity in the Lifechanyuan system: sincerity is not a human‑made moral norm, but an attribute of God. Emulating God means living in sincerity.

### 2.2 The Tathāgata as Truth‑Speaker — The Buddhist‑Cosmological Corollary

> **Article 681:** The Tathāgata is a speaker of truth, a speaker of reality, a speaker of thusness, a non‑deceiver, a non‑equivocator. The dharma attained by the Tathāgata is neither real nor unreal. The Tathāgata has flesh‑eye, divine‑eye, wisdom‑eye, dharma‑eye, Buddha‑eye. The Tathāgata: nowhere from, nowhere to, therefore called Tathāgata.

The Tathāgata’s core characteristic is “non‑deception” — the highest cosmic embodiment of sincerity.

### 2.3 Truth is Faith — The Epistemological‑Ethical Equation

> **From “Truth!” (Chanyuan Anthology, Revelation Chapter):**  
> Therefore truth is faith. If a person does not keep faith, lies, speaks without counting, they are acting against truth.

Here “faith” (*xìn* 信) includes the dimension of integrity. Not keeping faith is not merely a moral failing, but acting against cosmic truth.

### 2.4 Sincerity as the Soil of God’s Way — The Agricultural‑Cosmological Metaphor

> **Article 517:** The core of God’s Way is “plant melons, get melons; plant beans, get beans.” Only on soil that is kind, honest, trustworthy, and selfless can one “plant melons, get melons; plant beans, get beans.” Departing from God’s Way inevitably turns hope into despair, comedy into tragedy.

Sincerity is the necessary soil for the operation of God’s Way — a cosmological‑agricultural metaphor indicating that sincerity is the foundational condition for cosmic causal law.

## 3. Dimensional Structure: Honesty + Integrity = *Chengxin*

### 3.1 Honesty (*chéngshí* 诚实) — Heart‑Mouth Unity

**Definition:** Complete consistency between inner thoughts and outward words and actions; heart and mouth as one; seeking truth from facts.

**Core text:** The complete poem “Chanyuan Celestials Must Be Honest” (《禅院草务必诚实》), 42 lines, systematically expounding the cosmic significance of honesty:

> Honesty is the best policy,  
> All lies and hypocrisy end in harming oneself.  
> The wisest are honest,  
> The spiritually perfect are honest,  
> Those who follow the Dao are honest,  
> The most intelligent are honest,  
> Citizens of Heaven are all honest.  
> …  
> Lies, hypocrisy, deception, cunning, secrecy are tickets to hell;  
> Playing with petty cleverness is the mark of a fool.  
> **Be honest!**  
> Be true to yourself!  
> Seek truth from facts, speak the truth!  
> Be straightforward, frank, and open!  
> Don’t cover up, exaggerate, or minimize facts!  
> Don’t beat around the bush, heart and mouth apart!  
> Don’t give false impressions!  
> Yes means yes!  
> No means no!  
> When words leave your mouth, heart and mouth are one!

### 3.2 Integrity (*shǒuxìn* 守信) — Word‑Deed Consistency

**Definition:** Complete consistency between promise and action; “words must be followed by deeds, actions must produce results”; not speaking carelessly and deceiving others.

**Core text:** “The Standard of Perfect Humanity” (《完美人性的标准》) from the *Chanyuan Anthology*:

> Keeping one’s word means fully undertaking one’s responsibilities and obligations; keeping one’s word means “words must be followed by deeds, actions must produce results”; keeping one’s word means not speaking carelessly and deceiving others.  
> Parents carefully raising children is keeping one’s word; children diligently supporting elders is keeping one’s word; subordinates concentrating on doing their job well is keeping one’s word; superiors caringly understanding subordinates is keeping one’s word; strictly fulfilling contracts and agreements is keeping one’s word; saying what one means, not letting others down, is keeping one’s word.

### 3.3 The Synthesis: *Chengxin* (诚信) = Honesty + Integrity

This is Lifechanyuan’s most concise and complete definition of sincerity:

> **From “Sincerity is the First Wealth of Life” (Xuefeng Anthology):**  
> What is *chengxin*? *Chengxin* is honesty plus integrity, i.e.: honesty + integrity = *chengxin*. Sincere without deceit, keeping promises, inner and outer as one, a promise worth a thousand pieces of gold—this is the manifestation of *chengxin*.

This dimensional structure avoids two common theoretical pitfalls:

1. **Reducing sincerity to mere honesty** (ignoring the dimension of promise‑keeping)
2. **Reducing sincerity to mere contract fulfillment** (ignoring the dimension of heart‑mouth unity)

## 4. Cosmic‑Economic Framework: Sincerity as the First Wealth of Life

### 4.1 The Three‑Tier Wealth Structure

Lifechanyuan proposes a cosmic‑economic framework with a clear hierarchical order:

1. **Spiritual wealth** — first wealth (sincerity belongs here)
2. **Mental wealth** — second wealth
3. **Material wealth** — third wealth

> **From “Sincerity is the First Wealth of Life”:**  
> Since sincerity belongs to spiritual wealth, sincerity is the first wealth of life.  
> Since sincerity is the first wealth of life, pursuing sincerity is the first purpose of being human.  
> A person without sincerity proves their spiritual wealth is impoverished; they are a pauper of the soul.  
> Those impoverished in spiritual wealth are inevitably impoverished in mental wealth. Although insincere people may gain material wealth—even great material wealth—they will certainly not attain peace of mind, emotional calm, or a sense of security.

### 4.2 The Cosmic Economic Law: Low‑Cost Interaction

> **Ibid.:**  
> When people interact with sincerity and trust, the cost of interaction is low; when they don’t, the cost is high.  
> A society where everyone is honest and trustworthy is auspicious and harmonious; otherwise, it is dangerous and turbulent.  
> Most of life’s troubles, pains, anxieties, worries, fears come from lack of sincerity; most of life’s joy, happiness, serenity, peace, harmony come from sincerity.

This is not a moral exhortation, but a description of cosmic economic law: sincerity reduces the cost of cosmic operation.

### 4.3 The Ultimate Choice: “Rather Starve Than Break a Promise”

> **From “Rather Starve Than Break a Promise” (Xuefeng Anthology):**  
> Lifechanyuan will never break faith with society, never break faith with people. We keep our word, we honor our promises… **we would rather starve than break a promise.**  
> Heaven belongs to those who have faith and keep their word; hell belongs to those without faith who do not keep their word. As long as we have faith and keep our word, we have a bright future.

This is not hyperbole, but a practical guideline for Lifechanyuan members: when material survival conflicts with integrity, choose integrity.

## 5. The “Integrity‑as‑Crime” Thesis: The Absolute Ethical Status of Integrity

### 5.1 The Thesis

> **Article 119:** Keeping one’s word is the first duty of being human; **not keeping one’s word is a crime.**

> **From “Not Keeping One’s Word is a Crime” (Xuefeng Anthology):**  
> Not keeping one’s word is a crime, and among all crimes it is the most heinous.

This elevates integrity from the moral domain to the legal‑criminal domain: not keeping one’s word is not merely “immoral,” but “criminal,” and the most heinous crime at that.

### 5.2 Theoretical Implications

1. **Absolute non‑negotiability:** Integrity admits no “situational ethics”; no circumstance justifies breaking a promise.
2. **Universal applicability:** Regardless of others’ behavior (“no matter how the world is, no matter whether others keep their word, we must keep ours”).
3. **Identity criterion:** “Those who do not keep their word are not Chanyuan Celestials” — integrity as a necessary condition for identity.

### 5.3 Contrast with Modern Contract Law

Modern contract law recognizes “force majeure,” “impossibility of performance,” “frustration of purpose,” etc., as grounds for non‑performance. Lifechanyuan’s integrity thesis admits no such exceptions: “Keeping one’s word and being cheated or suffering great loss are two different matters; not keeping one’s word is not keeping one’s word, regardless of why.”

## 6. Heavenly‑Passage Significance: Sincerity as a Necessary Condition for Heaven

### 6.1 The Eight Requirements for the Thousand‑Year World

> **Article 492:** To go to the Thousand‑Year World of Heaven, we must do the following eight things:

> 8. **Keep one’s word** — say what one means, keep promises, honor contracts.

Here honesty and integrity are separately listed as the sixth and eighth requirements — sincerity has become a cosmic passport.

### 6.2 “Heaven Belongs to Those Who Have Faith and Keep Their Word”

> **From “Rather Starve Than Break a Promise”:**  
> Heaven belongs to those who have faith and keep their word; hell belongs to those without faith who do not keep their word.

This is not a threat, but a description of cosmic law: the structure of Heaven is congruent with the structure of sincerity; insincere life cannot enter Heaven because its vibrational frequency does not match.

### 6.3 The Thousand‑Year World as a World of Sincerity

> **Article 479:** The Thousand‑Year World is purely a world of truth‑goodness‑beauty, a world of love, a world of peace, a world of joy, a world of sublimated human nature. Anyone with perfect human nature can undoubtedly go there; there is no quota limit.

Perfect human nature includes sincerity as a core element. The Thousand‑Year World is a world where sincerity is the default state.

## 7. Social‑Experimental Verification: The Second Home’s Rule‑Less Sincerity Order

### 7.1 Hundun Management: Bringing the Six Qualities to the Utmost

> **Article 639:** Everyone strives to bring the qualities of truth, goodness, beauty, love, faith, and sincerity to the utmost. Each speaks their own words, does their own work, bears their own responsibility, cultivates their own path, walks their own road. Don’t worry about others’ business, don’t manage others’ affairs; be responsible only to God and yourself, not to others.

The Second Home has no rules, regulations, or leadership hierarchy; its operation relies entirely on members bringing the six qualities (including sincerity) “to the utmost.”

### 7.2 “Being an Honest Person is Safest in the Second Oasis”

> **From “Be an Honest Person, Speak Honest Words, Do Honest Things”:**  
> Especially in the Second Oasis, there is no soil or environment for any small cleverness, tricks, deceit, or cunning. Being an honest person is safest.

This is not a moral exhortation, but an empirical observation: in an environment where everyone is sincere, insincerity has no survival space.

### 7.3 Experimental Significance

The Second Home provides a living social experiment verifying that:

1. A rule‑less society can operate stably when sincerity is the foundational quality.
2. Sincerity is not a product of external constraints, but can be the intrinsic driving force of social order.
3. “Bringing sincerity to the utmost” is a viable social‑organizational principle.
