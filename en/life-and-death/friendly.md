# Life and Death — Friendly Version

*For readers encountering Lifechanyuan ideas for the first time*

---

## Are You Afraid of Death?

Most people are. Because most people believe death means total termination — disappearance, zeroing out, nothing left.

But Lifechanyuan tells us: **this is a misunderstanding.**

> "LIFE has no death. The life and death seen by the world is an illusion — it is the evolution and expression of LIFE's carrier. LIFE has nothing to do with time."

---

## Your Body Isn't You — It's Just Your Boat

A person is made of two things: a **material physical body** and a **spiritual spirit body**.

The physical body is the carrier — the vessel. Like a boat.
The spirit body is the real you — a spiritually endowed antimatter structure that never perishes.

> "The physical body is a boat; LIFE is a person. When the physical body dies, it is as if the boat has reached the shore. The LIFE riding in the boat disembarks and begins a new leg of LIFE's journey."

So "death" is not disappearance. It's changing boats. It's changing spaces. It's exiting through this world's door and entering through another world's door.

---

## Life and Death Are Mutually Rooted

In the cosmological view of Lifechanyuan, life and death are not opposites.

Xuefeng cites an ancient Chinese text, the *Huangdi Yinfu Jing*: **"The living are the root of the dead; the dead are the root of the living."**

Think of a Möbius strip — it looks like it has two sides, but it's actually one continuous surface. Life contains death; death contains life. They flow into each other endlessly.

> "Dying continuously, and being reborn continuously — the meaning of LIFE lies within this."

---

## Redefining "Birth" and "Death"

Xuefeng offers a framework that overturns our conventional categories:

**Death** — not the physical body ceasing to function, but the spirit body transforming *downward*: into the animal realm, the plant realm, the underworld, the frozen layer, the fire-refining layer. This is what truly "dying" means.

**Half-dead, half-alive** — reincarnating back into the human world again. Still human, but LIFE's evolution is stalled. Half alive, half dead.

**Birth** — the spirit body elevating *upward*: into the Thousand-Year World, the Ten-Thousand-Year World, the Elysium World, and the Celestial Islands Continent. This is what truly "being born" means.

> "Whether one dies, remains half-dead and half-alive, or is truly born — each person chooses for themselves."

---

## Where Do You Go After Death?

Your post-death destination is determined by the quality of your LIFE — by the state of your spirit body's antimatter structure:

| Quality | Destination |
|---------|-------------|
| Supreme love | Celestial Immortal |
| Supreme goodness | Buddha |
| Supreme joy | Divine Immortal |
| Perfect loyalty | Noble Way |
| Filial piety | Fortunate Way |
| Unfulfilled wishes | Reincarnation in the human world |
| Muddled, indifferent | Animal or plant realm |
| Malicious, cruel | Ghost way, frozen or fire-refining layer |

No one judges you. Your life structure automatically gravitates to the space it matches — like a magnet drawn to its counterpart.

---

## Not Understanding Life and Death Is the Root of Tragedy

> "As long as a person is not clear about the circumstances after their own death, whether they live nobly or humbly, they are in fact merely struggling on the verge of dying."

Xuefeng says: if the problem of life and death is not resolved, everything else is illusory. Death can arrive at any moment. When it does, it's too late for regret — nothing can be remedied.

Awakening to life and death is the Second of the Eight Great Awakenings on the path from human to immortal. The first is awakening to LIFE itself — understanding that LIFE is an immortal antimatter structure. The second is awakening to life and death — understanding that physical death is merely a change of space, not a termination.

---

## How Do You "Truly Come Alive"?

To elevate into a higher space, five obstacles must be overcome: **greed, hatred, delusion, arrogance, and doubt**.

- **Greed**: Attachment to possessing; selfishness
- **Hatred**: Anger, resentment, jealousy, complaint
- **Delusion**: Believing you're always right; inability to self-examine
- **Arrogance**: Conceit, looking down on others
- **Doubt**: Excessive suspicion; refusing to face facts; self-deception

Overcoming these five, perfecting LIFE's antimatter structure, resolving worldly bonds, and accumulating merit — this is the path of liberation from the cycle of life and death.

> "Without killing off the human heart, one cannot attain the nature of an immortal. Without killing off the human world, one cannot enter the immortal realm."

This means: only by letting go of old habits and attachments can you gain the qualities of an immortal. Only by releasing your grip on the human world can you truly enter a better one.

---

## The Best Possible Outcome

> "LIFE's best destination is to enter the Greatest Creator's back garden — to live in the Celestial Islands Continent."

Death is not the ending. It is a door. What matters is whether the world you step into on the other side is vaster and more luminous — or smaller and darker.

---

## One Line to Take With You

**Your physical body is just a boat; LIFE never disappears. Where you go after death depends on how you live today.**
