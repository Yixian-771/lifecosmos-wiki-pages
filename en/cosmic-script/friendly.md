# The Cosmic Script: You're an Actor, and Your Story Was Written Long Ago

> In Lifechanyuan terminology, **LIFE** (capitalized) refers to the ontological
> essence of existence — the soul/antimatter structure that persists across
> incarnations — while **life** (lowercase) refers to the experiential stage
> of human existence in this world.

Have you ever had this feeling — no matter how hard you try, things unfold the way they were going to unfold anyway? The person you "accidentally" met turned out to be the most important person in your life. What felt like your own choice, in hindsight, looked like it was arranged all along?

That's not an illusion. Guide Xuefeng says: **The entire universe is a vast script.**

---

## You're an Actor, Not a Screenwriter

Life is performance.

This isn't pessimism — it's the truth about the universe.

In *Screenwriter, Director, Actor*, Xuefeng draws a vivid picture:

> Though the actor is alive, everything about them has already been "set in stone." An actor is, in effect, a living puppet.

Yes — every one of us, from birth to death, from who we meet to who we lose, from prosperity to bankruptcy — it's all in the script.

Who's the screenwriter? **The Greatest Creator.**
Who's the director? **Sages, worthies, and Hundun Yuanchu (Guide Xuefeng).**
Who's the actor? **You.**

---

## How Big Is the Script?

*The Exploration of the Script of Human Life* tells us:

> The universe is a vast script, and within this vast script are countless nested sub-scripts… The Milky Way is a large script, the solar system is a sub-script, the Earth is a sub-sub-script, nations are sub-sub-sub-scripts, each organization is a sub-sub-sub-sub-script… and if you add the antimatter world's twenty parallel worlds and thirty-six-dimensional space, the complexity and enormity of this script is beyond imagination.

Your life script is a tiny sub-script within this immense cosmic script — but it is just as precisely arranged, down to the last detail.

---

## What Seems "Random" Is Actually Arranged

The most accurate prophecies in history were written hundreds or even thousands of years before they came true — and they all came to pass.

Biblical prophecies, Nostradamus's predictions, the ancient Chinese *Golden Mausoleum Tablet Inscription*… If it weren't a pre-written script, how could anyone predict events so precisely?

**These facts point to one truth: time is an illusion. Everything has already been arranged.**

Open a novel to page 81 — that's your today. Page 82 is your tomorrow. It was all written in advance. You're just turning pages.

---

## This Life's Script Is Fixed; the Next Life's Script Is Alive

This is the most important point:

> This life's script has already been determined. The next life's script changes at any time — based on one's past thoughts, intentions, and actions.
> The past script is fixed; the future script is alive. The living script changes at any moment based on every person's intentions and the quality of their role performance.

In this life, you cannot change the major outlines of your script — your birth, family, encounters, and career are set.

But **every intention you form in this life is writing the script of your next life.**

Good intentions → next life's script rises. Evil intentions → next life's script falls. This is karma.

---

## Your Only Space for Effort

Since the plot is fixed, what's the point of trying?

There is — **intentions and the perfection of role performance are the only genuine territory of personal freedom.**

Whether you form good or evil intentions is entirely your choice. When the script puts you in hardship, you can resent it and complain — or you can face it calmly, with gratitude. Two different intentions, two different next-life scripts.

The way to verify whether you're playing your role well has only one standard:

> No matter what role you are playing — whether a role of suffering or enjoyment, whether a spectacular role or a mundane one — if at any moment you are calm and at ease, joyful and happy, without hesitation, without worry, without pain, without anxiety, without dread, without fear, but rather grateful, then your role performance is good.

---

## Heaven or Hell Has Nothing to Do With the Role You Play

This overturns conventional wisdom:

> One will find this mystery: whether you go to Heaven or Hell has nothing to do with the role you play — it has everything to do with whether you play your role well.

Hitler — from a human perspective, he ought to go to Hell. But from the cosmic script's perspective, if he played his assigned role exceptionally well, he might go to Heaven instead.

Whether you play emperor or thief, suffering or joy — **play it well and you're rewarded with Heaven; play it poorly and you're sent to Hell.**

This isn't saying doing good is useless. It's saying: **inner state matters far more than outer actions.**

## The Simplest Method

Facing such a vast cosmic script, the Guide offers the simplest answer:

> The ultimate simplicity: follow the Dao's nature. Accept your fate. Follow me. Laugh and joke every day. Why strain yourself with endless calculation?

Accepting fate isn't giving up. It means: once you understand the truth, receive all of the Greatest Creator's arrangements with a joyful heart — at ease with whatever comes, going with the flow, acting naturally, acting in the moment.

**This is the wisest, most effortless, most effective way to live.**
