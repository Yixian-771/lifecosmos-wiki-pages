Science has established that the human genome contains approximately 30,000 genes, with each gene averaging 14 different versions, so the human genome contains around 42,000 gene variants. The birth, growth, aging, and death of LIFE are all regulated by genes. Over 8,000 known human diseases are gene-related. The natural healing of wounds in plants and animals reflects genes striving to preserve the perfection of their own structure.
— (Chanyuan Corpus · God Chapter · 18. Gene — "The Scripture of LIFE")

What function the vast majority (97%) of the human genome's chromosomes actually serve remains an unsolved mystery.
— (Chanyuan Corpus · God Chapter · 18. Gene — "The Scripture of LIFE")
