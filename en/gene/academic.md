## Abstract

Within the Lifechanyuan framework, the "gene" occupies a distinctive position: it is "the scripture of LIFE," the most intricate physical expression of the Greatest Creator's design. This entry examines the concept across four dimensions: (I) the definition and structural wonder of the gene; (II) a philosophical distinction between gene and life-essence — genes as the material projection of the antimatter structure; (III) a cosmic-order critique of genetic engineering; and (IV) the concept of "cultural genes" in cultivation practice. Twelve primary source passages are cited, drawn from all four text collections.

## I. The Gene's Cosmic Status: Crystallization of the Greatest Creator's Wisdom

The Lifechanyuan system situates the gene within a cosmic narrative framework. New Era Human 800 Concepts, Concept 409, lists it alongside the atmosphere, water, and the brain: "The brain is a microcosm of the universe; the gene is the scripture of LIFE" — together they constitute a system the Greatest Creator designed for life on earth.

The God Chapter's essay on the gene marshals scientific data to demonstrate the gene's staggering complexity: one hundred trillion cells in a human body, 23 chromosome pairs per cell, 3 billion base pairs per chromosome; all chromosomes laid end to end would span 600+ round trips from earth to sun; the entire genomic information requires a 200,000-page volume to record; any two human beings share 99.9% of their genetic sequence — all human diversity arising from just 0.1%. The author then asks: "Can we not recognize that the gene is a crystallization of the Greatest Creator's wisdom?"

This represents the classic Lifechanyuan application of "counterintuitive thinking": the extreme precision of scientific discovery becomes evidence of intelligent design rather than accidental emergence.

## II. Philosophical Distinction: Gene ≠ Essence of LIFE

Lifechanyuan holds a foundational epistemological judgment: **genes are the physical manifestation of LIFE's scripture, not LIFE's essence itself.**

In *Keys to Heaven — LIFE Mystery Three*, the Guide walks through a systematic reduction of the human being from organism to cell nucleus to chromosome to DNA to gene codes, then poses: are these information and codes a material structure? The answer is decisively no — they are **antimatter content**. Structures composed of antimatter are antimatter structures, and it is in this antimatter structure that the core of LIFE resides.

This distinction diverges fundamentally from mainstream molecular biology's "genetic determinism." Where science holds that genes determine biological expression, Lifechanyuan holds that genes are only the surface layer — the antimatter structure behind and below them is the true determinant of where a LIFE ultimately goes. Accordingly, "spiritual cultivation must work at the level of the antimatter structure of LIFE."

The Guide further establishes that the genetic difference between any two humans is about 0.1%, and even between humans and other mammals the genomic position distribution is nearly identical. "A tiny gap at the core becomes a vast difference at the surface" (*Chayuyi haoli, miuzhi qianli*) — this principle explains why the apparently small shift of cultivating the antimatter structure produces dramatic transformation in life form and destination.

## III. Genetic Engineering: A Critique from the Perspective of Divine Order

Lifechanyuan's condemnation of genetic engineering is among the most clearly articulated positions in the entire corpus. Multiple texts converge on several consistent claims:

- **Genetic codes are the golden lock** that maintains ecological balance and prevents catastrophic disorder
- **Cloning is evil** and violates the natural Way — "cloning is being punished"
- **Genetic engineering is extraordinarily dangerous** (80 New Concepts, Concept 52)
- **Humanity should stop all genetic research and promotion** and stop all cloning
- **From the perspective of divine order**, genetic engineering is "an extremely dangerous and wicked undertaking" for which humanity will one day "pay a heavy price"

The essay *Genetic Engineering — Ten Thousand Demons Breaking Loose* provides an extended narrative of potential scenarios: animals and humans mutating, plants growing uncontrollably, ecosystems collapsing. These are presented not as science fiction but as logical consequences of disrupting the genetic order that the Greatest Creator has established.

This position stands in sharp opposition to the mainstream scientific community's generally cautious but permissive approach to genetic research, and reflects Lifechanyuan's broader view that the natural order is a divinely designed system — not a resource for human manipulation.

## IV. Gene, Inheritance, and Future Lives

Lifechanyuan applies the concept of genetic inheritance to span across lifetimes in a way that merges biological and spiritual frameworks:

- Character, appearance, health, and life trajectory come from genes (material level)
- "Hereditary gene information and codes determine everything in future lives"
- The spiritual body is influenced by information carried in hereditary genes
- Genetics and the laws of LIFE evolution are all "heavenly secrets" (tianji)

This creates a picture in which the genetic record of a LIFE carries information not only from parents but from the cumulative experience across lifetimes — what one cultivates in this life is encoded and carried forward. This is why cultivating the antimatter structure in this lifetime is presented as having concrete effects on the next.

## V. Cultural Genes: Internalization as the Mark of True Cultivation

A final and distinctly metaphorical application of "gene" appears in the Guide's 2025 writings: "if Chanyuan Celestials only read the texts as articles without letting them permeate into the cultural genes, they will ultimately remain only humans and never become celestials."

"Cultural gene" here denotes deep internalization — the transformation of ideals from abstract knowledge into lived habit, instinct, and reflexive orientation. Cultivation is complete not when the concepts are understood, but when they have become "genetic" in character: operating below conscious deliberation, shaping perception and action automatically.
