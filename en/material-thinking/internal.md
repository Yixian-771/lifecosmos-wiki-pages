## I. Overview and Definition

Material Thinking is the first rung of the Eight Thinking Ladders, and also the lowest. Material Thinking is a mode of thinking that makes life-value judgments and takes action based on the surface phenomena of nature and human society, completely ignoring the causal relationships between things and phenomena, and never investigating the deep causes behind surface appearances. Material Thinking is the simplest and most practical of human thinking modes — it is the thinking of animals and hundun people, and the thinking possessed by the vast majority of ordinary worldly people. On the Eight Thinking Ladders, Material Thinking is the first rung, and also the starting point that cultivation must transcend.

**Core Definition I** (*Chanyuan Corpus · Revelation Section · "Material Thinking"*, complete original text):

> Material Thinking is a way of thinking that makes life-value judgments and takes action based on the surface phenomena of nature and human society, completely ignoring the causal relationships between things and phenomena, never investigating the deep causes behind surface appearances. It can therefore also be called Surface Thinking, Intuitive Thinking, Linear Thinking, One-Sided Thinking, Instinct Thinking, and 1+1=2 Thinking. This thinking is essentially the thinking of animals and hundun people, and the thinking possessed by the vast majority of ordinary worldly people.

## II. The Six Aliases of Material Thinking: Six Angles for Understanding the Same Thinking

Material Thinking has six aliases because, observed from different angles, this thinking presents different facets — but the essence is only one: superficial, direct, instinctual, linear.

**Alias 1: Surface Thinking — seeing only the surface, not the essence**

Only paying attention to the surface phenomena of things, never investigating deep causes. "Mountains are mountains, water is water" — never asking "why."

**Alias 2: Intuitive Thinking — direct perception, no reasoning needed**

Whatever the eyes see is what it is; sensory stimulation drives action directly, without any process of abstraction, generalization, or reasoning.

**Alias 3: Linear Thinking — unfolding in one dimension, knowing only sequence**

Seeing only the linear sequence of things, not the network of causal relationships between them. Only knowing "what comes first and what comes after," unable to see "why."

**Alias 4: One-Sided Thinking — seeing only one side, not the other**

Unable to simultaneously see both sides of things — positive and negative energy, the cycle of cause and effect. Draws conclusions from seeing only one side. Right and wrong are cut in one stroke. "The unity of opposites" is incomprehensible to them.

**Alias 5: Instinct Thinking — driven by instinct, no autonomous consciousness**

The driving force of action is mainly physiological instinct — hungry, eat; thirsty, drink; desire stirs, follow it; cold comes, avoid it. The only difference from animals is the form of the carrier; in essence, instinct governs behavior.

**Alias 6: 1+1=2 Thinking — knowing only quantity, not energy**

Quantitative thinking, replacing understanding of essence with numerical calculation. The most concise summary of Material Thinking — not understanding that "1+1=1 is an increase in energy" (Article 311), not understanding the mystery of consciousness transcending matter.
