## II. Historical Utopian Exploration and Its Failures

**Utopia and the Second Home**

The pursuit of an ideal realm, ideal society, ideal life, and ideal living is a shared human quality, and an enduring source of spiritual motivation. All the sages and philosophers of history shared the same wish: to guide humanity into an ideal society — whether Plato's *Republic*, Thomas More's *Utopia*, Bellamy's *Looking Backward*, Confucius's vision of the Grand Harmony, or Marxist-Leninist communist theory — all sought to bring humanity into an ideal society.

Humanity has explored the ideal society not only theoretically but also in practice — from the Taiping Heavenly Kingdom and the most famous American utopian communities like Brook Farm, the Fruit Lands, the Shakers, and Pullman's capitalist utopia, to Soviet collective farms, Chinese people's communes, Osho's commune, and various utopian communities in Japan, the United States, and Canada — all trying to pioneer a path of the ideal.

(Xuefeng Corpus · Lifechanyuan Community · Utopia and the Second Home)

---

**The Eight Defects of Historical Utopian Theory and Practice**

A synthesis of the theories, doctrines, and practices of utopian societies throughout history reveals eight main defects:

1. No correct worldview; no correct understanding of the Greatest Creator; vague understanding of time and space; no credible belief system.
2. No correct view of life; no comprehensive understanding of life's origin, development, and destination.
3. No correct outlook on life; no clear understanding or definition of the value and meaning of life.
4. Failure to escape the mental constraints of nation and family — still churning within those frameworks.
5. Neglect of the human tendencies toward laziness, corruption, and despotism.
6. Lack of systematic and clear theoretical explanation; heavy reliance on intimidation and threats, such as "going to hell."
7. Suppressed or unguided sexuality and romantic love — either repressed or indulged, lacking spiritual and soul guidance.
8. Life too monotonous, discipline too rigid, no allowance for acting on one's nature — lack of freedom.

(Xuefeng Corpus · Lifechanyuan Community · Utopia and the Second Home)

**Democracy as the Core Factor of Utopian Failure**

Utopian ideals have flourished for a long time — but why do they all flash and vanish? Take Robert Owen, the utopian socialist who sold his estate to build "New Harmony" in America — a magnificent vision of human life — yet within four years it was empty and gone. I have studied this carefully: all failed utopias share one common characteristic — democracy. Democracy is one of the core factors leading to utopian failure.

(Xuefeng Corpus · Lifechanyuan Community · Xuefeng's Autocracy Is a Democratic Model)
